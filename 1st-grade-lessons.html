Learning the sounds: a, e, i, o, u

Hey there, little climber! I'm Little Billy, and I'm going to help you learn the special sounds that letters make. These are called vowels, and they're like the heartbeat of words!

üßó Pre-Climb: What Are Vowels?

The Magic 5: A, E, I, O, U

Let's practice their short sounds:

  • A says "ah" like in cat
  • E says "eh" like in bed
  • I says "ih" like in sit
  • O says "oh" like in dog
  • U says "uh" like in bug

üéØ The Climb: Word Sorting Adventure

Parent/Teacher: Read these words with your child and sort them by vowel sound:

A words: cat, hat, map, bag, dad
E words: bed, pen, hen, net, wet
I words: sit, pig, big, win, hit
O words: dog, hop, box, dot, hot
U words: bug, hug, cup, sun, run

üèÜ Summit Check: Can you hear the vowel sound?

Game: Parent says a word, child identifies the vowel sound!

Try these: pan, ten, tin, top, tub

Answers: a, e, i, o, u

Wow! You're getting the hang of vowel sounds! Every word needs at least one vowel - they're super important for reading. Great climbing!

üèîÔ∏è Climb #2: Sentence Summit

Building Our First Sentences

Now that we know letter sounds, let's build sentences! A sentence is like a complete thought - it tells us something whole and interesting!
Little Billy's Mountain Story:

The goat ran up the hill.
Billy saw a big rock.
He can jump high.
The sun is hot.
Billy likes to climb.

üßó Pre-Climb: What Makes a Sentence?

Every sentence needs:

  • WHO (The goat, Billy, He)
  • WHAT (ran, saw, jump, is, likes)
  • Capital letter at the beginning
  • Period at the end

üéØ The Climb: Sentence Building

Activity 1: Complete the Sentence

Help Billy finish these sentences:

  1. The cat _______ on the mat. (sits, runs, jumps)
  2. Billy _______ up the hill. (walks, flies, swims)
  3. The _______ is red. (car, water, air)
  4. I can _______ my name. (eat, write, sleep)

Answers: sits, walks, car, write

Activity 2: Fix Billy's Sentences

Billy forgot something! What's missing?

  1. the dog runs fast (Missing capital T)
  2. Billy climbs the rock (Missing period .)
  3. can jump high (Missing WHO - He can jump high.)
  4. The bird (Missing WHAT - The bird flies.)
You're becoming a sentence builder! Remember: WHO does WHAT, start with a capital, end with a period. That's the sentence formula!

üèîÔ∏è Climb #3: Story Peak

Understanding What We Read

Reading isn't just saying the words - it's understanding the story! Let's read about my mountain adventure and see what we can learn!
Billy's Big Day

Little Billy woke up early. The sun was just coming up over the mountains. Billy was excited because today he would climb to a new peak.

Billy packed his backpack with water and snacks. He put on his climbing boots. His big brother gave him a lucky rock to carry.

Billy started climbing. The path was steep, but Billy was strong. He stopped to rest under a big tree. A friendly bird sang to him.

At the top of the mountain, Billy could see the whole valley below. He felt proud and happy. "I did it!" Billy said.

üßó Pre-Climb: Reading Strategies

Good readers:

  • Read slowly and think about the words
  • Picture the story in their minds
  • Ask questions about what happens
  • Remember the important parts

üéØ The Climb: Understanding Billy's Story

WHO Questions:

  1. Who is the main character? (Billy)
  2. Who gave Billy a lucky rock? (His big brother)
  3. Who sang to Billy? (A friendly bird)

WHAT Questions:

  1. What did Billy want to do? (Climb to a new peak)
  2. What did Billy pack in his backpack? (Water and snacks)
  3. What did Billy say at the top? ("I did it!")

WHERE and WHEN Questions:

  1. Where did Billy rest? (Under a big tree)
  2. When did Billy start his climb? (Early in the morning)
  3. Where could Billy see the whole valley? (At the top)

WHY and HOW Questions:

  1. Why was Billy excited? (He was going to climb a new peak)
  2. How did Billy feel at the top? (Proud and happy)
Great job understanding my story! When you read, always ask yourself: Who is in the story? What happens? Where and when does it happen? That helps you climb to the top of understanding!
