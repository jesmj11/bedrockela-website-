📚 Lesson 19: The Hero’s Journey Pattern

Comparing All Four Hero Stories

📚 Welcome!

No new reading today! Instead, you’re going to look at all four hero stories side by side and discover the pattern that connects them. Then — the exciting part — you’ll start planning your own hero story.


Today you will:

  • Map the Hero’s Journey for all four heroes
  • Compare and contrast the stories
  • Begin planning YOUR hero’s journey narrative

Time: About 50 minutes


🧐 Part 1: Before You Read

The Hero’s Journey has these steps:

1. HOME — The hero starts in their ordinary world.
2. THE CALL — Something happens that sends them on a quest.
3. CHALLENGES — The hero faces obstacles and dangers.
4. HELP — The hero receives help from friends or gifts.
5. THE BIG TEST — The hero faces the hardest challenge.
6. VICTORY — The hero succeeds.
7. RETURN — The hero returns home, changed.

📖 Part 2: Today’s Task

No new reading today! Instead, look back at your notes and journal entries from the past 18 days. You’re going to fill in a Hero’s Journey chart for all four heroes.


💡 Tip: It’s okay to flip back through your book and your journal. Good readers and writers go back to check their thinking!


💬 Part 3: Word of the Day

narrative (noun)

A story. When you write a narrative, you’re telling a story with characters, a setting, a problem, and a solution.


From the story: Every hero story is a narrative — a tale with a beginning, middle, and end. Now you’re going to write your own narrative.


Think about it: You’ve been reading narratives for 18 days. Now it’s your turn to write one. What story do YOU want to tell?

✍️ Part 5: Plan Your Hero’s Journey Story!

It’s time to write YOUR OWN hero’s journey! Over the next few days, you’ll plan, draft, and finish a short story (1-2 pages) that follows the Hero’s Journey pattern.


Today is PLANNING DAY. Fill in your story mountain:
