📚 Lesson 24: Author’s Chair: Share Your Story

Sharing & Celebrating Our Writing

📚 Welcome!

Today is one of the best days of the year: Author’s Chair Day! You wrote a hero’s journey story from scratch — planned it, drafted it, revised it, edited it, and published it. Now it’s time to share it.


Today you will:

  • Read your hero’s journey story (to a partner, group, or by recording yourself)
  • Listen to other stories and give kind feedback
  • Reflect on your growth as a writer

Time: About 45-50 minutes


🧐 Part 1: Before You Read

When you share your writing:

  • Read slowly and clearly.
  • Use expression — change your voice for dialogue!
  • Be proud. You MADE this.

When you listen to someone else’s story:

  • Listen respectfully.
  • Think of one specific thing you liked (“I loved the part where...”).
  • Think of one kind suggestion (“I wonder what would happen if...”).

📖 Part 2: Today’s Task

Read your final draft aloud. If you’re working independently, record yourself reading it, or read it to a family member, friend, or pet. (Pets are great listeners.)


Then, if your teacher has shared other students’ stories, read at least one of them and leave a comment using the feedback format below.


💡 Sharing your writing takes courage. Remember the Cowardly Lion? Courage isn’t the absence of fear — it’s doing the thing even when you’re nervous.


💬 Part 3: Word of the Day

feedback (noun)

Helpful comments about someone’s work. Good feedback is specific, kind, and useful.


From the story: When you give feedback on a story, focus on what worked AND what could be even better.


Think about it: What’s the difference between helpful feedback and just saying “it’s good”? Why is specific feedback more useful?


✅ Check Your Understanding

Answer these questions using what you’ve learned.


1. After reading or sharing your story, write a “STAR” (one thing you’re proud of) and a “WISH” (one thing you’d improve if you could write it again).

  • What did you learn about yourself as a writer during this project?
  • If you could write another hero’s journey, what would you do differently?
