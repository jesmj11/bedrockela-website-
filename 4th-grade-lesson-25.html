💬 Part 3: Word of the Day

evidence (noun)

Specific details, facts, or examples from a text that support your point. When you say “In Chapter 15, the Wizard admits he’s a humbug,” that’s evidence.


From the story: Strong writing uses evidence from the text to prove its point. Don’t just say what you think — show WHY you think it.


Think about it: Why is evidence important? What’s the difference between an opinion with evidence and an opinion without it?

✍️ Part 5: Your First Text-Evidence Essay

Write a response to this prompt. This is your first essay using evidence from more than one text!


PROMPT: How do heroes change during their journeys? Use evidence from The Wonderful Wizard of Oz AND one Greek myth (Perseus, Theseus, or Atalanta) to support your answer.


Your essay should include:

  • An introduction that answers the question (1-2 sentences)
  • A paragraph about The Wonderful Wizard of Oz with at least one specific example
  • A paragraph about your chosen Greek myth with at least one specific example
  • A conclusion that connects both stories (1-2 sentences)

Sentence starters if you need them:

  • Introduction: “Heroes change during their journeys because...”
  • Evidence: “In The Wonderful Wizard of Oz, Dorothy shows this when...”
  • Evidence: “Similarly, in the myth of [hero], we see...”
  • Conclusion: “Both stories show that...”
