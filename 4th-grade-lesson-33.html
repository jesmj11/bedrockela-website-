📚 Lesson 33: The Treasure Map

Treasure Island • Chapters 5-6

📚 Welcome!

Jim has the map. What does he do with it? Today, the adults get involved, a voyage is planned, and we meet new characters — including one you should NOT trust.


Today you will:

  • Read Chapters 5-6 of Treasure Island
  • Meet Dr. Livesey and Squire Trelawney
  • Think about what Jim can and can’t know as narrator
  • Write in your journal

Time: About 45-50 minutes


🧐 Part 1: Before You Read

First-person narrators can only tell you what THEY know. Jim can’t read other people’s minds. This means Jim — and the reader — can be fooled.


Think about this: If someone seems friendly but is secretly plotting against you, how would you know? What clues might you miss?


🔍 What to look for:

  • Who decides to go after the treasure?
  • What kind of people are Dr. Livesey and the Squire?
  • Does anyone mention hiring a crew? (This will matter later.)

📖 Part 2: Today’s Task

Read Chapters 5-6. Plans are made and the voyage takes shape.


💡 Reading tip: Start noticing what Jim notices and what he DOESN’T notice. A first-person narrator can’t see their own blind spots.


💬 Part 3: Word of the Day

countenance (noun)

A person’s face or facial expression. What their face reveals about how they’re feeling.


From the story: Jim describes the Squire’s excited countenance when he sees the map — the man can barely contain himself.


Think about it: What can you tell about a person from their countenance? Can someone’s face lie?
