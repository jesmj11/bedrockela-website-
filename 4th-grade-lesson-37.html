📚 Lesson 37: The Secret Weighs Heavy

Treasure Island • Chapter 12

📚 Welcome!

Jim knows Silver’s secret. Now he has to tell the people he trusts without Silver finding out. This chapter is about trust, strategy, and the weight of dangerous knowledge.


Today you will:

  • Read Chapter 12 of Treasure Island
  • Analyze Jim’s decision-making
  • Write your FIRST POV REWRITE

Time: About 50 minutes


🧐 Part 1: Before You Read

Jim has information that could save or doom everyone. How he handles it reveals his character.


Think about this: If you overheard a dangerous secret, who would you tell? How would you decide who to trust?


🔍 What to look for:

  • How does Jim share what he heard?
  • How do the captain, doctor, and squire react?
  • What plan do they make?

📖 Part 2: Today’s Task

Read Chapter 12: “Council of War.” Jim tells his allies what Silver said. The good guys make a plan.


💡 Reading tip: Notice how the mood shifts from Chapter 11’s panic to Chapter 12’s controlled tension. Good authors vary the pace.


💬 Part 3: Word of the Day

allegiance (noun)

Loyalty or commitment to a leader, group, or cause. Whose side you’re on.


From the story: Jim’s allegiance is to the captain and doctor. Silver’s crew has secretly given their allegiance to Silver.


Think about it: How do you decide where your allegiance lies? Is it always obvious who deserves your loyalty?

A) Turn the ship around

2. How has Jim’s role changed since Chapter 1?

A) He’s still just a cabin boy

B) He’s become someone the adults rely on for critical information

C) He’s become the captain

D) He doesn’t play any role


3. Jim started as a scared boy at an inn. Now he’s giving adults life-saving information. How has his character developed? Use evidence from at least two chapters.

💡 Hint: Compare Jim in Ch 1 (watching Billy Bones nervously) to Jim now (telling the captain about mutiny).

✍️ Your First POV Rewrite!

The apple barrel scene is told by Jim. But what was Silver thinking? Rewrite the scene from LONG JOHN SILVER’S perspective.


Requirements:

  • Write in first person (“I”) as Silver
  • 1 paragraph (5-7 sentences)
  • Show what Silver is thinking and feeling
  • Remember: Silver doesn’t know Jim is in the barrel!

Sentence starters if you need them:

“I leaned in close to the men and spoke in a low voice...”

“The captain was a fool if he thought...”
