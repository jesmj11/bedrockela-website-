📚 Lesson 38: Land Ho!

Treasure Island • Chapters 13-14

📚 Welcome!

The island is in sight. Both sides know what’s coming. The tension is about to break. And Jim makes a wild decision nobody expects.


Today you will:

  • Read Chapters 13-14 of Treasure Island
  • Analyze rising action building toward climax
  • Evaluate Jim’s decision-making
  • Write in your journal

Time: About 50 minutes


🧐 Part 1: Before You Read

Remember the story mountain? The rising action is when events get more intense, building toward the climax. We’re nearing the top.


Think about this: Have you ever acted on impulse — done something without thinking? Was it brave or reckless?


🔍 What to look for:

  • What does Jim do that surprises everyone?
  • How does the mood shift once the crew goes ashore?
  • What violence does Jim witness?

📖 Part 2: Today’s Task

Read Chapters 13-14. Jim sneaks ashore with the pirates. He witnesses something terrible, and the island adventure begins.


💡 Reading tip: Track the rising action. Each chapter should feel more tense than the last.


💬 Part 3: Word of the Day

reckless (adjective)

Acting without thinking about danger or consequences. Bold but potentially foolish.


From the story: Jim’s decision to sneak ashore could be called reckless — it puts him in danger, but gives him information no one else has.


Think about it: Is there a difference between bravery and recklessness? Can the same action be both?

3. Where on the story mountain are we?

A) The very beginning
