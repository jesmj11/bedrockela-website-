📚 Lesson 42: Jim’s Boldest Move

Treasure Island • Chapters 21-24

📚 Welcome!

Jim is out on his own. No adults, no plan, no backup. What happens next is some of the most exciting first-person narration in literature.


Today you will:

  • Read Chapters 21-24 (Jim’s sea adventure arc)
  • Experience the climax of Jim’s solo mission
  • Debate: hero or reckless kid?
  • Write in your journal

Time: About 55 minutes (longer reading — worth it!)


🧐 Part 1: Before You Read

These chapters are pure first-person adventure. Jim is alone, and everything is filtered through his eyes, fear, and determination.


Think about this: What’s the bravest thing you’ve ever done completely alone?


🔍 What to look for:

  • What does Jim do with Ben Gunn’s boat?
  • What happens when Jim boards the Hispaniola?
  • Who does Jim encounter on the ship?

📖 Part 2: Today’s Task

Read Chapters 21-24. Jim paddles out, climbs aboard the ship, and faces a life-threatening situation. The action is nonstop.


💡 Reading tip: These chapters are PEAK first-person. Notice how you feel Jim’s physical sensations — the rocking boat, cold water, exhaustion. First person puts you IN his body.


💬 Part 3: Word of the Day

perilous (adjective)

Full of danger. Extremely risky with a high chance of harm.


From the story: Jim’s solo voyage in the tiny boat is perilous — one wrong wave could drown him.


Think about it: What makes a situation perilous vs. just scary?


✅ Check Your Understanding

Answer these questions using what you’ve learned.


1. What does Jim manage to do during his solo adventure?

A) Nothing — he fails

B) He cuts the anchor rope and boards the Hispaniola

C) He swims to another island

D) He signals a passing ship


2. Why is Jim’s solo adventure the high point of his character arc?

A) He gets the treasure

B) He acts completely independently, making life-or-death decisions alone

C) He becomes captain

D) He defeats Silver in combat


3. Find one passage where Jim’s physical experience is especially vivid. Why does first person make sensory writing so effective?

💡 Hint: First person: “My arms ached” hits different than third person: “His arms ached.”

[Your answer]


4. The big debate: Is Jim a HERO or a RECKLESS KID? Write a paragraph defending one side using evidence from at least three chapters.

💡 Hint: Heroes often ARE reckless. The question is whether the risks are worth taking.

[Your answer]
