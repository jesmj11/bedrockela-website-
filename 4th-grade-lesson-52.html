Climax writing tips:

  • Slow down the most intense moment. Use short, punchy sentences.
  • Show physical reactions: My hands shook. My breath came in gasps.
  • Make the reader worry: will the narrator succeed?

Ending tips:

  • Show how the narrator has changed.
  • Echo something from the beginning.
  • The last line should stick with the reader.

pacing (noun)

The speed at which a story moves. Fast pacing uses short sentences and quick action. Slow pacing uses long descriptions and reflection.


From the story: During the climax, speed up with short sentences. During reflection, slow down.


Think about it: Look at your climax. Are your sentences short and punchy, or long and slow?
