📚 Lesson 53: Revision: Strengthen Your Voice

Writing Workshop — Revision

📚 Welcome!

Your first draft is done! Today, re-read it and make it BETTER. Focus on your narrator’s voice and on replacing weak words with strong ones.


Today you will:

  • Re-read your entire story
  • Revise for voice, details, and strong verbs
  • Use a revision checklist

Time: About 45-50 minutes


🧐 Part 1: Before You Read

Voice revision checklist:

✅ Does my narrator sound like a real person with a personality?

✅ Can I hear their voice, or do they sound generic?

✅ Do they react emotionally to events?

✅ Are there moments where I show their thoughts?


Strong verb revision:

Weak: I went to the door. → Strong: I crept toward the door.

Weak: She said angrily. → Strong: She snapped.

Weak: It was scary. → Strong: My skin prickled with dread.


📖 Part 2: Today’s Task

Re-read your ENTIRE story from start to finish. Read it OUT LOUD if possible.


Find your three weakest sentences and rewrite them with stronger verbs and more specific details.


💬 Part 3: Word of the Day

revise (revisited) (verb)

Re-seeing your work and making it stronger. You learned this in Unit 1. Now apply it to more complex writing.


From the story: Professional authors revise many times. Your story gets better every time you look at it with fresh eyes.


Think about it: What is the difference between fixing spelling (editing) and making the story better (revising)?


✅ Check Your Understanding

Answer these questions using what you’ve learned.


1. REVISION CHECKLIST — Rate yourself YES or NEEDS WORK:

  • Descriptive, sensory details: ___
  • Dialogue with quotation marks: ___
  • Strong verbs (crept, lunged, whispered): ___
  • My narrator changes by the end: ___
  • The last line is memorable: ___

    • 💡 Hint: Be honest! The best writers are their own toughest critics.
