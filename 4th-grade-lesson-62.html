üßê Part 1: Before You Read

In Oz, the settings were colorful and magical. In Treasure Island, the ship was tense but civilized. The Yukon in The Call of the Wild is something else entirely: it is a place where nature does not care about you. There are no good witches, no friendly doctors. There is only cold, hunger, and the law of survival.


Think about this: If you were suddenly dropped into a harsh environment with no preparation, what would you need to learn to survive?


What to look for:

  • What does Buck learn in this chapter?
  • How does the setting (the Yukon) force Buck to change?
  • What is the law of club and fang?

üìñ Part 2: Today‚Äôs Task

Read Chapter 2: The Law of Club and Fang.


Buck sees another dog killed for being weak. He learns to eat fast, steal food, sleep in the snow, and fight. The comfortable California dog is disappearing. Something older and wilder is waking up inside him.


Reading tip: Jack London uses setting as a FORCE in this story. The Yukon is not just a backdrop ‚Äî it is almost a character itself, shaping everything Buck becomes.

üí¨ Part 3: Word of the Day

adversity (noun)

Difficult or challenging circumstances. Hardship that tests your strength and forces you to adapt.


From the story: Buck faces extreme adversity in the Yukon ‚Äî cold, violence, starvation. But adversity is also what makes him stronger.


Think about it: Can adversity make someone (or an animal) stronger? Or does it only cause harm? Think about Buck and about characters from earlier books.
