üßê Part 1: Before You Read

So far, humans have mostly been obstacles or tools for Buck. They beat him, worked him, bought and sold him. John Thornton is the first human who treats Buck with genuine love. This creates a new conflict inside Buck: the pull of love vs. the pull of the wild.


Think about this: Can love hold someone back from becoming who they are meant to be? Is it possible for love to be both wonderful and a kind of chain?


What to look for:

  • How are the new owners (Hal, Charles, Mercedes) different from previous owners?
  • How does Thornton save Buck?
  • How does Buck feel about Thornton vs. how he feels about the wild?

üìñ Part 2: Today‚Äôs Task

Read Chapters 5-6. Chapter 5 is dark ‚Äî the bad owners push the dogs to collapse. Chapter 6 introduces Thornton and is one of the most emotional chapters in the book.


Reading tip: Notice the CONTRAST London creates. Chapter 5 shows the worst of humanity. Chapter 6 shows the best. London is asking: what is the relationship between humans and animals? Who benefits? Who suffers?

üí¨ Part 3: Word of the Day

devotion (noun)

Deep love and loyalty to someone or something. A powerful emotional bond that drives action.


From the story: Buck feels genuine devotion to Thornton ‚Äî a love so strong that it is the only thing keeping him from answering the call of the wild completely.


Think about it: Can devotion to one person conflict with devotion to your own nature? Is Buck‚Äôs love for Thornton holding him back or completing him?
