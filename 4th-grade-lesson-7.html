📚 Lesson 7: The Deadly Poppy Field

The Wonderful Wizard of Oz • Chapter 8: The Deadly Poppy Field

📚 Welcome!

Flowers. Beautiful, bright red flowers as far as you can see. Sounds lovely, right? Not this time. Today’s chapter is one of the most suspenseful in the whole book.


Today you will:

  • Read Chapter 8 of The Wonderful Wizard of Oz
  • Think about cause and effect
  • Notice how teamwork saves the day
  • Write in your reading journal

Time: About 45-50 minutes


🧐 Part 1: Before You Read

In stories, things that look safe can sometimes be dangerous. The author is about to use this trick on you.


Think about this: Can you think of something in real life that looks harmless but can actually be dangerous? (Maybe something in nature?)


🔍 What to look for while you read:

  • What happens when the group walks through the poppies?
  • Why are some characters affected and others aren’t?
  • How does the group solve this problem?

📖 Part 2: Read — Chapter 8: The Deadly Poppy Field

Open your digital copy and read Chapter 8: “The Deadly Poppy Field.”


This is a great chapter for noticing cause and effect — one thing causes another, which causes another. It’s like a chain reaction.


💡 Reading tip: Cause and effect means asking “What happened?” and “Why did it happen?” Try to connect events like links in a chain.


💬 Part 3: Word of the Day

poisonous (adjective)

Containing something harmful that can make you sick or cause damage, even if it looks safe.


From the story: The beautiful poppies are poisonous — their smell puts anyone who breathes it into a deep sleep they might never wake up from.


Think about it: Why is it more dangerous when something harmful looks beautiful? How does this connect to the idea that “things aren’t always what they seem”?


✅ Part 4: Check Your Understanding

Answer these questions using what you read.


1. Why do the poppies make Dorothy, Toto, and the Lion fall asleep?

A) They are tired from walking

B) A witch cast a sleeping spell on them

C) The poppies’ smell is poisonous to anything that breathes

D) It is nighttime and they are sleepy


2. Why aren’t the Scarecrow and Tin Woodman affected?

A) They held their breath

B) They ran through the field quickly

C) They are not made of flesh and don’t breathe

D) They have magical protection


3. Which is the best example of cause and effect from this chapter?

A) Dorothy walks through the poppies → she smells them → she falls asleep

B) Dorothy likes flowers → she picks some → she makes a bouquet

C) The Lion is brave → he fights the poppies → he wins

D) The Scarecrow is smart → he reads a book → he learns about flowers


4. How do the Scarecrow and the Tin Woodman save their friends? What does this show about their characters?

💡 Hint: Think about what qualities they use. Does the Tin Woodman show heart? Does the Scarecrow use his brain?

  • Why do you think the author put this obstacle in the story? What does it add?
