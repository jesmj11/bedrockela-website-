üßê Part 1: Before You Read

London‚Äôs toolkit:


  • IMAGERY: Descriptions that appeal to the five senses (sight, sound, touch, taste, smell)
  • SIMILE: A comparison using like or as ‚Äî The snow fell like ash from a cold fire.
  • METAPHOR: Saying something IS something else ‚Äî The trail was a white ribbon through the darkness.
  • PERSONIFICATION: Giving human qualities to non-human things ‚Äî The cold crept into his bones and whispered stay down.

London uses ALL of these constantly. Today you will find examples and then try writing your own.

üí¨ Part 3: Word of the Day

imagery (noun)

Vivid, descriptive language that appeals to the senses. Writing that makes you SEE, HEAR, FEEL, SMELL, or TASTE what is being described.


From the story: London‚Äôs imagery is so powerful that you can feel the Yukon cold in your bones while sitting in a warm room.


Think about it: Why is imagery more effective than just stating facts? What is the difference between the cold was bad and the cold bit through his fur like a thousand needles?
