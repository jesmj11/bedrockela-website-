üìö Welcome!

Your Unit 3 writing project is a descriptive essay about how a SETTING transforms a CHARACTER. This combines everything you have learned: setting as a force, character transformation, figurative language, and strong writing craft.


Today you will:

  • Understand the essay prompt
  • Choose your topic (from the books or original)
  • Plan your essay with an outline

Time: About 50 minutes


üßê Part 1: Before You Read

This essay is different from your Unit 1 and Unit 2 narratives. Those were STORIES. This is an ESSAY ‚Äî it makes an argument and supports it with evidence and description. But it still uses vivid writing!


Essay prompt options (choose ONE):


Option A: How does the Yukon setting transform Buck in The Call of the Wild? Use specific details and figurative language to describe the setting and show how it changes him.


Option B: How does the jungle setting shape who Mowgli becomes in The Jungle Book? Use specific details and figurative language to describe the setting and show how it shapes him.


Option C: Create an ORIGINAL setting and character. Describe a place so vividly that the reader can feel it, and show how that place transforms the character who enters it.


üìñ Part 2: Today‚Äôs Task

No new reading. This is a planning day.


If you choose Option A or B, flip through the book to find specific passages about the setting that you can reference. If you choose Option C, let your imagination go wild.


üí¨ Part 3: Word of the Day

vivid (adjective)

Producing strong, clear images in the mind. Writing that is so detailed and specific that the reader can picture it perfectly.


From the story: London‚Äôs descriptions of the Yukon are vivid ‚Äî you can see the snow, feel the cold, hear the wolves. Your essay should be just as vivid.


Think about it: What makes writing vivid? It is specificity. Not the place was cold but the wind drove ice crystals into every gap in his fur.

Choose your topic and plan your essay:


I am writing about (circle one): Option A (Buck/Yukon) / Option B (Mowgli/Jungle) / Option C (Original)


My setting: [Describe in 2-3 sentences ‚Äî what does it look, sound, feel like?]

Essay outline:


Introduction (2-3 sentences): Hook + state how the setting transforms the character


Body paragraph 1 ‚Äî THE SETTING (4-5 sentences): Describe the setting vividly using imagery, simile, metaphor. Make the reader FEEL it.


Body paragraph 2 ‚Äî THE TRANSFORMATION (4-5 sentences): Show how the setting changes the character. Use specific details or text evidence.


Body paragraph 3 ‚Äî THE MEANING (3-4 sentences): What does this transformation tell us about the nature vs. civilization theme? What is the deeper message?


Conclusion (2-3 sentences): Bring it together. End with a memorable final line.
