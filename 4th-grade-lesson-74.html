📚 Lesson 74: Drafting: Paint the Setting

Writing Workshop — Setting and Transformation Essay (Drafting)

📚 Welcome!

Today you write the first half of your essay: the introduction and the setting paragraph. Your goal is to make the reader FEEL the setting — not just know about it. Use every tool London taught you.


Today you will:

  • Write your introduction and setting paragraph
  • Use at least 2 examples of figurative language
  • Appeal to at least 3 senses

Time: About 45-50 minutes of writing


🧐 Part 1: Before You Read

Setting paragraph toolkit:


  • Use at least one SIMILE (like/as comparison)
  • Use at least one METAPHOR or PERSONIFICATION
  • Appeal to SIGHT, SOUND, and at least one other sense (touch, smell, taste)
  • Use SPECIFIC details, not vague ones

Examples:

Vague: The Yukon was cold and harsh.

Vivid: The Yukon was a white empire of silence, where the cold did not just surround you — it entered you, settling into your lungs like smoke from an ancient fire.


See the difference? Specificity and figurative language transform flat writing into powerful writing.


📖 Part 2: Today’s Task

No reading — WRITING DAY!


Use your outline from yesterday. Write your introduction (2-3 sentences) and your setting paragraph (4-5 sentences with figurative language).


Do not rush the setting paragraph. This is where you show off your descriptive skills. Make every sentence count.


💬 Part 3: Word of the Day

sensory (adjective)

Relating to the five senses: sight, sound, touch, smell, taste. Sensory writing helps the reader experience the scene physically.


From the story: London’s writing is deeply sensory — you do not just read about the Yukon, you feel the cold on your skin and hear the crunch of snow under paws.


Think about it: Which sense is easiest for you to write about? Which is hardest? Push yourself to use a sense you do not usually think about.


✍️ Draft: Introduction + Setting Paragraph

Write your introduction and setting paragraph.


Introduction checklist:

✅ Opens with a hook (a vivid image, question, or bold statement)

✅ Names the setting and character

Setting paragraph checklist:

✅ At least one simile

✅ At least one metaphor or personification

✅ Appeals to 3+ senses

✅ Specific details (not vague)

✅ 4-5 sentences minimum
