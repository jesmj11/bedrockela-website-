Finding Evidence and Making Inferences

Hey Trail Blazers! I'm Billy, and I'm here to teach you one of the most important climbing skills: finding evidence in text and making inferences. It's like being a detective while reading!
The Mystery of Stormy Summit

Erik pulled his heavy wool coat tighter around his shoulders as he approached the old cabin. The wind howled through the pine trees, and dark clouds gathered overhead. He had been hiking for three hours through the Norwegian mountains, following an old map his grandfather had given him.

The cabin looked abandoned. Paint peeled from the wooden walls, and one of the shutters hung crooked. But Erik noticed something odd: fresh footprints in the snow leading to the front door, and a thin trail of smoke rising from the chimney.

Erik's heart pounded as he knocked on the weathered door. After a moment, he heard shuffling footsteps inside. The door creaked open, revealing an old woman with kind eyes and a warm smile.

"I've been expecting you," she said with a twinkle in her eye. "Your grandfather told me you might come. Welcome to Stormy Summit!"

üßó Pre-Climb: Understanding Evidence vs. Inference

üìã TEXT EVIDENCE

What the text DIRECTLY tells us

  • Exact words from the story
  • Facts stated by the author
  • Details we can point to

Example: "Erik pulled his heavy wool coat tighter"

üîç INFERENCE

What we figure out using clues

  • Reading between the lines
  • Using text clues + what we know
  • Making educated guesses

Example: It's probably cold outside (coat + wind)

üéØ The Climb: Evidence Hunt

Part 1: Find the Evidence

Question: What evidence shows that Erik has been traveling for a while?

Text Evidence: "He had been hiking for three hours through the Norwegian mountains"

Question: What evidence suggests the cabin looks old and unused?

Text Evidence: "Paint peeled from the wooden walls, and one of the shutters hung crooked"

Question: What evidence shows someone might actually be in the cabin?

Text Evidence: "fresh footprints in the snow" and "thin trail of smoke rising from the chimney"

Part 2: Make Inferences

Inference Challenge: Use the evidence to make smart guesses!

  1. Why is Erik wearing a heavy wool coat?
    Inference: It's cold outside (Evidence: wind howling, dark clouds, snow)
  2. How do you think Erik is feeling when his heart pounds?
    Inference: Nervous or excited (Evidence: mysterious cabin, unexpected signs of life)
  3. What is the relationship between Erik's grandfather and the old woman?
    Inference: They know each other (Evidence: "Your grandfather told me you might come")
  4. Was this meeting planned?
    Inference: Yes, at least partially (Evidence: "I've been expecting you")
Excellent detective work! Remember: Evidence is what the text TELLS you directly. Inferences are what you FIGURE OUT using those clues plus your own knowledge. Both are essential for understanding stories!

Crafting Strong Paragraphs

Writing is like building a stone cairn (trail marker) on a mountain - each stone has to be carefully placed to create something strong and useful. Let's learn to build paragraph cairns!

üßó Pre-Climb: The Paragraph Formula

üìù The TOPIC Paragraph Structure

  1. Topic Sentence (main idea)
  2. Organized Details (3-4 supporting facts)
  3. Powerful Conclusion (wrap it up)
  4. Interesting Language (descriptive words)
  5. Clear Connection (ideas flow together)

üéØ The Climb: Building a Paragraph Cairn

Example: Billy's Perfect Paragraph

(Topic Sentence) Norwegian mountains are incredible places for adventure. (Detail 1) The dramatic fjords cut deep valleys between towering peaks that seem to touch the sky. (Detail 2) Ancient Viking paths wind through forests of spruce and pine, where hikers can discover hidden waterfalls and crystal-clear lakes. (Detail 3) During summer, the midnight sun creates a magical glow that lasts almost all night, while winter brings the dancing northern lights across the star-filled darkness. (Conclusion) With so many natural wonders to explore, Norway's mountains offer unforgettable experiences for any adventurer.

Paragraph Analysis Practice

Find these parts in Billy's paragraph:

  1. What is the topic sentence? (Norwegian mountains are incredible places for adventure.)
  2. How many supporting details are there? (3 details about fjords, paths, and seasons)
  3. What descriptive words make it interesting? (dramatic, towering, crystal-clear, magical, etc.)
  4. How does the conclusion connect to the topic sentence? (Both talk about adventures and experiences)

üèîÔ∏è Expedition #3: Word Mountain

Building Vocabulary Through Context Clues

Vocabulary is like having the right gear for mountain climbing - the more words you know, the better you can understand and communicate! Let's discover how to figure out new words using context clues.

üßó Pre-Climb: Types of Context Clues

üîç Definition Clues

The text directly tells you what the word means

"A fjord, which is a narrow inlet of the sea between cliffs, stretched before them."

üìö Example Clues

Examples help you understand the meaning

"The terrain was treacherous: loose rocks, steep cliffs, and hidden ice patches."

‚ÜîÔ∏è Contrast Clues

The opposite meaning is given

"Unlike his timid brother, Erik was bold and fearless on the mountain."

üß© Inference Clues

You use surrounding details to guess

"The hiker was parched after walking all day in the hot sun without water."

üéØ The Climb: Context Clue Detective Work

The Avalanche Warning

The meteorologist's forecast was ominous: heavy snowfall and dangerous winds were expected. The mountain rescue team had to postpone their expedition because the conditions would be too perilous for safe travel.

Erik was disappointed but understood the decision. His grandmother had always told him to be prudent in the mountains - better to wait for good weather than to risk lives. She had survived many harsh winters by being cautious and making wise choices.

While waiting, Erik studied the topography of the region. He spread detailed maps across the table, examining the elevation changes, valleys, and ridges they would encounter. Understanding the landscape was crucial for navigation.

Finally, the storm subsided, and the team could begin their ascent. They moved steadily upward, each step bringing them closer to the summit. Erik felt exhilarated by the challenge and the beautiful scenery surrounding them.

Word Detective Challenges

1. OMINOUS - "The meteorologist's forecast was ominous: heavy snowfall and dangerous winds were expected."

Type of clue: Definition/Example - dangerous conditions are described

Meaning: Threatening, suggesting something bad will happen

2. PERILOUS - "the conditions would be too perilous for safe travel"

Type of clue: Contrast - opposite of "safe"

Meaning: Dangerous, risky

3. PRUDENT - "be prudent in the mountains - better to wait for good weather than to risk lives... by being cautious and making wise choices"

Type of clue: Definition - explained as "cautious and making wise choices"

Meaning: Careful, showing good judgment

4. TOPOGRAPHY - "He spread detailed maps across the table, examining the elevation changes, valleys, and ridges"

Type of clue: Example - examples given: elevation, valleys, ridges

Meaning: The physical features of land/landscape

5. EXHILARATED - context about challenge and beautiful scenery, after reaching their goal

Type of clue: Inference - positive context suggests good feeling

Meaning: Very excited, thrilled

Outstanding vocabulary climbing! Remember: when you meet a new word, look around it for clues. The author usually gives you hints through definitions, examples, opposites, or context. Keep building your word gear!
