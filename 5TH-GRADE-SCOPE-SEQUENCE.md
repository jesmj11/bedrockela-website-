# 5th Grade ELA Curriculum: 180-Day Scope & Sequence
**Built Around Public Domain Classics & CCSS Standards**

## Overview

This comprehensive 180-day curriculum integrates classic public domain literature with rigorous reading comprehension instruction aligned to Common Core State Standards for 5th grade. Students will engage with timeless coming-of-age stories and adventures while building essential literacy skills through carefully crafted text passages and comprehension questions.

## Core Texts (All Public Domain)

1. **The Secret Garden** by Frances Hodgson Burnett (1911) - Days 1-30
2. **Treasure Island** by Robert Louis Stevenson (1883) - Days 31-60
3. **Little Women** by Louisa May Alcott (1868) - Days 61-90
4. **The Railway Children** by E. Nesbit (1906) - Days 91-120
5. **Anne of Green Gables** by L.M. Montgomery (1908) - Days 121-150
6. **The Wind in the Willows** by Kenneth Grahame (1908) - Days 151-180

## Vocabulary & Assessment Pattern

Following BedrockELA standards for grades 3-5:
- **2 vocabulary words per day** from actual reading passages
- **8-word vocabulary assessments** every 5th day (Days 5, 10, 15, 20, 25, 30, etc.)
- **Regular days**: 7 pages (title, welcome, vocab, reading, comprehension, journal, completion)
- **Assessment days**: 6 pages (shorter - vocab quiz + comprehension test, no new reading)

Total vocabulary: **288 words** from classic literature (144 days × 2 words)
Total assessments: **36 assessment days** (every 5th day)

## Key CCSS Standards Focus

### Reading Literature (RL.5)
- **RL.5.1**: Quote accurately when explaining text and drawing inferences
- **RL.5.2**: Determine theme from details; summarize text
- **RL.5.3**: Compare and contrast characters, settings, or events
- **RL.5.4**: Determine meaning of words/phrases including figurative language
- **RL.5.5**: Explain how chapters, scenes, or stanzas fit together
- **RL.5.6**: Describe how narrator's or speaker's point of view influences events
- **RL.5.7**: Analyze how visual/multimedia elements contribute to meaning
- **RL.5.9**: Compare and contrast stories in same genre

### Reading Foundational Skills (RF.5)
- **RF.5.3**: Know and apply grade-level phonics and word analysis skills
- **RF.5.4**: Read with sufficient accuracy and fluency to support comprehension

### Writing (W.5)
- **W.5.1**: Write opinion pieces with reasons and information
- **W.5.2**: Write informative/explanatory texts with clear topics
- **W.5.3**: Write narratives with descriptive details and clear sequences
- **W.5.4**: Produce clear writing appropriate to task and audience
- **W.5.9**: Draw evidence from literary texts to support analysis

### Speaking & Listening (SL.5)
- **SL.5.1**: Engage in collaborative discussions with diverse partners
- **SL.5.2**: Summarize written text read aloud or information presented
- **SL.5.3**: Summarize points a speaker makes

### Language (L.5)
- **L.5.1**: Demonstrate command of grammar and usage
- **L.5.2**: Demonstrate command of capitalization, punctuation, and spelling
- **L.5.4**: Determine meaning of unknown words using context clues, roots, affixes
- **L.5.5**: Demonstrate understanding of figurative language and word relationships

## UNIT 1: The Secret Garden (Days 1-30)
**By Frances Hodgson Burnett**

### Key Themes
- Transformation and growth (both garden and children)
- Healing power of nature
- Friendship and connection
- Overcoming grief and loss
- The magic of positive thinking

### Character Arc
- **Mary Lennox**: Spoiled, sour child → caring, joyful friend
- **Colin Craven**: Sickly, fearful invalid → healthy, confident boy
- **Dickon Sowerby**: Patient, nature-loving guide
- **Martha & Ben Weatherstaff**: Supporting characters who nurture growth

### Reading Schedule (30 Days)

**Days 1-5: Chapters 1-3** - Mary in India, parents die, sent to Misselthwaite Manor
- Vocab: contrary, wretched, tyrannical, disagree able, luxuriant, ayah, cholera, immune
- Assessment Day 5: 8-word quiz + comprehension test

**Days 6-10: Chapters 4-6** - Mary explores the manor, meets Martha, hears about the secret garden
- Vocab: bleak, moorland, peculiar, sallow, dismal, desolate, sullen, temper
- Assessment Day 10: 8-word quiz

**Days 11-15: Chapters 7-9** - Mary finds the key and the door to the garden
- Vocab: threshold, melancholy, persistent, robin, burrow, shabby, curious, venture
- Assessment Day 15: 8-word quiz

**Days 16-20: Chapters 10-13** - Mary meets Dickon, begins working in the garden
- Vocab: moor, hearty, trustworthy, tame, charming, genuine, enthusiasm, sprouting
- Assessment Day 20: 8-word quiz

**Days 21-25: Chapters 14-17** - Mary discovers Colin, the hidden invalid
- Vocab: hysteria, invalid, tantrum, wail, resent, pitiful, wretched, spoiled
- Assessment Day 25: 8-word quiz

**Days 26-30: Chapters 18-21** - Colin begins to believe he can get well
- Vocab: transform, vigor, nourish, convince, miracle, trembling, hopeful, magnificent
- Assessment Day 30: 8-word quiz + Unit Review

**Comprehension Focus:**
- Character transformation tracking
- Symbolism of the garden
- Theme identification: healing, growth, friendship
- Point of view: third-person omniscient
- Setting influence on characters

**Writing Connections:**
- Journal: Describe a place that makes you feel happy or peaceful
- Opinion: Is the "magic" in the garden real or imaginary?
- Narrative: Write about a time you helped something grow (plant, skill, friendship)

- Vocab: treachery, conspiracy, provisions, scoundrel, desperate, alliance, fortify, stockade

**Days 46-50: Chapters 13-17** - Jim meets Ben Gunn, conflict with pirates begins
- Vocab: marooned, eccentric, hermit, salvage, negotiate, barricade, siege, cunning
- Assessment Day 50: 8-word quiz

**Days 51-55: Chapters 18-22** - Battle at the stockade, Jim's adventures alone

**Days 56-60: Chapters 23-34** - Final confrontations, treasure found, journey home

## Scope & Sequence Summary

### Total Content
- **6 complete classic novels** (all public domain)
- **180 total lessons** (full school year)
- **288 vocabulary words** from actual text (2/day × 144 regular days)
- **36 assessments** (every 5th day)
- **144 comprehension lessons** with text-dependent questions
- **144 journal prompts** for writing practice

### Standards Coverage
All CCSS standards for 5th grade ELA comprehensively addressed through:
- Close reading of complex texts
- Text-dependent comprehension questions
- Vocabulary in context
- Writing across genres (narrative, opinion, informative)
- Speaking & listening through discussion prompts
- Grammar and language conventions

### Lesson Structure (CONSISTENT ACROSS ALL 180 DAYS)

**Regular Days (144 days) - 7 pages:**
1. **Title Page** - Lesson number, book title, day count
2. **Welcome Page** - Overview, reading passage preview
3. **Vocabulary Page** - 2 words from text with definitions, example sentences
4. **Reading Page** - Text passage from the book (2-4 paragraphs)
5. **Comprehension Page** - 4-5 text-dependent questions
6. **Journal Page** - Creative writing prompt connected to reading
7. **Completion Page** - Progress tracker, "Great job!" encouragement

**Assessment Days (36 days: 5, 10, 15, 20, 25, 30, etc.) - 6 pages:**
1. **Title Page** - "Assessment Day" designation
2. **Welcome Page** - Review instructions
3. **Vocabulary Quiz** - Multiple choice test on 8 words from the week
4. **Comprehension Test** - 5-6 questions reviewing the week's reading
5. **Writing Response** - Short essay question on theme/character/plot
6. **Completion Page** - "Assessment complete!" progress tracker

### Difficulty Progression
- **Unit 1 (Secret Garden)**: Accessible, character-driven, relatable themes
- **Unit 2 (Treasure Island)**: Adventure, complex plot, morally ambiguous characters
- **Unit 3 (Little Women)**: Longer, multi-character, historical context
- **Unit 4 (Railway Children)**: Mystery elements, social themes
- **Unit 5 (Anne of Green Gables)**: Rich vocabulary, figurative language, emotional depth
- **Unit 6 (Wind in Willows)**: Sophisticated themes, British idioms, allegory

## **Grammar & Language Standards Integration**

### **Weekly Pattern (Days 1-4)**
- **Monday (Day 1):** Grammar Practice (CCSS L.5.1)
- **Tuesday (Day 2):** Language Skills (CCSS L.5.4, L.5.5)
- **Wednesday (Day 3):** Grammar Practice (continued)
- **Thursday (Day 4):** Language Skills (continued)
- **Friday (Day 5):** Assessment (includes grammar & language quiz)

### **36-Week Grammar Rotation (Days 1 & 3)**

**Weeks 1-6: Secret Garden - Conjunctions & Tenses**
1. Conjunctions - Coordinating (FANBOYS, joining words/phrases/sentences)
2. Conjunctions - Subordinating (because, although, when, if, since; complex sentences)
3. Correlative Conjunctions (either/or, neither/nor, both/and; parallel structure)
4. Verb Tenses - Present, Past, Future (simple tenses, helping verbs)
5. Verb Tenses - Perfect Tenses (present/past/future perfect, time relationships)
6. Verb Tense Shifts (maintaining consistency, intentional shifts)

**Weeks 7-12: Treasure Island - Agreement & Prepositions**
7. Subject-Verb Agreement - Basic (singular/plural matching)
8. Subject-Verb Agreement - Tricky Cases (compound subjects, indefinite pronouns, inverted sentences)
9. Prepositions & Prepositional Phrases (common prepositions, object of preposition)
10. Prepositional Phrases as Modifiers (adjective phrases, adverb phrases)
11. Interjections & Punctuation (expressing emotion, punctuation rules)
12. Sentence Types - Declarative & Interrogative (statements vs. questions)

**Weeks 13-18: Little Women - Sentence Structures**
13. Sentence Types - Imperative & Exclamatory (commands, strong feelings, implied "you")
14. Simple Sentences (one independent clause, subject and predicate)
15. Compound Sentences (two independent clauses, comma + conjunction, semicolon)
16. Complex Sentences (independent + dependent clause, comma placement)
17. Expanding Sentences with Adjectives (single/multiple adjectives, predicate adjectives)
18. Expanding Sentences with Adverbs (modifying verbs/adjectives/adverbs, placement)

**Weeks 19-24: Railway Children - Punctuation Mastery**
19. Commas in a Series (lists of three or more, Oxford comma)
20. Commas After Introductory Elements (words, phrases, clauses at beginning)
21. Commas with Coordinating Conjunctions (in compound sentences, NOT in compound subjects/predicates)
22. Commas to Set Off Direct Address (names/titles when speaking to someone)
23. Quotation Marks - Direct Speech (punctuation inside quotes, comma before/period inside)
24. Quotation Marks - Titles (short works vs. long works, underlining/italics)

**Weeks 25-30: Anne of Green Gables - Apostrophes & Pronouns**
25. Apostrophes - Contractions (common contractions, it's vs. its)
26. Apostrophes - Possessives (singular possessives, plural possessives)
27. Pronouns - Subject & Object (I/me, he/him, she/her, we/us, they/them)
28. Pronouns - Possessive (my/mine, your/yours, his, her/hers, its, our/ours, their/theirs)
29. Pronouns - Reflexive & Intensive (myself, yourself, himself, herself; reflexive vs. intensive)
30. Pronoun-Antecedent Agreement (matching number, indefinite pronouns, clear antecedents)

**Weeks 31-36: Wind in the Willows - Mechanics & Review**
31. Avoiding Pronoun Confusion (vague references, too many pronouns, clarity)
32. Capitalization - Proper Nouns (names, places, specific things, titles of people)
33. Capitalization - Titles & First Words (first word of sentence/quote, important words in titles)
34. Spelling - Common Patterns (i before e, silent e, doubling consonants)
35. Commonly Confused Words (there/their/they're, to/too/two, your/you're, then/than)
36. Grammar Review & Application (identify parts of speech, fix errors, edit paragraph)

### **36-Week Language Rotation (Days 2 & 4)**

**Weeks 1-6: Secret Garden - Context Clues & Basic Roots**
1. Context Clues - Using Definitions (signal words like "is," "means," "or")
2. Context Clues - Using Examples (signal words like "such as," "including," "for example")
3. Context Clues - Using Synonyms (restatement in different words, similar meanings)
4. Context Clues - Using Antonyms (opposition words, what it's NOT tells what it IS)
5. Greek & Latin Roots - Common Set 1 (auto, bio, geo, graph - self, life, earth, write)
6. Greek & Latin Roots - Common Set 2 (phon, photo, tele, scope - sound, light, far, see)

**Weeks 7-12: Treasure Island - More Roots & Prefixes**
7. Greek & Latin Roots - Common Set 3 (port, dict, scrib/script, ject - carry, say, write, throw)
8. Greek & Latin Roots - Common Set 4 (struct, form, tract, rupt - build, shape, pull, break)
9. Prefixes - Negative Meanings (un-, in-, im-, non-, dis- for creating opposites)
10. Prefixes - Number (uni-, bi-, tri-, multi- for precise meanings)
11. Prefixes - Time & Order (pre-, post-, re-, mid- for sequence and repetition)
12. Prefixes - Size & Degree (super-, sub-, over-, under- for amount and position)

**Weeks 13-18: Little Women - Suffixes & Word Relationships**
13. Suffixes - Making Nouns (-er/-or, -tion/-sion, -ness for person/act/state)
14. Suffixes - Making Adjectives (-ful, -less, -able, -ous for qualities)
15. Suffixes - Making Verbs (-ify, -ize, -en for action words)
16. Suffixes - Making Adverbs (-ly for "in a certain way")
17. Synonyms - Shades of Meaning (similar but not identical, precise choices)
18. Antonyms - Opposites (direct opposites, using for contrast)

**Weeks 19-24: Railway Children - Homophones & Figurative Language**
19. Homographs - Same Spelling, Different Meanings (bow/bow, lead/lead; context determines)
20. Homophones - Sound-Alikes (there/their/they're, to/too/two; spelling based on meaning)
21. Multiple-Meaning Words (many dictionary definitions, context clues)
22. Similes (comparisons using "like" or "as," vivid descriptions)
23. Metaphors (comparisons without "like" or "as," implied relationships)
24. Personification (giving human qualities to non-human things)

**Weeks 25-30: Anne of Green Gables - More Figurative Language**
25. Hyperbole (extreme exaggeration for effect, literal vs. figurative)
26. Idioms (common expressions with non-literal meanings like "piece of cake")
27. Onomatopoeia (words that sound like what they mean: buzz, crash, hiss)
28. Alliteration (repetition of beginning consonant sounds, effect on rhythm)
29. Proverbs & Sayings (traditional wisdom, "A stitch in time saves nine")
30. Connotation - Positive & Negative (emotional associations, childish vs. youthful)

**Weeks 31-36: Wind in the Willows - Advanced Vocabulary**
31. Word Relationships - Part to Whole (wheel to car as page to book; analogies)
32. Word Relationships - Cause & Effect (studying to learning; logical connections)
33. Academic Vocabulary (words used across subjects like analyze, compare, conclude)
34. Domain-Specific Words (literature terms: character, setting, plot, theme)
35. Word Choice for Effect (powerful vs. weak verbs, specific vs. vague nouns)
36. Language Review & Application (use all strategies, identify figurative language, analyze author's choices)

### **Integration with Classic Literature**
All grammar and language examples are drawn from the current book:
- **Secret Garden:** Descriptive language, transformation vocabulary, garden imagery
- **Treasure Island:** Nautical terms, adventure vocabulary, dialogue punctuation, pirate speech
- **Little Women:** Family relationship terms, historical vocabulary, character dialogue, sisterhood language
- **Railway Children:** British idioms, mystery vocabulary, train terminology, kindness themes
- **Anne of Green Gables:** Figurative language ("scope for imagination"), poetic expressions, friendship vocabulary
- **Wind in the Willows:** Animal personification, British countryside terms, friendship language, river imagery
