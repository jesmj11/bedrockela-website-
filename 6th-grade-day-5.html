üìù Vocabulary Quiz

Fill in the blanks with the correct vocabulary word from this week:

1. Mark Twain used _______ to make fun of human nature.

Hint: the use of humor, irony, or exaggeration to criticize

2. Tom Sawyer has an _______ structure with many different adventures.

Hint: made up of separate episodes or events

3. The _______ River was central to life in 1840s Missouri.

Hint: the great river along which Tom's town sits

4. The story takes place in the _______ South before the Civil War.

Hint: existing before a war, especially the Civil War

5. Twain's characters speak in _______ dialect rather than formal English.

Hint: informal language used in everyday conversation

6. Tom _______ his friends into doing his work for him.

Hint: charmed or enchanted, sometimes in a deceptive way

7. Aunt Polly _______ to scold Tom for his mischief.

Hint: began or started

8. Tom could be quite _______ when he didn't get his way.

Hint: bad-tempered and difficult to deal with

9. Tom _______ he could trick Ben into whitewashing the fence.

Hint: thought or supposed

10. Tom would _______ on ways to avoid work.

Hint: to think deeply or focus one's mind

11. Tom made himself _______ in front of Becky Thatcher.

Hint: standing out and attracting attention

12. Tom showed no _______ when it came to his schoolwork.

Hint: careful and persistent work or effort

13. The other boys looked at Tom with _______ when he failed.

Hint: contemptuous ridicule or mockery

14. Tom was _______ when Becky saw him make a fool of himself.

Hint: deeply embarrassed or humiliated

15. Tom was _______ in his pursuit of Becky's affection.

Hint: admirably purposeful and determined

16. The boys agreed on a _______ at midnight in the graveyard.

Hint: a planned meeting at a specific time and place

17. Tom pretended to have a serious _______ to avoid school.

Hint: an illness or disease

18. Tom found great _______ in his freedom with Huck.

Hint: pleasure or satisfaction

19. School was an _______ to Tom's adventurous plans.

Hint: a hindrance or obstruction

20. Huck Finn was treated as a _______ by the respectable townspeople.

Hint: an outcast rejected by society
