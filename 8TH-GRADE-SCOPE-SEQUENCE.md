# 180-Day YA Classics Curriculum Map
## BedrockELA Classic Adventures Series

### **Course Overview**
12 thrilling classic novels adapted for modern 8th graders, designed to build reading love through page-turning adventure while developing critical thinking and literary analysis skills.

### **Assessment Schedule**
- **Monday-Thursday:** Reading, discussion, activities
- **Friday:** Weekly assessment (comprehension, vocabulary, writing)
- **Every 3rd Friday:** Unit test covering the completed book

### **Pacing Strategy**
- **High-action books:** 2-3 weeks to maintain momentum
- **Complex classics:** 3-4 weeks for deeper analysis
- **Shorter psychological thrillers:** 2 weeks for intensity

## **Assessment Structure**

### **Weekly Assessments (Every Friday)**
- **Comprehension Questions:** 10-15 multiple choice and short answer
- **Vocabulary:** 8-10 words from the week's reading
- **Quick Write:** One paragraph response to theme or character question
- **Time:** 30-40 minutes

### **Book Tests (Every 3rd Friday)**
- **Reading Comprehension:** Passage analysis with questions
- **Character Analysis:** Essay question on character development
- **Theme Identification:** Short answer on major themes
- **Vocabulary:** 15-20 words from entire book
- **Creative Component:** Alternative ending, character diary, or modern adaptation
- **Time:** Full class period

### **Quarter Exams**
- **Cross-textual Analysis:** Compare themes across quarter's books
- **Literary Terms:** Genre characteristics, narrative techniques
- **Extended Writing:** 3-paragraph essay on major theme
- **Time:** Full class period

### **Final Portfolio**
- **Reading Log:** Reflections on all 12 books
- **Character Gallery:** Favorite characters with analysis
- **Theme Connections:** How books connect to modern world
- **Personal Growth:** Reading development throughout year

## **Grammar & Language Standards Integration**

### **Weekly Pattern (Days 1-4)**
- **Monday (Day 1):** Grammar Practice (CCSS L.8.1)
- **Tuesday (Day 2):** Language Skills (CCSS L.8.4, L.8.5)
- **Wednesday (Day 3):** Grammar Practice (continued)
- **Thursday (Day 4):** Language Skills (continued)
- **Friday (Day 5):** Assessment (includes grammar & language quiz)

### **36-Week Grammar Rotation (Days 1 & 3)**

**Weeks 1-9: Verbals & Voice**
1. Verbals - Gerunds (identify vs. present participles, gerunds as subjects/objects)
2. Verbals - Participles (present/past, participial phrases for description)
3. Verbals - Infinitives (as nouns/adjectives/adverbs, split infinitives)
4. Active vs. Passive Voice (identify, when to use each, convert between)
5. Mood - Indicative & Imperative (statements vs. commands)
6. Mood - Subjunctive (wishes, demands, "If I were..." constructions)
7. Sentence Structure - Simple & Compound (independent clauses, FANBOYS)
8. Sentence Structure - Complex (subordinating conjunctions, clause placement)
9. Sentence Structure - Compound-Complex (multiple clauses, punctuation)

**Weeks 10-18: Punctuation & Agreement**
10. Comma Usage - Series & Coordinate Adjectives (Oxford comma, cumulative vs. coordinate)
11. Comma Usage - Introductory Elements (words, phrases, clauses)
12. Comma Usage - Nonrestrictive Elements (essential vs. non-essential, which vs. that)
13. Semicolons & Colons (joining clauses, introducing lists)
14. Dashes & Parentheses (emphasis vs. de-emphasis)
15. Ellipses & Quotation Integration (omitting words, embedding quotes)
16. Subject-Verb Agreement - Tricky Cases (collective nouns, indefinite pronouns, inverted order)
17. Pronoun-Antecedent Agreement (indefinite pronouns, collective noun antecedents)
18. Pronoun Case - Who vs. Whom (subject vs. object pronouns)

**Weeks 19-27: Clarity & Style**
19. Adjective vs. Adverb Usage (good/well, comparative/superlative forms)
20. Misplaced & Dangling Modifiers (placing modifiers correctly, fixing danglers)
21. Parallel Structure (in lists, comparisons, correlative conjunctions)
22. Sentence Fragments (identifying incomplete thoughts, dependent clauses as sentences)
23. Run-On Sentences & Comma Splices (identifying, four ways to fix)
24. Verb Tense Consistency (maintaining timeline, intentional shifts for effect)
25. Conditional Sentences (real vs. unreal conditions, if-then structures)
26. Direct vs. Indirect Speech (reporting what others said, tense shifts)
27. Appositives (renaming nouns, restrictive vs. non-restrictive)

**Weeks 28-36: Advanced Techniques**
28. Relative Clauses (who, whom, whose, which, that; essential vs. non-essential)
29. Avoiding Wordiness (cutting redundancy, tightening structure)
30. Formal vs. Informal Language (academic vs. conversational tone)
31. Avoiding Clichés (recognizing overused phrases, fresh alternatives)
32. Sentence Variety for Effect (short for impact, long for flow)
33. Transitions Between Ideas (logical connectors, paragraph coherence)
34. Emphasis Through Structure (periodic vs. cumulative sentences)
35. Avoiding Ambiguity (clear pronoun references, precise word choice)
36. Grammar Review & Application (identify multiple errors, apply all skills to revision)

### **36-Week Language Rotation (Days 2 & 4)**

**Weeks 1-9: Context Clues & Word Parts**
1. Context Clues - Definition & Restatement (signal words, appositives)
2. Context Clues - Example & Illustration (learning from what follows)
3. Context Clues - Contrast & Antonym (opposition signal words)
4. Context Clues - Inference from General Sense (overall meaning, background knowledge)
5. Greek Roots - Foundation (graph, phon, tele, bio, geo)
6. Greek Roots - Advanced (chron, morph, path, psych, dem)
7. Latin Roots - Foundation (port, dict, scrib, ject, struct)
8. Latin Roots - Advanced (jud, liber, mort, tempor, ver)
9. Prefixes - Negation (un-, in-, im-, il-, ir-, non-, dis-)

**Weeks 10-18: Affixes & Relationships**
10. Prefixes - Number & Quantity (mono-, bi-, tri-, multi-, poly-, semi-)
11. Prefixes - Position & Direction (pre-, post-, trans-, sub-, super-, inter-)
12. Suffixes - Noun Formation (-tion, -ment, -ness, -ity, -ance, -ence)
13. Suffixes - Adjective Formation (-able, -ible, -ful, -less, -ous, -ive)
14. Suffixes - Verb Formation (-ify, -ize, -ate, -en)
15. Word Relationships - Synonyms (shades of meaning, connotation differences)
16. Word Relationships - Antonyms (direct opposites, gradable vs. absolute)
17. Word Relationships - Analogies (part to whole, cause to effect)
18. Figurative Language - Simile & Metaphor (explicit vs. implied comparisons)

**Weeks 19-27: Figurative Language & Meaning**
19. Figurative Language - Personification (human traits to non-human, effect on tone)
20. Figurative Language - Hyperbole (extreme exaggeration for effect)
21. Figurative Language - Idioms (common expressions, cultural context)
22. Figurative Language - Allusion (references to mythology, literature, history)
23. Denotation vs. Connotation (dictionary vs. emotional association)
24. Euphemisms & Loaded Language (softening realities, persuasive word choice)
25. Academic Vocabulary - Tier 2 Words (cross-disciplinary words like analyze, interpret)
26. Domain-Specific Vocabulary (literature terms, technical terms from texts)
27. Multiple-Meaning Words (same spelling, different meanings, using context)

**Weeks 28-36: Advanced Vocabulary & Style**
28. Homophones & Homographs (sound-alikes, same spelling different pronunciation)
29. Word Etymology - Origins (how words entered English, borrowed words)
30. Semantic Change - How Meanings Shift (words that changed over time)
31. Jargon & Slang (specialized vs. informal language, appropriate contexts)
32. Oxymorons & Paradoxes (contradictory terms together, meaning through contrast)
33. Onomatopoeia & Sound Words (words that imitate sounds, effect on imagery)
34. Symbolism in Language (objects/colors representing ideas)
35. Tone & Word Choice (how diction creates mood, formal vs. informal)
36. Language Review & Application (decode complex vocabulary, analyze figurative language)

## **Series Themes Progression**

### **Quarter 1:** Wonder and Discovery
Students experience the excitement of scientific exploration and adventure

### **Quarter 2:** Human Ingenuity
Focus on problem-solving, survival, and engineering creativity

### **Quarter 3:** Dark Science
Examination of ethical issues and psychological complexity

### **Quarter 4:** Timeless Values
Classic themes of heroism, justice, and moral choice

This progression moves from pure excitement and engagement to increasingly sophisticated literary analysis, building both reading love and critical thinking skills throughout the year.
