## 📖 Story Overview

Alice is bored on a summer day until she sees a White Rabbit with a pocket watch run past. Following him down a rabbit hole, she tumbles into Wonderland—a bizarre world where nothing makes sense! She grows and shrinks, attends a mad tea party, plays croquet with a queen who yells "Off with their heads!", and meets talking animals, grinning cats, and living playing cards. Can Alice find her way home from this topsy-turvy world?

**Key Characters:**
- **Alice** - Curious, logical girl
- **White Rabbit** - Always late, always worried
- **Cheshire Cat** - Mysterious, grinning cat
- **Mad Hatter** - Host of the endless tea party
- **March Hare** - The Hatter's tea party friend
- **Queen of Hearts** - Temperamental ruler
- **Caterpillar** - Philosophical creature on mushroom
- **Mock Turtle** - Tells sad stories

## ✍️ Writing Focus: VOCABULARY JOURNALS & OPINION

**Major shift: Reading journals → Vocabulary journals**

**Days 121-130:** Launch vocabulary journals
- Each day: pick 2-3 interesting words from reading
- Write: word, definition, use in a sentence, draw a picture
- Focus on Carroll's wordplay and made-up words

**Days 131-140:** Wordplay writing
- Create your own portmanteau words
- Write poems with made-up words (like "Jabberwocky")
- Riddles and puns
- Practice creative, playful writing

**Days 141-150:** Formal opinion writing (introduced!)
- Opinion essay structure: intro, reasons, conclusion
- Topics like "Is Alice brave or reckless?" "Should the Queen be obeyed?"
- Students recognize they've been giving opinions all along in journals
- Now just adding structure

**Day 150 Capstone:**
- Opinion essay: "What is the most important lesson from Alice's adventure?"
- Spans all six units of the year
- Evidence from multiple books read

## 🎮 Grammar & Language Skills

### Grammar Focus (Days 121, 123, 126, 128, 131, 133, 136, 138, 141, 143, 146, 148)
- Exclamations and emphasis (Off with their heads!)
- Questions and question marks
- Dialogue punctuation (lots of talking in this book!)
- Adjectives for description (mad, curious, furious)
- Adverbs (curiouser and curiouser - discussing proper/improper forms)
- Playful grammar (breaking rules on purpose)

### Language Skills (Days 122, 124, 127, 129, 132, 134, 137, 139, 142, 144, 147, 149)
- Portmanteau words (slithy = slimy + lithe)
- Multiple meaning words (trunk, suit, club, diamond)
- Idioms ("mad as a hatter," "grinning like a Cheshire cat")
- Context clues for made-up words
- Antonyms (grow/shrink, polite/rude)
- Word origins and etymology

## 📋 Comprehension Questions

**3 questions per lesson:**
- **Literal:** What happened in this chapter?
- **Inferential:** Why is everyone in Wonderland so strange?
- **Analysis:** How has Alice changed since the beginning?
- **Wordplay:** What does this made-up word mean?
- **Opinion:** Would you want to visit Wonderland? Why or why not?
- **Theme:** What is Carroll teaching us about rules and logic?

## 🏆 Assessment Structure

**Every 5th Day (6 assessments):**
1. Vocabulary Quiz (8 words + wordplay)
2. Reading Comprehension
3. Grammar Review
4. Language/Wordplay Review
5. Writing: Vocabulary journal entry OR opinion paragraph

**Day 150 Capstone Assessment:**
- Full opinion essay
- "What is the most important lesson from our year of reading?"
- Can reference ANY book from the year
- Introduction, 3 reasons with evidence, conclusion
