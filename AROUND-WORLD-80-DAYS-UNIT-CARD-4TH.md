## 📖 Story Overview

Phileas Fogg, a wealthy and precise English gentleman, makes a bet that he can travel around the entire world in just 80 days. Accompanied by his loyal servant Passepartout, Fogg races across continents by train, ship, elephant, and more. Detective Fix wrongly suspects Fogg of robbery and follows them, creating obstacles. The journey is filled with dangers, delays, and amazing adventures as they visit India, Japan, America, and beyond!

**Key Characters:**
- **Phileas Fogg** - Precise, calm gentleman
- **Passepartout** - Loyal French servant
- **Detective Fix** - Persistent detective (wrong suspect!)
- **Aouda** - Indian princess rescued by Fogg
- **Reform Club members** - Gentlemen who make the bet

## ✍️ Writing Focus: TRAVEL JOURNAL PROJECT

**This is a sustained multi-entry writing project!**

**Days 91-100:** Launch travel journal
- Students keep a journal AS IF they're traveling with Fogg
- Daily entries (3-5 sentences) about each location
- Blend narrative + informational (What did we see? What did we learn?)

**Days 101-110:** Add research paragraphs
- Mini-research: Pick one country and write a paragraph
- Use informational texts + outside sources
- Practice taking notes and citing sources

**Days 111-120:** Create travel guide
- Final assessment: Create a "Travel Guide to the World"
- Include: 3 countries, facts about each, why to visit
- Integrate information from multiple sources (RI.4.9)
- Add maps, illustrations, descriptions

## 🎮 Grammar & Language Skills

### Grammar Focus (Days 91, 93, 96, 98, 101, 103, 106, 108, 111, 113, 116, 118)
- Proper nouns (countries, cities, people)
- Capitalization rules for place names
- Commas in dates and places
- Present tense for facts, past tense for narrative
- Transition words (first, next, then, finally)
- Technical writing (clear, precise language)

### Language Skills (Days 92, 94, 97, 99, 102, 104, 107, 109, 112, 114, 117, 119)
- Geography vocabulary
- Cultural words from different languages
- Multiple meaning words (train, port, bank)
- Analogies (ship is to ocean as train is to track)
- Context clues for unfamiliar place names
- Academic vocabulary for research writing

## 📋 Comprehension Questions

**3 questions per lesson:**
- **Geography:** Where are they now? How did they get there?
- **Problem-solving:** What obstacle do they face? How do they overcome it?
- **Character:** How does Fogg stay calm under pressure?
- **Cultural:** What do we learn about this country/culture?
- **Informational:** What facts can we learn from this chapter?
