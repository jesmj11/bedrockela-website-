## 📖 Story Overview

**Part 1: The Call of the Wild (Weeks 13-15, Days 61-75)**  
Buck, a powerful dog living comfortably in California, is stolen and sold as a sled dog in the Yukon during the Gold Rush. As he faces harsh conditions and cruel masters, Buck's wild instincts awaken. He must learn to survive in the wilderness and ultimately choose between civilization and the call of his ancestors.

**Part 2: The Jungle Book (Weeks 16-18, Days 76-90)**  
Mowgli, a human child, is raised by wolves in the Indian jungle. With the help of Baloo the bear, Bagheera the panther, and his wolf family, Mowgli learns the Law of the Jungle. He must face the fierce tiger Shere Khan and discover where he truly belongs—in the jungle or with humans.

**Key Characters:**
- **Buck** - Noble dog who becomes wild
- **John Thornton** - Buck's kind master
- **Spitz** - Buck's rival sled dog
- **Mowgli** - Human boy raised by wolves
- **Baloo** - Wise bear teacher
- **Bagheera** - Black panther protector
- **Shere Khan** - Dangerous tiger
- **Akela** - Leader of the wolf pack

## ✍️ Writing Focus: DESCRIPTIVE & COMPARATIVE

**Days 61-70:** Descriptive nature writing
- Use figurative language to describe settings
- Practice sensory details (what Buck sees, hears, smells)
- Poetry writing (haiku about nature)

**Days 71-80:** Figurative language hunts
- Find similes and metaphors in the text
- Create their own comparisons
- Journal entries using figurative language

**Days 81-90:** FIRST MULTI-PARAGRAPH ESSAY
- Compare Buck and Mowgli
- Theme essay: What does it mean to belong?
- Structured: Intro, 2 body paragraphs, conclusion
- Use evidence from BOTH texts

## 🎮 Grammar & Language Skills

### Grammar Focus (Days 61, 63, 66, 68, 71, 73, 76, 78, 81, 83, 86, 88)
- Descriptive adjectives (powerful, fierce, gentle)
- Adverbs for action (swiftly, cautiously, fiercely)
- Combining sentences with conjunctions
- Varied sentence beginnings
- Verb tenses (present, past, past perfect)
- Writing for effect (short sentences for tension)

### Language Skills (Days 62, 64, 67, 69, 72, 74, 77, 79, 82, 84, 87, 89)
- Similes ("as fierce as a tiger")
- Metaphors ("the call of the wild")
- Personification (nature as a character)
- Sensory language
- Animal vocabulary and behaviors
- Compare/contrast vocabulary (similar, different, however, both)

## 📋 Comprehension Questions

**3 questions per lesson:**
- Character transformation (How has Buck changed?)
- Theme (What does "the call of the wild" mean?)
- Comparison (How are Buck and Mowgli similar?)
- Inference (Why does Buck feel torn?)
- Evidence (Find proof that Mowgli belongs in the jungle)
