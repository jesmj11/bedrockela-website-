## PUBLIC DOMAIN SOURCES (Free to Use)

### Primary Sources:
1. **Project Gutenberg** (gutenberg.org)
   - Historical texts, essays, biographical works
   - 100% public domain, no restrictions

2. **Internet Archive** (archive.org)
   - Historical magazines, encyclopedias, textbooks
   - Search by year (pre-1928 = public domain)

3. **Wikisource** (en.wikisource.org)
   - Verified public domain texts
   - Well-formatted, easy to extract

4. **Google Books** (books.google.com)
   - Filter: "Free Google eBooks" + year range
   - Public domain books fully readable

5. **Library of Congress Digital Collections**
   - Primary source documents
   - Historical newspapers, pamphlets

### Secondary Sources (Educational Use):
6. **Smithsonian Open Access**
   - Museum articles, educational content
   - CC0 license (free to adapt)

7. **Khan Academy** (khanacademy.org)
   - Reading passages on history, science
   - Check Creative Commons licensing

8. **ReadWorks** (readworks.org)
   - Free educator account
   - Nonfiction passages by grade level

9. **CommonLit** (commonlit.org)
   - Free teacher resources
   - Paired texts with literature

10. **Newsela** (newsela.com)
    - Current events adapted to reading levels
    - Free teacher account

## EXTRACTION WORKFLOW

### For Each Article:
1. **Find source** using recommended databases above
2. **Extract 500-800 words** (8th grade appropriate)
3. **Adapt language** if needed (maintain readability)
4. **Add source citation** at bottom of article
5. **Format as markdown** with:
   - Title (h2)
   - Author/source (if known)
   - Body paragraphs
   - Key vocabulary in **bold**
   - Citation

### Example Format:
```markdown
## The Science of Observation

*Adapted from "The Principles of Psychology" by William James (1890)*

Observation is more than simply seeing. It is the **deliberate** act of...

[800 words of content]

---
**Source:** James, William. *The Principles of Psychology*. Henry Holt and Company, 1890. Public domain.
```
