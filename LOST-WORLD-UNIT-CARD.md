## 📚 Story Overview

A modern adaptation of Arthur Conan Doyle's classic adventure. Student journalist Maya Chen joins an expedition to a remote Amazon plateau where prehistoric creatures still exist. The team must survive dinosaurs, hostile terrain, and ethical dilemmas about whether to reveal their discovery to the world.

**Setting:** Present day, remote Amazon plateau  
**Protagonist:** Maya Chen (17, aspiring science journalist)  
**Supporting Cast:**
- Professor Challenger - eccentric zoologist
- Dr. Summerlee - skeptical paleontologist  
- Lord John Roxton - experienced explorer
- Maya's mentor Tom - environmental journalist

**Arc:** From skeptical observer → active participant → ethical decision-maker

## 💬 Discussion Questions (24 total - 2 per reading day)

### Day 1 (Chapters 1-2)
1. Why do you think Maya is initially skeptical of Professor Challenger's claims? What would it take to convince you?
2. How does the media's treatment of Challenger reflect how society often responds to unconventional scientific theories?

### Day 2 (Chapters 3-4)
1. What motivates each member of the expedition team? How might their different goals create conflict later?
2. How does Maya's background as a journalist both help and hinder her ability to experience the expedition?

### Day 3 (Chapters 5-6)
1. How does the discovery of the pteranodon change the team's understanding of what's possible?
2. What does the team's reaction to danger reveal about their characters?

### Day 4 (Chapters 7-8)
1. Why is establishing a base camp both practical and symbolic for the expedition?
2. How might the existence of this isolated ecosystem challenge current scientific understanding?

### Day 6 (Chapters 9-10)
1. How do the dinosaurs on the plateau compare to what we know from fossils? What might explain the differences?
2. What ethical questions arise when the team considers collecting specimens?

### Day 7 (Chapters 11-12)
1. How does encountering the ape-men complicate the team's mission?
2. What does the ape-men's society structure tell us about evolution and intelligence?

### Day 8 (Chapters 13-14)
1. How does being captured change Maya's perspective on the expedition?
2. What parallels can you draw between the ape-men's territorial behavior and human conflicts?

### Day 9 (Chapters 15-16)
1. What makes the team's escape both thrilling and morally complex?
2. How has Maya's role in the expedition evolved from observer to participant?

### Day 11 (Chapters 17-18)
1. How does the volcanic threat force the team to make difficult choices?
2. What does the team's response to danger reveal about their priorities?

### Day 12 (Chapters 19-20)
1. How do you think the team should balance documenting their discovery with ensuring their survival?
2. What would you choose to save if you could only bring back limited evidence?

### Day 13 (Chapters 21-22)
1. Should the team reveal the plateau's existence to the world? What are the arguments for and against?
2. How might public knowledge of the plateau affect the ecosystem and its inhabitants?

### Day 14 (Chapters 23-24)
1. How has Maya changed from the beginning of the expedition to the end?
2. What do you think is the most important lesson from the Lost World expedition?

## 🔄 Adaptation Notes

### For 7th Grade:
- Reduce vocabulary to 2 words/day (8 words/week)
- Simplify grammar topics (focus on sentence types, not verbals)
- Shorter informational texts (300-400 words)
- More guided comprehension questions

### For 9th Grade:
- Increase vocabulary to 4 words/day (16 words/week)
- Add literary analysis (symbolism, motifs, archetypes)
- Longer, more complex informational texts (800+ words)
- More open-ended, analytical comprehension questions
- Research component (find and cite additional sources)

### For 5th Grade (Adventure Unit):
- Reduce vocabulary to 2 words/day (8 words/week)
- Focus on basic grammar (sentence types, capitalization)
- Simpler language concepts (synonyms, antonyms, prefixes)
- More concrete comprehension questions (plot-focused)
- Shorter story chapters (split current chapters into smaller chunks)
