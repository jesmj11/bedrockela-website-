## 📖 CHAPTER BREAKDOWN (2 Chapters Per Day)

### Week 10: SURVIVAL AND INNOVATION (Days 46-50)

**Day 46: Chapters 1-2 - "The Balloon Escape"**
- Ch 1: The Balloon Escape - Five Union prisoners escape Confederate prison
- Ch 2: Crash Landing - Washed ashore on unknown island
- **Characters:** Cyrus Smith (engineer), Gideon Spilett (journalist), Nab (freed slave), Jack Harbert (young engineer), Pencroff (sailor)
- **Theme:** Desperate escape, teamwork from the start
- **Discussion:** What skills would you bring to a deserted island?

**Day 47: Chapters 3-4 - "First Priorities"**
- Ch 3: First Priorities - Shelter, fire, fresh water
- Ch 4: Making Fire - Using engineering to create fire from nothing
- **Engineering:** Glass lens from sand, friction methods
- **Theme:** Basic survival needs, scientific problem-solving
- **Discussion:** What's the first thing you'd do if stranded?

**Day 48: Chapters 5-6 - "Building Tools"**
- Ch 5: Creating Pottery - Making containers and storage
- Ch 6: The Forge Begins - Starting metallurgy operations
- **Engineering:** Clay firing, early metalworking
- **Theme:** Tool-making enables all other progress
- **Discussion:** Why are tools the foundation of civilization?

**Day 49: Chapters 7-8 - "Advanced Engineering"**
- Ch 7: The Engineering Challenge - Building a real forge with hydraulic power
- Ch 8: Secrets of the Island - Discovering mysterious help
- **Engineering:** Water-powered bellows, iron smelting, steel production
- **Theme:** Innovation through necessity, mysterious benefactor
- **Discussion:** How hot does fire need to be to melt iron?

**Day 50: ASSESSMENT #1 - Week 10 Review**
- Vocabulary Quiz: 40 words (Chapters 1-8)
- Comprehension: Survival and early engineering
- Writing: "Rank the five castaways by importance. Defend your choice."

## 🎭 CHARACTER ARCS

**Cyrus Smith (Engineer):**
- Brilliant engineer, natural leader
- Uses science to solve every problem
- Nemo's successor, learns from the master
- Represents applied knowledge

**Gideon Spilett (Journalist):**
- Documents everything, strategic thinker
- Connects technology to society
- Asks "why" not just "how"
- Represents communication and planning

**Nab (Freed Slave):**
- Wilderness survival expert
- Finds and prepares materials
- Bridges practical and theoretical knowledge
- Represents resilience and adaptation

**Jack Harbert (Young Engineer):**
- Student learning from Smith
- Theoretical knowledge meets practical application
- Grows from boy to engineer
- Represents future potential

**Pencroff (Sailor):**
- Maritime and mechanical expertise
- Practical problem-solver
- Comic relief but essential contributor
- Represents hands-on skill

- Legendary engineer in exile
- Dying genius passing knowledge
- Mysterious benefactor
- Represents peak human achievement
