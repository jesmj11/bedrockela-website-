## 📖 Story Overview

Mary Lennox, a sour and spoiled orphan, is sent to live with her uncle at Misselthwaite Manor on the Yorkshire moors. She discovers a hidden, locked garden that has been abandoned for ten years. With the help of Dickon, a kind local boy who understands animals, and Colin, her sickly cousin who has been hidden away, Mary brings the garden—and everyone in it—back to life. The garden's magic transforms them all.

**Key Characters:**
- **Mary Lennox** - Orphaned girl who starts sour, becomes kind
- **Colin Craven** - Sickly boy who learns to walk and laugh
- **Dickon Sowerby** - Kind boy with a gift for nature and animals
- **Martha** - Friendly maid who helps Mary
- **Ben Weatherstaff** - Grumpy gardener with a soft heart
- **Archibald Craven** - Mary's uncle, grieving and distant
- **Robin** - The bird who shows Mary the garden key

## ✍️ Writing Focus: RESEARCH PROJECT & SYNTHESIS

**This is the CAPSTONE unit!**

**Days 151-160:** Begin research project
- Students choose topic related to gardens, nature, OR transformation
- Learn: topic selection, finding sources, taking notes
- Create note cards or digital notes

**Days 161-170:** Draft and revise research project
- Organize notes into outline
- Write introduction, body paragraphs, conclusion
- Practice W.4.2 (informative/explanatory text)
- Cite sources (simple format for 4th grade)

**Days 171-175:** Complete and present research
- Final draft with illustrations or visual aids
- Brief oral presentation (2-3 minutes)
- Practice SL.4.4, SL.4.5

**Days 176-180:** YEAR-SPANNING THEME ESSAY
- Final writing piece: "What is the most important theme we explored this year?"
- Students choose theme that appears in 3+ books from the year
- Evidence from multiple texts
- Demonstrates growth from Day 1 to Day 180

## 📋 Comprehension Questions

**3 questions per lesson:**
- **Character transformation:** How has Mary/Colin changed?
- **Theme:** What does the garden represent?
- **Connection:** How is this similar to other books we've read?
- **Inference:** Why does the garden have such power?
- **Analysis:** What lesson can we take from this story?
- **Synthesis:** Compare Mary's journey to Dorothy's/Buck's/Alice's

## 🏆 Assessment Structure

**Every 5th Day (Days 155, 160, 165, 170, 175):**
- Regular assessments (vocab, comprehension, grammar, language, writing)

**Day 180 - FINAL COMPREHENSIVE ASSESSMENT:**
1. **Vocabulary Review** (12 words from Secret Garden + earlier books)
2. **Reading Comprehension** (Secret Garden + synthesis question)
3. **Grammar & Language Mastery Test**
4. **Year-Spanning Theme Essay:**
   - Choose theme: friendship, courage, belonging, transformation, etc.
   - Provide evidence from 3+ books read this year
   - Introduction, 3 body paragraphs, conclusion
   - Shows ALL writing skills learned
5. **Reflection:** "How have I grown as a reader and writer this year?"
6. **Portfolio Review:** Students review best work from the year
7. **Writing Portfolio Presentation** (optional celebration)
