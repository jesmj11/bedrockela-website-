## Themes to Explore

1. **Observation and Logic**
   - "You see but you do not observe"
   - Details matter
   - Logic reveals truth

2. **Science vs. Superstition**
   - Rational explanations for "impossible" events
   - Scientific method in detection
   - Evidence-based reasoning

3. **Intellect as Weapon**
   - Brain over brawn
   - Knowledge is power
   - Moriarty as intellectual equal

4. **Justice and Morality**
   - Legal vs. true justice
   - When to prosecute, when to show mercy
   - Gray areas in right and wrong

5. **Friendship and Loyalty**
   - Holmes and Watson partnership
   - Trust and companionship
   - Complementary strengths

## Discussion Questions

1. How does Holmes' scientific method differ from police investigation?
2. Why is Watson the narrator instead of Holmes?
3. What makes Moriarty a worthy opponent for Holmes?
4. When does Holmes choose mercy over prosecution? Why?
5. How realistic are Holmes' deductions?
6. What role does Victorian London play in these mysteries?
7. Why has Sherlock Holmes remained popular for over 130 years?
8. How does Holmes influence modern detective shows (CSI, House, etc.)?
9. Is Holmes a good person or just a brilliant detective?
10. What can we learn from Holmes' methods in everyday life?

## Literary Devices & Mystery Conventions

**Mystery Structure:**
- **Setup** - Crime introduced, stakes established
- **Investigation** - Clues gathered, suspects questioned
- **Red herrings** - False leads that mislead
- **Climax** - Truth revealed through deduction
- **Resolution** - Justice served, mystery explained

**Narrative Devices:**
- **Watson as narrator** - Reliable chronicler, represents reader
- **Fair play** - All clues available to reader
- **Locked room mysteries** - Seemingly impossible crimes
- **Deduction scenes** - Holmes explains reasoning

**Character Types:**
- **Brilliant detective** - Holmes as archetype
- **Loyal companion** - Watson supports and documents
- **Bumbling police** - Lestrade needs Holmes' help
- **Criminal mastermind** - Moriarty as intellectual equal

## Why Double-Length Unit?

**Sherlock Holmes spans 30 days instead of 15 because:**

1. **11 Complete Cases** - More stories than other units
2. **Skills Development** - Part 2 teaches detective/analytical skills
3. **Writing Workshop** - Students create own mysteries
4. **Genre Study** - Deep dive into mystery conventions
5. **Perfect Midpoint** - Bridges adventure units (1-4) and Gothic units (6-10)

**Flexible Implementation:**
- Can be compressed to 15 days (cases only)
- Can be expanded to full quarter (add more skills)
- Part 1 and Part 2 can be separated if needed
