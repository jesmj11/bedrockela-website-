## Unit Overview

**Stories:** *The Adventures of Sherlock Holmes* by Sir Arthur Conan Doyle (adapted)  
**Genre:** Detective Mystery / Logical Deduction  
**Theme:** Observation, Logic, Scientific Method, Justice, Intellect vs. Crime  
**Total Chapters:** 24  
**Days:** 15 (Mon-Fri for 3 weeks)  
**Assessment Day:** Day 15 (Friday, Week 15)

## Character Guide

**Sherlock Holmes** - World's greatest detective using observation and deduction  
**Dr. John Watson** - Holmes' friend, assistant, and chronicler  
**Professor Moriarty** - Criminal mastermind and Holmes' nemesis  
**Inspector Lestrade** - Scotland Yard detective who relies on Holmes  
**Irene Adler** - "The Woman" who outsmarted Holmes  
**Mrs. Hudson** - Landlady at 221B Baker Street

## Themes to Explore

1. **Observation and Logic**
   - "You see but you do not observe"
   - Details matter
   - Scientific method in detection

2. **Intellect vs. Crime**
   - Brain over brawn
   - Moriarty as intellectual equal
   - Knowledge is power

3. **Justice and Mercy**
   - Legal vs. true justice
   - When to prosecute, when to forgive
   - Gray areas in morality

4. **Friendship and Partnership**
   - Holmes and Watson complementary strengths
   - Trust and loyalty
   - Different perspectives unite

---

## Discussion Questions

1. How does Holmes' scientific method differ from police investigation?
2. Why is Watson the narrator instead of Holmes?
3. What makes Moriarty a worthy opponent for Holmes?
4. When does Holmes choose mercy over prosecution? Why?
5. How realistic are Holmes' deductions?
6. Why has Sherlock Holmes remained popular for over 130 years?
7. How does Watson complement Holmes' abilities?
8. What can we learn from Holmes' methods in everyday life?

---

## Writing Prompts

**Literary Analysis:**
- Analyze Watson's role as narrator and how it shapes the reader experience
- Compare Holmes' methods to modern forensic science
- Explore the Holmes-Moriarty dynamic as intellectual battle
- Discuss Victorian attitudes reflected in the mysteries

**Argument Essays:**
- Is Holmes a hero or an anti-hero?
- Does the scientific method work in all detective situations?
- Is Moriarty necessary to make Holmes great?
- Should detectives ever choose mercy over law?

**Creative Extensions:**
- Write your own Sherlock Holmes mystery
- Modernize a Holmes story (set in present day)
- Write from Moriarty's perspective
- Create a case Watson never published
