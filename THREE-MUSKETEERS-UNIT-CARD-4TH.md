## 📖 Story Overview (Age-Appropriate)

Young D'Artagnan leaves his home in Gascony to join the King's Musketeers in Paris. He quickly befriends three legendary musketeers—Athos, Porthos, and Aramis—and together they embark on exciting adventures. The four friends must outwit villains, protect the Queen's honor, and prove that "All for one, and one for all!" is more than just a motto.

**Key Characters:**
- **D'Artagnan** - Brave young hero from the countryside
- **Athos** - Wise and noble musketeer
- **Porthos** - Strong and flashy musketeer
- **Aramis** - Clever and educated musketeer
- **Cardinal Richelieu** - Powerful antagonist
- **Milady de Winter** - Mysterious villain
- **Queen Anne** - Kind queen who needs help
- **King Louis XIII** - The king of France

**Plot Points (Simplified for 4th Grade):**
1. D'Artagnan's journey to Paris
2. Meeting the three musketeers
3. Becoming friends through duels
4. The Queen's diamond studs mystery
5. Adventures across France and England
6. Outsmarting the Cardinal's spies
7. Protecting the Queen's honor
8. The triumph of friendship

## ✍️ Writing Prompts (24 regular lessons)

**Opinion Writing (Days 1, 3, 6, 8, 11, 13, 16, 18, 21, 23, 26, 28):**
1. Is D'Artagnan brave or reckless? Give two reasons.
2. Would you want to be a musketeer? Why or why not?
3. Who is the bravest of the four friends? Explain.
4. Should D'Artagnan have left home so young? Give your opinion.
5. Is the Cardinal a villain or just doing his job?
6. Which musketeer would you want as a best friend?
7. Was the Queen right to ask for help from the musketeers?
8. Is it ever okay to fight a duel? Explain your thinking.
9. Should Athos have told his friends about his past sooner?
10. Do you think Milady deserved forgiveness? Why or why not?
11. Which adventure was the most exciting? Defend your choice.
12. What does "All for one, one for all" really mean?

**Journal Responses (Days 2, 4, 7, 9, 12, 14, 17, 19, 22, 24, 27, 29):**
1. Describe a time you made a new friend. How is it similar to D'Artagnan's story?
2. What would you do if you met the King? Write about it.
3. If you could join the musketeers on one adventure, which would you choose?
4. Write a diary entry as D'Artagnan after his first week in Paris.
5. How do you think the Queen felt when she lost the diamonds?
6. Describe the most exciting part of today's chapter in your own words.
7. What surprised you about life in the 1600s?
8. If you could ask one character a question, who and what would you ask?
9. What lesson did you learn from today's reading?
10. Write about a time you had to be loyal to a friend.
11. How has D'Artagnan changed since the beginning of the story?
12. What do you think happens after the story ends?

## 🎮 Grammar & Language Skills (Rotating)

### Grammar Focus (Days 1, 3, 6, 8, 11, 13, 16, 18, 21, 23, 26, 28)
- **Week 1:** Compound sentences with coordinating conjunctions (and, but, or, so)
- **Week 2:** Complex sentences with subordinating conjunctions (because, when, if)
- **Week 3:** Action verbs vs. helping verbs
- **Week 4:** Past tense verbs (regular and irregular)
- **Week 5:** Quotation marks and dialogue
- **Week 6:** Combining sentences for variety

### Language Skills (Days 2, 4, 7, 9, 12, 14, 17, 19, 22, 24, 27, 29)
- **Week 1:** Context clues to figure out word meanings
- **Week 2:** Using French words in English (musketeer, guard, cardinal)
- **Week 3:** Similes in descriptions ("brave as a lion")
- **Week 4:** Historical vocabulary (duke, palace, banquet)
- **Week 5:** Multiple meaning words (cardinal, court, arms)
- **Week 6:** Idioms and sayings ("All for one, one for all")
