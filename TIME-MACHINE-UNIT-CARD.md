## 📚 UNIT OVERVIEW

**Theme:** Evolution, Class Division, Consequences of Progress  
**Central Question:** What happens when humanity achieves paradise?

**Why This Story:**
- Classic sci-fi exploring time travel and future evolution
- Shows how class divisions might evolve over millennia
- Eloi (leisure class) and Morlocks (working class) as dystopian warning
- Perfect for social commentary and critical thinking
- H.G. Wells's most famous work

## 📖 CHAPTER BREAKDOWN (2 Chapters Per Day)

### Week 1: THE MACHINE AND THE JOURNEY (Days 1-5)

**Day 1: Chapters 1-2 - "Time as Dimension"**
- Ch 1: Paradise Lost - Introduction to Victorian scientists discussing time
- Ch 2: The Watchers Below - Class division theories
- **Vocabulary:** paradise, innocence, helplessness, idyllic, descendants, civilization, industrial, machinery, underground, division
- **Theme:** Victorian social concerns, time as 4th dimension
- **Discussion:** What is the 4th dimension?

**Day 2: Chapters 3-4 - "The Victorian Question"**
- Ch 3: The Victorian Question - Theory of time travel explained
- Ch 4: The Machine Revealed - The inventor shows his time machine
- **Vocabulary:** theory, dimension, temporal, skepticism, demonstration, principles, mechanics, laboratory, construction, experiment
- **Theme:** Scientific discovery, skepticism vs. belief
- **Discussion:** Would you believe a time traveler?

**Day 3: Chapters 5-6 - "Into Tomorrow"**
- Ch 5: Into Tomorrow - First time travel journey begins
- Ch 6: The Garden World - Arrival in year 802,701 AD
- **Vocabulary:** inaugural, acceleration, sensation, voyage, millennia, utopian, lush, gorgeous, luxury, concealed
- **Theme:** Far future, apparent utopia
- **Discussion:** What would the future look like?

**Day 4: Chapters 7-8 - "The Eloi"**
- Ch 7: Beautiful Children - Meeting the Eloi, childlike future humans
- Ch 8: The Machine Vanishes - Time machine stolen!
- **Vocabulary:** mentality, degradation, purpose, terror, potential, horror, evidence, intelligence, operating, invisible
- **Theme:** Paradise with a price, mystery deepens
- **Discussion:** Why are the Eloi so childlike?

**Day 5: ASSESSMENT #1 - Week 1 Review**
- Vocabulary Quiz: 40 words (Chapters 1-8)
- Comprehension: Time travel and Eloi introduction
- Writing: "Is the year 802,701 a utopia? Why or why not?"

### Week 2: UNDERGROUND DISCOVERIES (Days 6-10)

**Day 6: Chapters 9-10 - "The Morlocks"**

## 🎭 CHARACTER DEVELOPMENT

**The Time Traveler:**
- Curious scientist and inventor
- Victorian gentleman with modern ideas
- Becomes traumatized by future knowledge
- Isolated by what he's seen
- Final departure = escape from unbearable present

**Weena (Eloi):**
- Childlike but capable of gratitude
- Shows humanity hasn't completely lost emotion
- Her death = loss of connection
- Represents innocence and fragility

**The Eloi:**
- Beautiful, weak, purposeless
- Result of leisure class evolution
- Lost all struggle, lost all strength
- Warning about comfort without challenge

**The Morlocks:**
- Underground workers evolved into predators
- Nocturnal, light-sensitive, mechanical
- Not evil, just evolved for different niche
- Warning about workers becoming inhuman
