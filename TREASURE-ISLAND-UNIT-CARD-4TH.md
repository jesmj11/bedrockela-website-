## 📖 Story Overview

Young Jim Hawkins discovers a treasure map in the sea chest of a mysterious old pirate who dies at his family's inn. Jim, Dr. Livesey, and Squire Trelawney set sail on the Hispaniola to find the buried treasure. But their crew includes the cunning Long John Silver and his band of pirates! Jim must use his wits and courage to survive mutiny, treasure hunts, and dangerous enemies.

**Key Characters:**
- **Jim Hawkins** - Brave young hero
- **Long John Silver** - Charming but dangerous pirate
- **Captain Smollett** - Honest ship captain
- **Dr. Livesey** - Wise doctor and friend
- **Squire Trelawney** - Wealthy gentleman
- **Ben Gunn** - Marooned sailor
- **Billy Bones** - Old pirate with the map
- **Blind Pew** - Terrifying pirate

## ✍️ Writing Focus: POV & SCENE WRITING

**Building on Unit 1's journals:**

**Days 31-40:** Continue journals + introduce POV
- Rewrite scenes from Long John Silver's perspective
- Rewrite scenes from Dr. Livesey's perspective
- Compare how different characters see the same event

**Days 41-50:** Comparison writing
- Compare Jim at beginning vs. end
- Compare honest characters vs. pirates
- First informational paragraph: "Life of a Pirate"

**Days 51-60:** Second complete narrative
- Students write their own adventure story
- Must include: hero, villain, treasure, twist ending
- Apply lessons from Treasure Island

## 🎮 Grammar & Language Skills

### Grammar Focus (Days 31, 33, 36, 38, 41, 43, 46, 48, 51, 53, 56, 58)
- Complex sentences with subordinating conjunctions
- Dialogue punctuation (quotation marks)
- Commas in compound sentences
- Past tense verbs (regular and irregular)
- Possessive nouns (Jim's map, pirates' ship)
- Sentence variety for effect

### Language Skills (Days 32, 34, 37, 39, 42, 44, 47, 49, 52, 54, 57, 59)
- Nautical vocabulary (port, starboard, bow, stern)
- Multiple meaning words (sail, ship, quarter)
- Idioms ("walk the plank," "dead men tell no tales")
- Context clues for pirate slang
- Figurative language (metaphors about the sea)
- Word origins from sailing

## 📋 Comprehension Questions

**3 questions per lesson (mix of):**
- Character motivation (Why does Silver pretend to be friendly?)
- Plot prediction (What will Jim do next?)
- Theme analysis (Is Long John Silver good or evil?)
- Comparison (How is Jim different from the pirates?)
- Evidence-based (Find proof that Jim is brave)
