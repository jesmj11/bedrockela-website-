## 📚 Story Overview

A modern adaptation of H.G. Wells' classic science fiction masterpiece. Science journalist James Morrison witnesses the Martian invasion of Earth firsthand, documenting humanity's desperate struggle against technologically superior alien invaders. Themes of survival, human ingenuity, and the fragility of civilization.

**Setting:** Present day London and surrounding areas  
**Protagonist:** James Morrison (23, science correspondent for The London Telegraph)  
**Supporting Cast:**
- Dr. Phillips - Military doctor and survivor
- Sarah Chen - Engineering student
- The Martians - Desperate refugees from a dying planet

**Arc:** From naive observer → desperate survivor → witness to humanity's resilience

## 💬 Discussion Questions (24 total - 2 per reading day)

### Day 16 (Chapters 1-2)
1. How does Morrison's role as a journalist affect how he experiences and reports the invasion?
2. What does the heat-ray's instant destruction reveal about the Martians' capabilities and intentions?

### Day 17 (Chapters 3-4)
1. Why do you think human military weapons are ineffective against the Martian machines?
2. How does the story explore the theme of technological superiority?

### Day 18 (Chapters 5-6)
1. What does the mass exodus from London reveal about human nature under extreme pressure?
2. How do different characters respond to the crisis? What does this tell us about leadership?

### Day 19 (Chapters 7-8)
1. Why do you think the Martians use tripod machines instead of other forms of warfare?
2. What parallels can you draw between this fictional invasion and real historical conflicts?

### Day 21 (Chapters 9-10)
1. How does going underground change Morrison's perspective on the invasion?
2. What survival strategies do the characters employ? Which are most effective?

### Day 22 (Chapters 11-12)
1. What does the red weed symbolize in the story?
2. How is the Earth being transformed by the Martian presence?

### Day 23 (Chapters 13-14)
1. What is the emotional impact of seeing London as a "dead city"?
2. How does isolation affect Morrison's mental state?

### Day 24 (Chapters 15-16)
1. What happens when the infrastructure of civilization collapses?
2. How do humans adapt to a world without their familiar systems?

### Day 26 (Chapters 17-18)
1. What are the first signs that the Martians are not invincible?
2. How does hope begin to return to the survivors?

### Day 27 (Chapters 19-20)
1. Why is it ironic that bacteria—not human weapons—defeats the Martians?
2. What does this ending say about adaptation and evolution?

### Day 28 (Chapters 21-22)
1. How do survivors begin to rebuild after such devastating loss?
2. What does reconstruction require beyond just physical rebuilding?

### Day 29 (Chapters 23-24)
1. How has Morrison changed from the beginning of the story to the end?
2. What lessons does humanity learn from the Martian invasion?
