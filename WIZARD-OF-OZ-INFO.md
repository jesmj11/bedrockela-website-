# The Wonderful Wizard of Oz - Complete Adaptation

## Overview
**Complete 4th-5th grade adaptation** of L. Frank Baum's classic novel
**24 chapters** ready for curriculum integration
**Source file:** `wizard-of-oz-complete.txt` (98KB)

## Chapter Breakdown

### Part 1: The Journey Begins (Chapters 1-6)
1. **The Cyclone** - Dorothy arrives in Oz
2. **The Council with the Munchkins** - Meets Witch of North, gets silver shoes
3. **How Dorothy Saved the Scarecrow** - First companion joins
4. **The Road Through the Forest** - Journey continues
5. **The Rescue of the Tin Woodman** - Second companion joins
6. **The Cowardly Lion** - Third companion joins

### Part 2: The Quest (Chapters 7-12)
7. **The Journey to the Great Oz** - Crossing the ditch
8. **The Deadly Poppy Field** - Dorothy falls asleep
9. **The Queen of the Field Mice** - Saving the Lion
10. **The Guardian of the Gates** - Entering Emerald City
11. **The Great and Terrible Oz** - Meeting the "Wizard"
12. **The Search for the Wicked Witch** - Wolves, crows, bees attacks

### Part 3: The Witch (Chapters 13-15)
13. **Dorothy the Prisoner** - Life in the castle
14. **The Melting of the Witch** - Water defeats the Witch
15. **The Winged Monkeys** - Flying back to Emerald City

### Part 4: The Truth (Chapters 16-18)
16. **The Discovery of Oz the Terrible** - Toto reveals the humbug
17. **The Magic of Oz** - Giving the "gifts"
18. **The Balloon** - Failed escape attempt

### Part 5: The Journey Home (Chapters 19-24)
19. **The Journey South** - Heading to Glinda
20. **The Dainty China Country** - Fragile people
21. **The Country of the Quadlings** - Arriving at Glinda's castle
22. **Glinda Grants Dorothy's Wish** - Friends find their kingdoms
23. **There's No Place Like Home** - Silver shoes revelation
24. **Home Again** - Return to Kansas

## Curriculum Integration Options

### Option 1: 12-Day Unit (2 chapters per day)
- **Week 1** (Days 1-5): Chapters 1-10
- **Week 2** (Days 6-10): Chapters 11-20  
- **Week 3** (Days 11-12): Chapters 21-24 + Assessment

### Option 2: 18-Day Unit (varied pacing)
- Slow start (1 chapter/day for setup)
- Faster middle (2-3 chapters/day for action)
- Slow finish (1 chapter/day for resolution)

### Option 3: 24-Day Unit (1 chapter per day)
- Full immersion experience
- Time for deep analysis of each chapter
- Best for 4th grade (younger readers)

## Content Features

**Reading Level:** 4th-5th grade
**Themes:** Home, friendship, courage, wisdom, kindness, self-belief
**Character Development:** Clear arc for all 4 companions
**Lesson Potential:**
- Vocabulary (prairie, cyclone, humbug, etc.)
- Comprehension (plot, character motivation, theme)
- Writing prompts (courage essay, alternate ending, character analysis)
- Grammar focus (dialogue, descriptive language)
