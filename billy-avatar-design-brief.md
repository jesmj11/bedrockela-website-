# Billy the Goat - Avatar Design Brief

## Project Overview

**Character Name:** Billy the Goat  
**Purpose:** AI writing buddy mascot for BedrockELA curriculum (grades 1-8)  
**Usage:** Web app UI, educational materials, marketing  
**Target Audience:** Children ages 6-14, parents, homeschool educators  

---

## Character Personality

Billy is:
- **Friendly & approachable** - Not intimidating, inviting
- **Encouraging & supportive** - Makes kids feel safe to try
- **Playful but helpful** - Fun without being silly/distracting
- **Trustworthy** - Kids should feel comfortable asking him questions
- **Growth-minded** - Celebrates effort, not perfection

**NOT:**
- Scary or aggressive
- Overly cutesy/babyish
- Corporate/sterile
- Too detailed/complex (needs to work at small sizes)

---

## Visual Style

### Overall Aesthetic
- **Style:** Friendly cartoon, clean lines, modern but timeless
- **Vibe:** Picture book illustration meets educational app
- **Inspiration:** Think Duolingo owl (friendly, iconic) but for a goat

### Reference Styles (Examples)
- Duolingo's mascots (friendly, simple, expressive)
- PBS Kids characters (educational, approachable)
- Headspace animations (calm, warm, inviting)

**NOT like:**
- Realistic goat (too literal)
- Anime/manga style (not the right vibe)
- 3D rendered (too expensive, harder to animate)
- Overly detailed fur texture (needs to scale down)

---

## Character Description

### Physical Features

**Body:**
- Small, compact goat (not too tall/lanky)
- Friendly, round shapes (avoid sharp/pointy)
- Standing upright on two legs (anthropomorphic)
- Could hold objects (pencil, book) with front hooves/hands

**Face:**
- Large, expressive eyes (kind, approachable)
- Small smile (default friendly expression)
- Rounded snout (cute, not horse-like)
- Small curved horns (friendly, not aggressive)

**Color Palette:**
- **Primary:** Soft sage green (#8BA888) or warm cream/tan
- **Accents:** Warm gold (#F4A460), soft orange
- **Eyes:** Dark brown or warm amber
- **Details:** Light touches of white (belly, hooves)

**Size/Proportions:**
- Slightly larger head (cuter, more approachable)
- Short legs (stable, grounded)
- Rounded body (huggable, non-threatening)

**Optional Accessories:**
- Small pencil behind ear?
- Tiny backpack or satchel?
- Glasses? (optional - could make him look smarter)

**Keep it simple** - Character needs to work at 40√ó40px

## Required Expressions

Billy needs multiple expressions for different UI states:

### 1. **Default / Friendly** (Most common)
- Gentle smile
- Eyes open, bright
- Relaxed posture
- **Usage:** Idle state, general greeting
- **Feeling:** "I'm here to help!"

### 2. **Encouraging / Celebrating** üéâ
- Bigger smile, maybe open mouth (excited)
- Eyes sparkle or have happy crinkles
- Arms raised or one hoof up (high-five gesture)
- Maybe confetti or sparkles around him
- **Usage:** Student makes progress, completes writing
- **Feeling:** "You did it! I'm proud!"

### 3. **Thinking / Pondering** ü§î
- One hoof on chin
- Eyes looking up/to side
- Slight tilt of head
- Thoughtful expression (not confused)
- **Usage:** Billy is processing, considering question
- **Feeling:** "Hmm, let's think about this..."

### 4. **Idea / Light Bulb Moment** üí°
- Eyes wide, eyebrows raised
- Mouth in "O" shape (eureka!)
- Light bulb above head (optional)
- Excited but not frantic
- **Usage:** Billy has a helpful suggestion
- **Feeling:** "Ooh, I have an idea!"

### 5. **Listening / Attentive** üëÇ
- Slight lean forward
- Eyes focused
- Gentle, patient expression
- One ear perked up
- **Usage:** Student is typing, Billy is waiting
- **Feeling:** "I'm listening, take your time"

### 6. **Supportive / Comforting** üíö
- Warm smile
- Soft eyes (caring)
- Maybe one hoof on heart
- Gentle, reassuring posture
- **Usage:** Student is struggling or frustrated
- **Feeling:** "It's okay, you've got this"

## Technical Specifications

### File Formats Needed
- **Vector:** SVG (primary) - for web, scalable
- **Raster:** PNG with transparency (multiple sizes)
  - 512√ó512px (high res for marketing)
  - 256√ó256px (standard web)
  - 128√ó128px (UI elements)
  - 64√ó64px (small icons)
  - 40√ó40px (minimum size - must be recognizable)

### Color Mode
- RGB (for screens)
- Provide color codes in HEX and RGB
- Ensure colors are WCAG AA compliant for accessibility

### Layering
- Provide layered source files (Figma, Illustrator, etc.)
- Separate layers for:
  - Body
  - Face elements (eyes, mouth, horns)
  - Accessories (if any)
  - Expressions (so they can be swapped)

### Variations Needed
1. Each of the 6 expressions listed above
2. Possible: Left-facing and right-facing versions
3. Possible: Different poses (standing, sitting, waving)

## Style Guidelines

### Do's ‚úÖ
- **Friendly shapes:** Rounded edges, soft curves
- **Expressive eyes:** Eyes convey emotion clearly
- **Simple details:** Must work at small sizes (40√ó40px)
- **Consistent style:** All expressions should feel cohesive
- **Warm colors:** Inviting, educational, safe
- **Clear silhouette:** Recognizable even as a shadow

### Don'ts ‚ùå
- No aggressive features (sharp teeth, angry eyes)
- No overly complex textures (fur, shading)
- No realistic anatomy (keep it cartoony)
- No trendy styles that will date quickly
- No elements that don't scale down well
- No colors that are too bright/harsh

## Context & Usage

### Where Billy Appears

**Primary Usage:**
- Web app chat interface (side panel)
- Next to student's writing area
- Loading screens ("Billy is thinking...")
- Success messages ("Great job!")

**Secondary Usage:**
- Marketing website (hero image, about page)
- Social media graphics
- Email newsletters
- Printed materials (worksheets, teacher guides)

### Size Considerations
- **Large (256√ó512px):** Marketing, hero images - full detail visible
- **Medium (64√ó128px):** Chat UI, profile icons - clear features
- **Small (40√ó60px):** Status indicators, inline mentions - must be recognizable

**Test:** At 40√ó40px, can you still tell it's a goat? Can you tell which emotion?

## Budget & Timeline

### Estimated Cost
- **Freelancer (Fiverr, Upwork):** $200-500
- **Professional illustrator:** $500-1,500
- **Design agency:** $1,500-5,000+

**Recommendation:** Start with mid-tier freelancer ($300-600) for MVP, can always upgrade later.

### Timeline
- Initial concepts: 3-5 days
- Revisions: 1-2 rounds (5-7 days)
- Final deliverables: 2-3 days
- **Total:** 2-3 weeks from start to finish

## Evaluation Criteria

When reviewing designer submissions, check:

### ‚úÖ Style Fit
- Does it match the friendly, educational vibe?
- Would kids feel comfortable asking this character for help?
- Does it fit BedrockELA's brand aesthetic?

### ‚úÖ Technical Quality
- Does it scale well to 40√ó40px?
- Are expressions clearly different from each other?
- Is the silhouette recognizable?
- Are colors accessible (contrast)?

### ‚úÖ Flexibility
- Can it be animated later if needed?
- Can expressions be easily swapped?
- Does it work in different contexts (web, print, social)?

### ‚úÖ Uniqueness
- Is it distinctive enough to be memorable?
- Does it avoid looking too generic?
- Could it become an iconic mascot?
