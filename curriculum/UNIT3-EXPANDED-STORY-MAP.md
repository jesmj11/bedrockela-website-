### **Lessons 106-111: Vocabulary & Context Clues**

**Skills Focus:** Context clues, prefixes (un-, re-), suffixes (-ful, -less, -ly), word families

#### Story #12: **Rumpelstiltskin** (Lessons 106-108)
- **Length:** Medium (12-14 pages)
- **Why perfect:** Unusual name = perfect for word work! Context clues (what does "spin straw into gold" mean?), prefix practice (un-happy, re-turn)
- **Comprehension focus:** Using context to figure out word meanings
- **Vocabulary:** strange, promise, spin, straw, gold, bargain, guess
- **Reading level:** Independent

#### Story #13: **The Velveteen Rabbit** (Simple Version) (Lessons 109-111)
- **Length:** Medium-Long (14-16 pages)
- **Why perfect:** Beautiful, emotional story! TONS of suffix practice (beautiful, wonderful, careful, carefully, hopeless, happiness)
- **Comprehension focus:** Context clues, word families (real/really/unreal, love/lovely/loveless)
- **Theme:** What makes something real? Love!
- **Reading level:** Independent to advanced

### **Lessons 112-116: Advanced Vocabulary**

**Skills Focus:** Homophones, synonyms, antonyms, shades of meaning, categories, action words, describing words

#### Story #14: **The Tale of Peter Rabbit** (Lessons 112-114)
- **Length:** Medium (10-12 pages)
- **Why perfect:** Classic! Action words (hopped, squeezed, ran, hid), describing words (naughty, frightened, tired)
- **Comprehension focus:** Action vocabulary, descriptive language, shades of meaning (scared vs. frightened vs. terrified)
- **Reading level:** Independent

#### Story #15: **Tikki Tikki Tembo** (Lessons 115-116)
- **Length:** Medium (10-12 pages)
- **Why perfect:** Chinese tale! Long name = memorable! Vocabulary (well, bridge, rescue, remember), categories (family words, place words)
- **Comprehension focus:** Vocabulary categories, why short names are useful!
- **Cultural origin:** Chinese
- **Reading level:** Independent

- **Reading level:** Independent

- **Comprehension focus:** Time sequence, emotions, descriptive vocabulary
- **Cultural significance:** First picture book with African-American protagonist!
- **Theme:** Simple joys, childhood wonder
- **Reading level:** Independent

- **Comprehension focus:** Character development, theme (be yourself), comparing characters, text-to-text connections (Ugly Duckling!)
