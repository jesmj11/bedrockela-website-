## üåü Day 1 - Monday (Lesson 1)

### Theme: "Meet Billy and Learn M, S, P, A!"

---

### üìñ READING SESSION (15 minutes)

**Minutes 1-2: Welcome & Introduction**
- "Today we start our reading adventure with Billy the Goat! üêê"
- Show Billy illustration: "Billy loves to climb mountains and read stories!"
- "This week we'll read about an ENORMOUS turnip!"

**Minutes 3-6: Phonics Lesson (4 mins)**
- **Introduce letter M:**
  - Show uppercase M and lowercase m
  - "M says /m/ like in 'mountain' and 'mom'"
  - Practice sound together: "mmmm"
  - Trace M in the air

- **Introduce letter S:**
  - Show uppercase S and lowercase s
  - "S says /s/ like in 'snake' and 'sun'"
  - Practice: "ssss"
  - Trace S in the air

- **Introduce letter P:**
  - "P says /p/ like in 'pop' and 'pig'"
  - Practice: "p-p-p"

- **Introduce letter A:**
  - "A says /a/ like in 'apple' and 'ant'"
  - Practice: "aaa"

**Minutes 7-13: Story Reading (7 mins)**
- Open Enormous Turnip to pages 1-3
- **Teacher reads aloud** (students follow along with finger)
- Point to words as you read
- Emphasize the sounds: "Listen for our letters! M-A-N, S-A-T"
- **Interactive:** 
  - "What letter do you hear at the start of 'man'?" (M!)
  - "What about 'sat'?" (S!)

**Minutes 14-15: Quick Check (2 mins)**
- "What sound does M make?" (/m/)
- "What sound does S make?" (/s/)
- "Can you show me the letter A?"
- **Praise!** "You learned 4 letters today! Great job!"

---

### ‚úçÔ∏è WRITING SESSION (15 minutes)

**Minutes 1-5: Mini-Lesson - Letter Formation (5 mins)**

**Teach Uppercase M:**
- Draw on board: "Start at the top. Down, up, down-up, down!"
- Students trace in air while saying directions
- Practice on whiteboard/paper 3 times

**Teach Uppercase S:**
- "Start at the top. Curve left, curve right, like a snake!"
- Trace in air together
- Practice 3 times

**Teach Uppercase P:**
- "Down, up and around!"
- Trace, practice

**Teach Uppercase A:**
- "Diagonal down, diagonal down, across!"
- Trace, practice

**Minutes 6-12: Guided Practice (7 mins)**
- Hand out letter formation worksheet
- Students trace each letter 3 times
- **Teacher circulates, gives feedback:**
  - Check pencil grip
  - Guide hand placement
  - Praise effort!

**Minutes 13-15: Journal Time (3 mins)**
- Open student journals
- **Prompt:** "Draw the enormous turnip! Then trace the word 'man' at the bottom."
- Provide word card: MAN (dotted letters for tracing)
- Students draw and trace
- Share drawings: "Look at all these turnips!"

---

### üè† HOMEWORK (Optional):
- Practice writing M, S, P, A at home
- Tell family about the story!

## üåü Day 2 - Tuesday (Lesson 2)

### Theme: "New Letters N, T, B, J + Enormous Turnip Continues!"

---

### üìñ READING SESSION (15 minutes)

**Minutes 1-2: Review Yesterday**
- "What letters did we learn yesterday?" (M, S, P, A)
- Quick sound practice: Hold up each letter, students say sound
- "Who remembers what happened in the story?" (Man planted a turnip!)

**Minutes 3-6: Phonics Lesson - New Letters! (4 mins)**

**Introduce N:**
- "N says /n/ like in 'nose' and 'net'"
- Practice together
- Trace in air

**Introduce T:**
- "T says /t/ like in 'top' and 'turn'"
- Practice the sound
- Trace in air

**Introduce B:**
- "B says /b/ like in 'ball' and 'Billy'!"
- "Billy the goat starts with B!"
- Practice, trace

**Introduce J:**
- "J says /j/ like in 'jump' and 'jam'"
- Practice, trace

**Quick Blending Practice:**
- "Let's blend some sounds!"
- Write on board: M-A-N ‚Üí "mmmaaannn = MAN!"
- Write: S-A-T ‚Üí "sssaaattt = SAT!"
- Students blend with you

**Minutes 7-13: Story Reading (7 mins)**
- "Let's see what happens next in our story!"
- Read pages 4-6 together
- **Shared reading:** Teacher reads, students echo repeated phrases
- Point out CVC words: "Look! T-A-N! Let's sound it out together!"
- **Interactive questions:**
  - "Who came to help pull the turnip?"
  - "What letter does 'big' start with?" (B!)

**Minutes 14-15: Quick Check (2 mins)**
- Flash cards: Show N, T, B, J - students say sounds
- "Thumbs up if you can hear /n/ in 'turnip'!" üëç
- Celebrate: "You now know 8 letters!"

---

### ‚úçÔ∏è WRITING SESSION (15 minutes)

**Minutes 1-5: Mini-Lesson - Lowercase Letters (5 mins)**

**Teach lowercase letters:**
- Show how lowercase is different from uppercase
- "Little m looks like this..." (demonstrate)
- Practice: m, s, p, a, n, t, b, j
- **Focus on:** 
  - Starting at the right spot
  - Going the right direction
  - Size (lowercase is smaller!)

**Minutes 6-12: Guided Practice (7 mins)**
- Worksheet: Uppercase and lowercase matching
- Students write: Man, Sat, Tan, Bat, Pan
- **Teacher helps:** 
  - Check formation
  - Praise correct letter shapes
  - Gentle corrections

**Minutes 13-15: Journal Time (3 mins)**
- **Prompt:** "Draw the old man pulling the turnip. Write the word 'man' by yourself (no tracing today!)"
- Some may copy from board - that's okay!
- Celebrate attempts: "You're writing real words!"

---

### üè† HOMEWORK:
- Practice writing your name
- Find 3 things at home that start with B, T, or N

## üåü Day 3 - Wednesday (Lesson 3)

### Theme: "Blending Words + Turnip Won't Come Up!"

---

### üìñ READING SESSION (15 minutes)

**Minutes 1-2: Warm-Up Review**
- "Show me the letter that says /m/!" (students write in air)
- Quick drill: Teacher says sound, students name letter
- "Who can blend M-A-T?" (students try!)

**Minutes 3-6: Phonics Lesson - Blending! (4 mins)**
- **Teach blending procedure:**
  - "We put sounds together to make words!"
  
- **Model on board:**
  - Write: C-A-T
  - Point to each letter: "/c/ /a/ /t/"
  - Slide finger under: "ccaaattt = CAT!"
  
- **Practice together:**
  - M-A-N (man)
  - S-A-T (sat)
  - P-A-T (pat)
  - B-A-T (bat)
  - M-A-T (mat)

- **Students try independently:**
  - Show word card: TAN
  - Students sound out: "/t/ /a/ /n/ = tan!"

**Minutes 7-13: Story Reading (7 mins)**
- Read pages 7-9 of Enormous Turnip
- **Student participation:** 
  - Let students read the simple words they can decode
  - Teacher reads harder parts
  - Act out pulling the turnip! (stand up, pretend to pull)
- **Questions:**
  - "Why won't the turnip come up?"
  - "Who came to help this time?"

**Minutes 14-15: Quick Check (2 mins)**
- Write on board: SAT
- "Can you read this?" (Students read together)
- Write: PAN
- Students read!
- **Celebrate:** "You're READING! That's amazing!"

---

### ‚úçÔ∏è WRITING SESSION (15 minutes)

**Minutes 1-5: Mini-Lesson - Writing CVC Words (5 mins)**

**Teach how to write words:**
- "Today we write words all by ourselves!"
- Model on board: 
  - Say "mat"
  - "What sounds do you hear?" (/m/ /a/ /t/)
  - "What letters make those sounds?" (M-A-T)
  - Write M-A-T
  
- Practice dictation:
  - Teacher says: "sat"
  - Students repeat: "sat"
  - Students stretch sounds: "sssaaattt"
  - Students write: SAT

**Minutes 6-12: Guided Practice (7 mins)**
- **Dictation practice (teacher says, students write):**
  1. man
  2. pan
  3. bat
  4. mat
  5. sat

- **Teacher support:**
  - Walk around, watch students
  - Help sound out: "What sound do you hear first?"
  - Praise correct attempts
  - Fix reversals gently

**Minutes 13-15: Journal Time (3 mins)**
- **Prompt:** "Draw someone pulling the turnip. Write: 'The man sat.'"
- Provide word bank on board: man, sat, The
- Students copy into journal with illustration
- **Celebrate:** "You wrote a real sentence!"

---

### üè† HOMEWORK:
- Practice reading the words: man, bat, sat, pan, mat
- Find something "enormous" at home!

## üåü Day 4 - Thursday (Lesson 4)

### Theme: "Review All Letters + Turnip Comes Up!"

---

### üìñ READING SESSION (15 minutes)

**Minutes 1-2: Review Game**
- "Letter Sound Race!" 
- Teacher holds up letter cards randomly
- Students shout out the sound
- Keep score: "10 out of 10! You're experts!"

**Minutes 3-6: Phonics Lesson - Blending Practice (4 mins)**

**Review blending with familiar words:**
- Quick drill: Show word cards, students read
  - MAT, SAT, BAT, TAN, PAN, MAN

**Introduce concept: "Every word needs a VOWEL!"**
- "Remember our vowels: A, E, I, O, U"
- "Today we know A!"
- "Let's try words with A in the middle"

**Blend new words:**
- TAP (tap your desk!)
- SAP (sticky like tree sap!)
- NAP (pretend to sleep!)

**Minutes 7-13: Story Reading (7 mins)**
- Read pages 10-12 - THE TURNIP COMES UP!
- **Excitement!** "They pulled and pulled and... POP!"
- Students act it out: everyone pretends to pull, then fall backward!
- **Discussion:**
  - "Why did the turnip finally come up?"
  - "Who helped?"
  - "What was the best part?"

**Minutes 14-15: Quick Check (2 mins)**
- "Can you read this word?" ‚Üí Show: NAP
- "How about this one?" ‚Üí Show: TAP
- "Last one!" ‚Üí Show: SAP
- **Celebrate:** "You finished the Enormous Turnip story AND learned to read!"

---

### ‚úçÔ∏è WRITING SESSION (15 minutes)

**Minutes 1-5: Mini-Lesson - Dictation Practice (5 mins)**

**More advanced dictation:**
- Teacher says words, students write:
  1. tap
  2. nap
  3. sap
  4. jab (new!)
  5. tab (new!)

**Teach:** "Listen carefully to all the sounds!"
- Some students might be ready for: "The man sat."
- Others still on single words - that's okay!

**Minutes 6-12: Guided Practice (7 mins)**
- **Worksheet:** Write 5 CVC words from pictures
  - Picture of bat ‚Üí write "bat"
  - Picture of cat ‚Üí write "cat"
  - Picture of map ‚Üí write "map"
  - Etc.

- **Teacher circulates:**
  - Help sound out
  - Check letter formation
  - Celebrate success!

**Minutes 13-15: Journal Time (3 mins)**
- **Prompt:** "Draw the turnip coming out of the ground! Write the word 'up' and any other words you know!"
- Students may write: up, man, big, pop, etc.
- **Share:** "Who wants to show their picture?"
- Celebrate all efforts!

---

### üè† HOMEWORK:
- Read the word cards to your family: man, bat, sat, tan, nap
- Draw your own enormous vegetable!

## üåü Day 5 - Friday (Lesson 5)

### Theme: "Celebration Day! We Finished Our First Story!"

---

### üìñ READING SESSION (15 minutes)

**Minutes 1-2: Celebration Intro**
- "We read an entire story this week!"
- "You learned 8 letters and how to read words!"
- "Today we celebrate and review!"

**Minutes 3-6: Phonics Review - Game Time! (4 mins)**

**Game: "Sound Bingo"**
- Students make 3x3 grid
- Write letters: M, S, P, A, N, T, B, J, (blank)
- Teacher calls out sounds
- Students cover letters
- First to get 3 in a row wins!

**Quick blending race:**
- Who can read these fastest?
- MAT, SAT, BAT, TAN, PAN, MAN, NAP, TAP, SAP

**Minutes 7-13: Story Re-Reading & Retelling (7 mins)**
- **Fast read-through** of whole story (teacher + students)
- Students join in on parts they can read
- **Retelling activity:**
  - "What happened first?"
  - "What happened next?"
  - "How did it end?"
- Act it out one more time! (assign roles: man, woman, boy, girl, dog, cat, mouse, turnip)

**Minutes 14-15: Quick Check & Celebration (2 mins)**
- "Count how many words you can read!"
- Show word cards, students read
- Keep score: "You can read ___ words! Amazing!"
- **Billy the Goat says:** "Great job, readers! Next week, new adventure!"

---

### ‚úçÔ∏è WRITING SESSION (15 minutes)

**Minutes 1-5: Mini-Lesson - Write 3 Words Independently (5 mins)**

**Challenge: "Can you write these words without help?"**
- Teacher says word
- Students write independently
- Check together

**Words:**
1. bat
2. man
3. sat

**For advanced students:** Try "The man sat."

**Minutes 6-12: Guided Practice - Free Writing! (7 mins)**

**Choice time:**
- Students can:
  - Write any 5 words they know
  - Copy their favorite sentence from the story
  - Try writing "I see a man"

**Teacher observes:**
- Note who needs more support
- Note who is ready for next level
- Give specific feedback

**Minutes 13-15: Journal Time - Final Page! (3 mins)**
- **Prompt:** "Draw your favorite part of the Enormous Turnip story. Write as many words as you can!"
- Students work independently
- Some may write 1 word, some may write 5+ words
- **ALL EFFORTS CELEBRATED!**

### üìä ASSESSMENT CHECK (Teacher Notes)

**By end of Week 1, students should be able to:**
- [ ] Recognize and name letters M, S, P, A, N, T, B, J
- [ ] Say the sound for each letter
- [ ] Write uppercase and lowercase letters
- [ ] Blend simple CVC words (mat, sat, bat)
- [ ] Write 3-5 CVC words from dictation
- [ ] Follow along with a story
- [ ] Draw and label pictures

**If students struggle:**
- Spend extra time on letter sounds
- More practice with tracing before independent writing
- Break blending into smaller steps
- That's okay! Everyone learns at their own pace!

**If students excel:**
- Introduce more CVC words
- Try simple sentences
- Add sight word "the"
- Ready for Week 2!

## üí° Teacher Tips for Week 1:

1. **Go slow!** This is just Week 1. Foundation is everything.
2. **Celebrate small wins** - Every letter learned is huge!
3. **Differentiate** - Some students will zoom ahead, some need more time. Both are okay!
4. **Make it fun** - Games, acting, silly voices, movement!
5. **Parent communication** - Send home what you're working on!
6. **Adjust as needed** - If 15 mins feels too long, go shorter. If they need more, add time!
7. **Track progress** - Note who needs extra support
8. **Be patient** - This is HARD work for 6-year-olds!
9. **Billy is your friend** - Use Billy the Goat as motivation! "Billy says great job!"
10. **You've got this!** üí™
