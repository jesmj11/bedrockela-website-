# Lesson 1: Blending & Letter Sounds (m, s, p, a)

## Audio Script for Billy (~3-4 minutes)

---

**[INTRO - Warm & Excited]**

Today is a super special day. Do you know why? Because today, you're going to learn the SECRET to reading. Yep ‚Äî the actual secret! And once you know it, you'll be able to read words all by yourself. How cool is that?

**[TEACHING - The Blending Secret]**

Okay, here's the big secret: Words are made of SOUNDS pushed together. 

Let me show you what I mean. Listen to this: /mmm/ - /aaa/ - /p/

Did you hear those three sounds? Now watch what happens when I push them together really fast: /mmm/-/aaa/-/p/... MAP! 

The word is MAP! We call this "blending" ‚Äî pushing sounds together to make words. You're going to be a blending superstar!

**[TEACHING - Letter Sound: M]**

Let's learn our first sound. This is the letter M. 

The letter M says /mmm/. 

Say it with me: /mmm/

Great! It sounds like when you eat something yummy: "Mmm, delicious!" 

/mmm/ - just like that!

**[TEACHING - Letter Sound: S]**

Next up is the letter S.

The letter S says /sss/. 

Say it with me: /sss/

It sounds like a sneaky snake! Sssssss. 

Can you make the snake sound? /sss/ Perfect!

**[TEACHING - Letter Sound: P]**

Now let's learn the letter P.

The letter P says /p/. It's a quick, quiet sound ‚Äî just a little puff of air.

Say it with me: /p/

Feel that little puff? That's the /p/ sound. Like when you pop a bubble: /p/!

**[TEACHING - Letter Sound: A]**

Our last sound today is the letter A.

Now A is special ‚Äî it's called a VOWEL. Every word needs at least one vowel, and A is our first one!

The letter A says /aaa/. 

Say it with me: /aaa/

It's the sound you make when the doctor looks in your throat: "Say /aaa/!" 

**[PRACTICE - Blending Together]**

Okay mountain climber, let's use our new sounds to read a word!

I'll say the sounds slowly, and then we'll blend them together. Ready?

/sss/ - /aaa/ - /mmm/

You just read your first word! The word is SAM! Sam could be someone's name!

Let's try another one!

/mmm/ - /aaa/ - /p/

Blend it together: /mmm/-/aaa/-/p/... MAP!

You did it! MAP! A map helps us find our way up the mountain!

**[WRAP UP - Celebration]**

Wow, wow, WOW! You learned FOUR letter sounds today AND you read TWO words! 

Let's remember what we learned:
- M says /mmm/
- S says /sss/  
- P says /p/

And the secret to reading? Push the sounds together ‚Äî that's called BLENDING!

I'm so proud of you, mountain climber. Now it's time to practice what you learned. You've got this!

## Teaching Notes

**Duration:** ~3-4 minutes at natural speaking pace

**Voice Direction:** 
- Warm, encouraging, enthusiastic but not over-the-top
- Slow down and emphasize when saying individual sounds
- Pause briefly after "say it with me" prompts to let kids respond
- Celebrate genuinely at the end

**Key Vocabulary:**
- Blending: pushing sounds together to make words
- Vowel: a special letter (a, e, i, o, u) that every word needs

**Words Used:**
- SAM (s-a-m)
- MAP (m-a-p)

## Practice Questions (Following Audio)

1. **Sound Match:** Which letter makes the /mmm/ sound? [M] [S] [P]
2. **Sound Match:** Which letter makes the /sss/ sound? [M] [S] [P]  
3. **Sound Match:** Which letter makes the /p/ sound? [M] [S] [P]
4. **Sound Match:** Which letter says /aaa/? [A] [M] [S]
5. **Blending:** Listen: /s/-/a/-/m/. What word is it? [SAM] [MAP] [PAM]
6. **Blending:** Listen: /m/-/a/-/p/. What word is it? [SAM] [MAP] [PAM]
7. **Blending:** Listen: /p/-/a/-/m/. What word is it? [SAM] [MAP] [PAM]
8. **Sound Position:** What sound does MAP start with? [/m/] [/a/] [/p/]
9. **Drag & Build:** Drag letters to spell SAM: [S] [A] [M]
10. **Drag & Build:** Drag letters to spell MAP: [M] [A] [P]
