# The Boy Who Cried Wolf
## A Beginning Reader for Lessons 28-33
### A Public Domain Retelling For Beginning Readers

---

## For the Teacher or Parent

**Teacher/Parent Note:**  
This retelling of The Boy Who Cried Wolf is a short, powerful fable perfect for practicing **blends** (especially the word "wolf" itself with the /wl/ sound, plus cr, gr, sh, etc.) and discussing **cause & effect** and **character consequences**. The repetitive "Wolf! Wolf!" makes it easy for children to participate. **Bold words** are key blend/digraph words. *Underlined words* are sight words to practice.

**Target Lessons:** 28-33  
**Phonics Focus:** Initial blends (cr, gr, sh, bl, fl, tr, wl), final blends (-ft, -st, -nd, -lp), review of all patterns  
**Key Sight Words:** the, a, was, there, said, he, they, came, come, help, no, what, but, when, then, all, too, so, now  
**Decodable Blend Words:** wolf, cried, sheep, help, hill, grass, craft, tricks, glad, flock, trust, left, must, just, fast, last  
**Comprehension Skills:** Cause & effect, character motivation, consequences, honesty/lying, story moral/lesson, predictions

---

## How to Use This Book:

1. **First Read:** Read the story aloud to the child. Let them enjoy it! Use an excited voice for the boy's trick.
2. **Second Read:** Read together. Let the child shout "Wolf! Wolf!" with you!
3. **Third Read:** Let the child try to read with your help. Point to each word.
4. **Practice:** Use the blending words page and TALK about the lesson: Why is lying wrong? What happened because the boy lied?

His job was to watch the **sheep**.

All day **long**, he **sat** in the **green grass**

**Munch, munch, munch.**

## Back Matter

### Blending Practice: Initial Blends

**CR blends:** **cr**ied, **cr**ept  
**GR blends:** **gr**een, **gr**ass, **gr**ay  
**SH digraph:** **sh**eep, **sh**arp  
**TR blends:** **tr**ick, **tr**icked, **tr**ust, **tr**uth  
**FL blends:** **fl**ock  
**BL blends:** **bl**eieve  

**The word WOLF:** Practice the /wl/ blend sound at the start!

**Practice Sentence:**  
"The boy **cried** 'Wolf!' and **tricked** the people on the **green** **hill**."

---

**-ST blends:** ju**st**, la**st**, tru**st**, fa**st**, lo**st**  
**-FT blends:** le**ft**  
**-ND blends:** a**nd**  
**-LP blends:** he**lp**  

**Practice Sentence:**  
"The people ran **fast** up the hill to **help**, but they were **just** being **tricked**."

### Talk About the Story

#### Comprehension Questions:
1. What was the boy's job?
2. Why did the boy shout "Wolf! Wolf!" the first time?
3. What happened when the people ran up the hill?
4. How many times did the boy trick the people?
5. What happened when a REAL wolf came?
6. Why didn't the people come to help the third time?
7. How did the boy feel at the end?
8. What is the lesson of this story?

#### Deep Discussion Questions:
9. Was the boy's trick funny? Why or why not?
10. How do you think the people felt when they found out it was a trick?
11. Have you ever told a lie? What happened?
12. Why is it important to tell the truth?
13. What would YOU do if you were bored watching sheep?
14. Can the boy earn back the people's trust? How?

### Extension Activities

#### **Cause & Effect Chart**
Make a chart showing what happened:

| CAUSE (What the boy did) | EFFECT (What happened) |
|--------------------------|------------------------|
| The boy cried "Wolf!" (lie #1) | People came to help |
| The boy laughed and said "I tricked you!" | People were not glad |
| The boy cried "Wolf!" again (lie #2) | People came again |
| The boy laughed again | People got mad, said they wouldn't trust him |
| A REAL wolf came | The boy cried for help |
| The people heard him cry | They did NOT come (thought it was another trick) |
| No one helped | The boy lost three sheep |

**Question:** What was the BIGGEST consequence of lying?

- **Be the boy:** Shout "WOLF! WOLF! HELP! HELP!"

#### **Moral/Lesson**
This is a **fable** â€” a story that teaches a lesson.

**The lesson:** When you lie, people will not trust you. Even when you tell the truth, they might not believe you.

**Other ways to say it:**
- "Honesty is the best policy"
- "If you lie, people won't believe you when you tell the truth"
- "Trust is hard to earn and easy to lose"

**Question:** How can you be an honest person?

---

#### **Compare to Other Stories**
- **Three Billy Goats Gruff:** The troll tried to trick the goats, but the BIG goat stopped him. The boy tried to trick the people, and he got in trouble.
- **Three Little Pigs:** The pigs who worked hard (big pig) had good consequences. The boy who lied had bad consequences.
- **Connection:** Your CHOICES have CONSEQUENCES!

---

#### **Billy's Connection**
Billy the Goat teaches honesty on the reading mountain! When you make a mistake, you admit it and try harder next time. You don't trick your teacher or parent by pretending to read when you're guessing. Billy says: **Be honest, work hard, and people will trust you!**

Also: The boy's job was to watch SHEEP (animals). Billy is a GOAT (also an animal). What other animal jobs can you think of?

## Additional Teaching Notes

### Why This Story is Perfect for Lessons 28-33:

1. **Blends practice:** wolf, cried, sheep, tricks, trust, help, fast, just, last
2. **Short & punchy:** Great for maintaining attention
3. **Clear moral:** Easy to discuss consequences
4. **Repetitive structure:** "Wolf! Wolf! Help! Help!" gives kids confidence
5. **Cause & effect:** Every action has a consequence (perfect for comprehension skill)
6. **Character motivation:** WHY did the boy lie? (boredom â†’ bad choice)
7. **Real-world application:** Honesty matters in daily life

### Cross-Curricular Connections:

- **Social Studies:** Community helpers (people in the town helped each other)
- **Character Education:** Honesty, trustworthiness, consequences of lying
- **SEL (Social-Emotional Learning):** Understanding how our actions affect others

### Vocabulary to Teach:

- **lie** (tell something that's not true)
- **trust** (believe someone will tell the truth)
- **trick** (fool someone)
- **consequence** (what happens because of your choice)
- **fable** (a short story with a lesson/moral)
- **moral** (the lesson the story teaches)
