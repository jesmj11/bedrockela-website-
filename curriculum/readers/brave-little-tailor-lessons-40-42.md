# The Brave Little Tailor
## A Beginning Reader for Lessons 40-42
### A Fairy Tale Retelling For Beginning Readers

---

## For the Teacher or Parent

**Teacher/Parent Note:**  
This retelling uses **AI/AY vowel team patterns** and reviews **Magic E**. The repeated phrase "Seven at one blow!" encourages fluency and excitement! **Bold words** feature target patterns. *Underlined words* are sight words. This story teaches cleverness and confidence.

**Target Lessons:** 40-42  
**Phonics Focus:** AI/AY vowel teams, Magic E review  
**Key Sight Words:** the, a, said, was, one, two, put, they  
**Decodable Words:** brave, tailor, wait, may, day, say, away, stay, tail, pain, made, came, name, safe  
**Comprehension Skills:** Problem-solving, character traits, sequence

---

## How to Use This Book:

1. **First Read:** Read with expression! Make "Seven at one blow!" sound proud!
2. **Second Read:** Child joins in on repeated phrases.
3. **Third Read:** Point to AI/AY words and review the vowel team rule.
4. **Practice:** Two vowels go walking, the first one does the talking!

## The Story

### Page 1
Once upon a **day**, there lived a little **tailor**. He **made** clothes. He sat and sewed all **day** long.

One **day**, he **made** some jam. He put it on bread.

But flies came! Seven flies landed on his jam!

[Buzz like flies! Seven flies on the jam!]

---

### Page 2
"Go **away**, flies!" said the **tailor**. He was mad.

He grabbed a cloth. **SMACK!**

He hit all seven flies at once! They all fell down.

"Seven at one blow!" he cried. "I am **brave**!"

[Say it loud and proud: "Seven at one blow!"]

---

### Page 3
The **tailor** **made** a belt. On it, he wrote:

**"SEVEN AT ONE BLOW!"**

He put on the belt. He felt very **brave**.

"I will go out into the world!" he said. "I will show everyone how **brave** I am!"

And off he went.

[Act confident like the tailor! Stand tall!]

---

### Page 4
As the **tailor** walked, he **came** to a big hill. A giant sat on top!

The giant saw the belt. It said, "SEVEN AT ONE BLOW!"

"Wow!" said the giant. "You must be very strong! Did you defeat seven men at one blow?"

The **tailor** just smiled. He did not **say** he meant flies!

[The tailor is being clever! He didn't lie, but he didn't explain!]

---

### Page 5
"Let me test you," said the giant. He picked up a rock. He squeezed it. Water dripped out!

"Can you do that?" asked the giant.

The **tailor** thought fast. He had some cheese in his pack. He pulled it out and squeezed. **Whey** dripped out!

"Of course I can!" said the **tailor**.

[The tailor is smart! He used cheese, not a rock!]

---

### Page 6
The giant was amazed. "Okay, let's see you throw," he said.

The giant threw a rock. It flew far, then fell.

The **tailor** had a little bird in his pack. He threw the bird up into the sky!

The bird flew **away** and did not come back!

"Mine did not fall!" said the **tailor**. "It flew **away**!"

[The tailor tricks the giant again!]

---

### Page 7
The giant was impressed. "Come **stay** with me!" he said.

That night, the **tailor** slept in the giant's cave. But the bed was too big!

So the **tailor** slept in the corner instead.

The giant tried to smash the bed in the night! But the **tailor** was **safe** in the corner!

[Smart thinking kept the tailor safe!]

---

### Page 8
In the morning, the giant ran **away**. He thought the **tailor** was too strong to defeat!

The **tailor** kept walking. He **came** to a castle.

The king saw his belt: "SEVEN AT ONE BLOW!"

"You are very **brave**!" said the king. "Will you help me? A dragon is in the woods. It will not go **away**."

[Now there's a real challenge! A dragon!]

---

### Page 9
The **tailor** agreed. He went into the woods.

He found the dragon. It was huge! But it was asleep and snoring.

The **tailor** climbed a tree. He dropped pebbles on the dragon's nose.

The dragon woke up! It thought another dragon hit it!

[Clever again! The tailor makes the dragon confused!]

---

### Page 10
The dragon roared! It looked around. It saw its own shadow and thought it was another dragon!

The two "dragons" (really just one confused dragon!) fought. The dragon chased its own **tail**!

It got so dizzy, it ran **away** into the hills!

The **tailor** climbed down. "The dragon is gone!" he called.

[The dragon fought itself! The tailor was very clever!]

---

### Page 11
The king was amazed! "You are the **bravest** man I know!" he said.

"You **may** marry my daughter and live in the castle!"

And so the little **tailor** became a prince! He lived happily ever after.

All because he was clever and **brave**!

[The tailor used his brain, not his muscles!]

---

### Page 12
**"SEVEN AT ONE BLOW!"**

And that is the story of the **brave** little **tailor** who defeated seven flies, tricked a giant, and chased away a dragon‚Äîall with cleverness and courage!

[The end! What a clever hero!]

## Back Matter

### Blending Practice
**AI/AY Vowel Team Words from the Story:**  
brave, tailor, wait, may, day, say, away, stay, tail, pain, grain, rain, chain

**Magic E Review:**  
made, came, name, safe, smack, smoke

**Other Decodable Words:**  
flies, jam, hit, fell, belt, show, rock, hill, test, pack

### Repeated Phrases to Practice
**"Seven at one blow!"** | **"Go away!"** | **"I am brave!"**

### Talk About the Story
1. What did the tailor hit? (Seven flies!)
2. What did the giant think the tailor defeated? (Seven men!)
3. How did the tailor trick the giant with the rock? (He squeezed cheese instead!)
4. How did he trick the giant with throwing? (He threw a bird, not a rock!)
5. How did he defeat the dragon? (He made it fight itself!)
6. What makes the tailor clever? (He uses his brain to solve problems!)

### Extension Activities
- **AI/AY Hunt:** Find all the AI and AY words. Make two lists!
- **Act It Out:** Act out the tailor squeezing cheese and throwing the bird!
- **Draw & Write:** Draw the tailor's belt with "Seven at one blow!" on it.
- **Billy's Connection:** Billy the Goat is clever like the tailor! He uses his brain to solve mountain problems. You can be clever too!

### Character Education
**Theme:** Intelligence and quick thinking can be more powerful than strength. The little tailor wasn't strong, but he was smart! You don't have to be the biggest to be the bravest!
