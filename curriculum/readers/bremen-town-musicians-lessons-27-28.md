# The Bremen Town Musicians
## A Beginning Reader for Lessons 27-28
### A Public Domain Retelling For Beginning Readers

---

## For the Teacher or Parent

**Teacher/Parent Note:**  
This retelling of The Bremen Town Musicians is PERFECT for practicing **onomatopoeia** (sound words) from Lesson 27! Kids LOVE making animal sounds. **Bold words** are key blend words and sound words. *Underlined words* are sight words to practice.

**Target Lessons:** 27-28  
**Phonics Focus:** Onomatopoeia (cock-a-doodle-doo, meow, woof, hee-haw), blends (Bremen, friends, stand, strum, stomp)  
**Key Sight Words:** the, a, was, were, he, said, I, we, will, not, can, are, my, what, who, they, all, then, come, there  
**Decodable Blend Words:** donkey, Bremen, friends, stand, grand, roost, stomp, crept, strum, crash, scare, scared, blast  
**Comprehension Skills:** Teamwork, onomatopoeia/sound words, problem/solution, sequence, characters working together

---

## How to Use This Book:

1. **First Read:** Read the story aloud with LOTS of animal sounds!
2. **Second Read:** Have the child make ALL the animal sounds with you!
3. **Third Read:** Let the child try to read. Keep the sound effects!
4. **Practice:** Stand on each other like the animals! Make a silly tower!

## The Story

### Page 1
There was an old **donkey**.

He was too old to work.

"I will go to **Bremen** Town," said the **donkey**.  
"I will be a **musician**!  
I will sing!"

**HEE-HAW! HEE-HAW!**

And off he went!

[Make donkey sounds! HEE-HAW!]

---

### Page 2
On the path, the **donkey** saw a **dog**.

The **dog** was sad.

"**What** is wrong?" said the **donkey**.

"I am too old to hunt," said the **dog**.  
"I have **no** home."

**WOOF! WOOF!** (Sad woof.)

[Make sad dog sounds! Woof...]

---

### Page 3
"**Come** with me!" said the **donkey**.  
"We **will** go to **Bremen** Town!  
We **will** be **musicians**!  
We **will** sing!"

"Yes!" said the **dog**. "I **will** **come**!"

**HEE-HAW! WOOF! WOOF!**

And off **they** went!

[HEE-HAW! WOOF! Both sounds!]

---

### Page 4
On the path, **they** saw a **cat**.

The **cat** was sad.

"**What** is wrong?" said the **donkey**.

"I am too old to **catch** mice," said the **cat**.  
"I have **no** home."

**MEOW... MEOW...** (Sad meow.)

[Make sad cat sounds! Meow...]

---

### Page 5
"**Come** with us!" said the **donkey**.  
"We **will** go to **Bremen** Town!  
We **will** be **musicians**!  
We **will** sing!"

"Yes!" said the **cat**. "I **will** **come**!"

**HEE-HAW! WOOF! MEOW!**

And off **they** went!

[Three sounds now! HEE-HAW! WOOF! MEOW!]

---

### Page 6
On the path, **they** saw a **rooster**.

The **rooster** was sad.

"**What** is wrong?" said the **donkey**.

"I am too old to wake people up," said the **rooster**.  
"I have **no** home."

**COCK-A-DOODLE-DOO!** (Sad crow.)

[Make rooster sounds! COCK-A-DOODLE-DOO!]

---

### Page 7
"**Come** with us!" said the **donkey**.  
"We **will** go to **Bremen** Town!  
We **will** be **musicians**!  
We **will** sing!"

"Yes!" said the **rooster**. "I **will** **come**!"

**HEE-HAW! WOOF! MEOW! COCK-A-DOODLE-DOO!**

And off **they** went!

[ALL FOUR sounds together! Make it loud!]

---

### Page 8
It got dark.

**They** were tired.  
**They** were hungry.

"We must **stop** for the night," said the **donkey**.

**Then** the **rooster** saw a light!

"Look! A house!" said the **rooster**.

[Point! Look over there!]

---

### Page 9
**They** **crept** to the house.

The **donkey** looked in the window.

"There are **robbers** in there!" said the **donkey**.  
"**They** are eating a **big** feast!"

"I am **so** hungry!" said the **dog**.

[Peek in the window! Robbers!]

---

### Page 10
"Let's **scare** them out!" said the **cat**.

"We **will** make **music**!" said the **donkey**.

"**Stand** on my back!" said the **donkey** to the **dog**.  
"**Stand** on the **dog**!" said the **donkey** to the **cat**.  
"**Stand** on the **cat**!" said the **donkey** to the **rooster**.

**They** made a tall tower!

[Stack your hands like a tower!]

---

### Page 11
"Now... SING!" said the **donkey**.

**HEE-HAW! HEE-HAW!**  
**WOOF! WOOF! WOOF!**  
**MEOW! MEOW! MEOW!**  
**COCK-A-DOODLE-DOO!**

**CRASH!** **They** all fell through the window!

[Make ALL the sounds! CRASH!]

---

### Page 12
The **robbers** were **so scared**!

"A monster!" **they** yelled!

The **robbers** ran out of the house!  
**They** ran and ran and ran!

**They** did **not** come back!

[Run in place! Run away! So scared!]

---

### Page 13
The **four friends** went into the house.

**They** ate the feast.

**Munch, munch, munch!**

**Then they** went to sleep.

The **donkey** slept by the door.  
The **dog** slept on the mat.  
The **cat** slept by the fire.  
The **rooster** slept on the **roost**.

[Pretend to sleep in different spots!]

---

### Page 14
One **robber** **crept** back.

He went into the dark house.

**Creak, creak, creak...**

**Then**...

[Whisper! Tiptoe! Creak, creak...]

---

### Page 15
The **cat** **scratched** him!  
**MEOW!**

The **dog** **bit** him!  
**WOOF!**

The **donkey** kicked him!  
**HEE-HAW!**

The **rooster** **crowed** at him!  
**COCK-A-DOODLE-DOO!**

The **robber** ran away!

"A terrible monster!" he yelled.

[Make all the sounds! Scratch! Bite! Kick! Crow!]

---

### Page 16
The **robbers** never came back.

The **four friends** lived in the house.

**They** sang **all** the time!

**HEE-HAW! WOOF! MEOW! COCK-A-DOODLE-DOO!**

**They** were the **Bremen Town Musicians**!

**The lesson:**

**Friends** working together  
can do **big** things!

**The end!**

[Sing together! Happy ending!]

## Back Matter

### Sound Words Practice (ONOMATOPOEIA!)

**Onomatopoeia** = Words that sound like what they mean!

**Animal Sounds in This Story:**
- **Donkey:** HEE-HAW! HEE-HAW!
- **Dog:** WOOF! WOOF! WOOF!
- **Cat:** MEOW! MEOW! MEOW!
- **Rooster:** COCK-A-DOODLE-DOO!

**Other Sound Words:**
- **CRASH!** (falling through window)
- **Creak, creak, creak** (door opening)
- **Munch, munch, munch** (eating)

**Practice:** What sounds do these make?
- A door: SLAM! CREAK!
- Eating: MUNCH! CRUNCH!
- Water: SPLASH! DRIP!
- Footsteps: STOMP! TAP! TIP-TOE!

---

### Blending Practice: Blends in the Story

**Initial Blends:**
- **Bremen** (br-)
- **friends** (fr-)
- **stand** (st-)
- **stomp** (st-)
- **crept** (cr-)
- **scare** / **scared** (sc-)

**Final Blends:**
- **roost** (-st)
- **robbers** (double consonant + blend)

**Practice Sentence:**  
"My best **fr**iends will **st**and and **st**omp to **sc**are the robbers in **Br**emen!"

---

### Repeated Phrases to Practice
"**What** is wrong?"  
"I have **no** home."  
"**Come** with us! We **will** be **musicians**!"  
"**HEE-HAW! WOOF! MEOW! COCK-A-DOODLE-DOO!**"  
"**They** ran and ran and ran!"

---

### Talk About the Story

1. Who were the four animals? (donkey, dog, cat, rooster)
2. Where were they going? (Bremen Town)
3. What did they want to be? (musicians!)
4. Why were they all sad at first? (too old, no home)
5. What did they find? (a house with robbers)
6. How did they scare the robbers? (made loud music! HEE-HAW! WOOF! MEOW! COCK-A-DOODLE-DOO!)
7. What happened at the end? (they lived in the house together!)
8. What is the lesson?

### Extension Activities

#### **Teamwork Tower!**
Act out the story's BEST part:
- Four people (or stuffed animals!) stand on each other
- (Safely! Maybe just stack toys or use your hands!)
- Everyone makes their animal sound AT THE SAME TIME!
- Fall down (gently!) like they crashed through the window!

**Lesson:** Together, we can do BIG things!

---

#### **Make Your Own Animal Band**
- Each person picks an animal
- Make that animal's sound
- "Conduct" the band: Point to each person, they make their sound!
- Try it LOUD! Try it soft! Try it FAST!
- You're musicians too!

**Animal Ideas:**
- Dog: WOOF!
- Cat: MEOW!
- Cow: MOO!
- Pig: OINK!
- Duck: QUACK!
- Sheep: BAA!

---

#### **Onomatopoeia Hunt**
Find sound words in OTHER stories!
- **Billy Goats Gruff:** Tap, tap, tap! Stomp, stomp, stomp! Nom, nom, nom!
- **Three Little Pigs:** Huff, puff, WHOOSH, CRASH!
- **Goldilocks:** Knock, knock! Creak, creak! CRACK! BOOM!

**Make a list!** How many sound words can you find?

---

#### **Problem & Solution**

| PROBLEM | SOLUTION |
|---------|----------|
| Animals have no home | They go to Bremen to be musicians |
| They're hungry and tired | They find a house |
| Robbers are in the house | They scare them away with music! |
| Robbers might come back | Animals defend the house! |

**Lesson:** Every problem has a solution! Work together!

---

#### **Sequence the Story**
Put these in order:
1. Donkey meets dog
2. Dog meets cat
3. Cat meets rooster
4. They find house with robbers
5. They make music and scare robbers
6. They eat and sleep
7. Robber comes back
8. Animals scare him again!
9. They live happily in the house

---

#### **Character Traits**
Each animal was:
- **Brave** (faced the robbers!)
- **Helpful** (helped each other)
- **Creative** (made a plan to scare robbers)
- **Loyal** (stuck together as friends)

**Talk about it:**
- Have YOU ever been brave?
- How do YOU help your friends?
- What creative ideas have YOU had?

---

#### **Compare to Other Stories**
- **Enormous Turnip:** Everyone worked together to pull it up!
- **Bremen Musicians:** Everyone worked together to scare robbers!
- **Little Red Hen:** She did NOT work together (friends didn't help!)
- **Connection:** Teamwork is powerful!

---

#### **Draw the Tower**
Draw the four animals standing on each other:
1. Donkey on bottom (biggest)
2. Dog on donkey
3. Cat on dog
4. Rooster on top (smallest)

Label each animal! Draw sound bubbles coming from their mouths!

## Additional Teaching Notes

### Why This Story is Perfect for Lessons 27-28:

1. **Onomatopoeia focus:** TONS of sound words! (Lesson 27 skill!)
2. **Repetitive structure:** Each animal joins, building pattern recognition
3. **Animal sounds = phonics practice:** Breaking sounds into parts
4. **Blend practice:** Bremen, friends, stand, stomp, crept, scared
5. **Teamwork theme:** Beautiful lesson about cooperation
6. **FUN!** Kids LOVE making animal sounds!
7. **Cumulative story:** Adds one animal at a time (like "The House That Jack Built")

### Cross-Curricular Connections:

- **Music:** Animal sounds = music! Rhythm, loud/soft, conducting
- **Science:** Animal sounds (what animals really sound like in nature?)
- **Social Studies:** Cooperation, community, helping those in need
- **Character Education:** Teamwork, bravery, friendship, loyalty
- **Drama:** Perfect for acting out!

### Vocabulary to Teach:

- **musician** (someone who makes music)
- **onomatopoeia** (words that sound like what they mean‚ÄîWOOF!, CRASH!, MEOW!)
- **robber** (someone who steals)
- **feast** (a big, fancy meal)
- **roost** (where a bird sleeps)
- **tower** (tall structure‚Äîthe animals stacked up!)
- **teamwork** (working together)

### Extension: Real Musicians!
- Listen to animal sounds in music (Carnival of the Animals, Peter and the Wolf)
- Make simple instruments (shake a box of rice = maracas!)
- March like a band!
