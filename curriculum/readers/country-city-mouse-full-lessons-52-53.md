# The Country Mouse and the City Mouse (Full Version)
## A Beginning Reader for Lessons 52-53
### An Aesop's Fable Retelling For Beginning Readers

---

## For the Teacher or Parent

**Teacher/Parent Note:**  
This FULL version of a Unit 1 favorite focuses on **IR and UR r-controlled vowels** and reviews all r-controlled patterns (AR, OR, ER). **Bold words** feature r-controlled vowels. *Underlined words* are sight words. Theme: Simple life vs. fancy life.

**Target Lessons:** 52-53  
**Phonics Focus:** IR/UR r-controlled vowels, review AR/OR/ER  
**Key Sight Words:** the, a, was, they, said, you, your, were  
**Decodable Words:** first, third, chirp, dirt, stir, turn, burst, purr, fur, hurt, burn, curb  
**Comprehension Skills:** Compare/contrast, setting, character preferences

---

## How to Use This Book:

1. **First Read:** Use different voices for country mouse (calm) and city mouse (fancy)!
2. **Second Read:** Compare the two settings. Which would you choose?
3. **Third Read:** Hunt for r-controlled words!
4. **Practice:** When R comes after a vowel, it changes the sound!

## The Story

### Page 1
Once there were two mice. One lived in the country. One lived in the city.

They were cousins.

One day, Country Mouse invited City Mouse to visit.

"Come see my home!" he said. "It is simple, but nice."

[Two cousins! One country, one city!]

---

### Page 2
City Mouse took the train. She rode **first** class!

When she arrived, Country Mouse met her at the **corner** of the field.

"Welcome!" he said. "Come, I will show you my home!"

They walked through tall grass. Birds **chirped**. The sun was warm.

[Country Mouse is happy to show his home!]

---

### Page 3
Country Mouse lived in a cozy burrow under a **fir** tree.

Inside, it was simple. A bed of soft grass. A small table.

"Sit!" said Country Mouse. "I will make us **dinner**!"

He served seeds, **corn**, and fresh water.

[A simple home. Simple food. But cozy!]

---

### Page 4
City Mouse looked at the food. She wrinkled her nose.

"Is this all?" she said. "Just seeds and **corn**?"

"Yes," said Country Mouse. "But it is fresh! I picked it this morning."

City Mouse nibbled a seed. It **was** plain.

"You poor thing," she said. "You must come visit me! I will show you how fancy mice live!"

[City Mouse thinks this is boring!]

---

### Page 5
The next week, Country Mouse took the train to the city. He rode **third** class.

City Mouse met him at the **curb**. Cars zoomed by! Horns honked!

"This way!" said City Mouse. "Hurry! Don't get **hurt**!"

Country Mouse's heart began to beat fast. The city **was** loud and busy!

[The city is noisy! Country Mouse is nervous!]

---

### Page 6
City Mouse lived in a grand house. She had a little hole in the wall behind the kitchen.

Inside, her home **was** fancy! A soft cushion bed. A **mirror**. A tiny **silver** spoon!

"Wow!" said Country Mouse. "This is **very** nice!"

"Wait until you see **dinner**!" said City Mouse.

[City Mouse's home is FANCY!]

---

### Page 7
That night, the mice snuck into the kitchen. The table **was** full of food!

Cheese! Cake! Roast beef! **Butter**! Fresh bread!

"Help yourself!" said City Mouse.

Country Mouse's eyes grew wide. "I have **never** seen so much food!"

He took a bite of cheese. It **was** delicious!

[So much fancy food! Country Mouse is amazed!]

---

### Page 8
Suddenly, the door **burst** open!

A huge cat ran in! It **was** the house cat!

"Run!" cried City Mouse. "Hurry! **Turn** here!"

The mice ran as fast as they could! The cat chased them! It **purred** and licked its chops!

[Danger! The cat is chasing them!]

---

### Page 9
The mice dove into the wall. They were safe. But Country Mouse's heart pounded.

"That **was** close!" he gasped. "I almost got **hurt**!"

"Oh, that happens all the time," said City Mouse calmly. "You get used to it."

Country Mouse **was** **very** scared. His **fur** stood on end.

[Country Mouse does NOT like this!]

---

### Page 10
"Let's go back and eat!" said City Mouse. "The cat will leave soon."

But Country Mouse shook his head.

"No thank you," he said. "I would rather eat plain seeds in peace than fancy food in fear."

"I am going home," he said. "I miss my quiet burrow."

[Country Mouse has made his choice!]

---

### Page 11
Country Mouse took the train back home. He **was** happy to see his field again!

Birds **chirped**. The grass swayed. It **was** quiet and safe.

He ate his simple **dinner** of seeds and **corn**. No cat chased him. No danger.

"This is the life **for** me," he said happily.

[Home sweet home!]

---

### Page 12
**THE END**

**Better a simple life in safety than a fancy life in danger.**

Country Mouse chose peace **over** fancy food. And he **was** happy!

[What would YOU choose? Peace or fancy danger?]

## Back Matter

### Blending Practice
**IR R-Controlled Words:**  
first, third, chirp, dirt, stir, bird, girl, shirt, skirt, twirl

**UR R-Controlled Words:**  
turn, burst, purr, fur, hurt, burn, curb, church, nurse, turtle

**Review AR/OR/ER Words:**  
corn, corner, morning, for, over, never, dinner, silver, mirror, butter, servant

**Other Decodable Words:**  
mice, seeds, home, train, fast, ran, dove, safe, back, calm, glad

### Repeated Phrases to Practice
**"Come visit!"** | **"Hurry!"** | **"Run!"** | **"I am going home."**

### Talk About the Story
1. Where did Country Mouse live? (In a burrow under a fir tree)
2. What did Country Mouse serve for dinner? (Seeds, corn, and water)
3. Where did City Mouse live? (In a hole in a grand house)
4. What fancy food did they find? (Cheese, cake, roast beef, butter, bread)
5. What danger did they face? (A big cat chased them!)
6. Which mouse made the better choice? (Answers may vary! Discuss!)

### Extension Activities
- **R-Controlled Hunt:** Find all the words with AR, OR, ER, IR, and UR. Sort them!
- **Act It Out:** Act out running from the cat! Use a scared voice!
- **Draw & Write:** Draw Country Mouse's burrow and City Mouse's fancy home. Which would you choose?
- **Billy's Connection:** Billy the Goat lives a simple mountain life. He has fresh air, good friends, and safety. That's all he needs!

### Character Education
**Theme:** Happiness comes from safety and peace, not from fancy things. Country Mouse chose a simple, safe life over a dangerous, fancy one. What matters most to you?

**Discussion:** Would you rather have lots of toys but always be in trouble, or fewer toys but always be safe and happy?
