# The Enormous Turnip
## A Beginning Reader for Lessons 1-5
### A Public Domain Retelling For Beginning Readers

---

## For the Teacher or Parent

**Teacher/Parent Note:**  
This retelling of The Enormous Turnip uses the VERY FIRST letter sounds taught in Lessons 1-5 (m, s, p, a, n, t, b, j). The repetitive "Pull! Pull! Pull!" refrain makes it perfect for early participation. **Bold words** are key blending words. *Underlined words* are sight words to practice.

**Target Lessons:** 1-5  
**Phonics Focus:** First letter sounds (m, s, p, a, n, t, b, j), basic CVC words  
**Key Sight Words:** the, a, was, is, said, he, it, not, come, but, and  
**Decodable CVC Words:** man, sat, pan, pat, tap, mat, nap, jam, am, an, tan, pop, top, mop, big, bag, tag, jab  
**Comprehension Skills:** Sequence (who came first, next, last), teamwork, predictions, size concepts (enormous/big/small)

---

## How to Use This Book:

1. **First Read:** Read the story aloud to the child. Let them enjoy it! Use different voices for each character.
2. **Second Read:** Read together. Have the child PULL with you! Say "Pull! Pull! Pull!" together.
3. **Third Read:** Let the child try to read with your help. Point to each word.
4. **Practice:** Act it out! Line up toys or family members and pull an imaginary turnip!

## The Story

### Page 1
A **man** had a garden.

He **planted** a **turnip** seed.

"Grow, **turnip**, grow!" said the **man**.

And it did!

It grew and grew and grew!

[Make growing motions with your hands!]

---

### Page 2
The **turnip** grew **ENORMOUS**!

It was as **big** as a **tub**!  
It was as **big** as a **bag**!  
It was **ENORMOUS**!

[Spread your arms WIDE! Show how BIG!]

---

### Page 3
The **man** wanted the **turnip**.

He grabbed the **turnip**.

**Pull! Pull! Pull!**

**But** the **turnip** did **not** come up.

[Pretend to pull! Pull! Pull! Pull!]

---

### Page 4
The **man** called **his** wife.

"**Come** help me!" said the **man**.

The wife grabbed the **man**.  
The **man** grabbed the **turnip**.

**Pull! Pull! Pull!**

**But** the **turnip** did **not** come up.

[Pull together! Pull! Pull! Pull!]

---

### Page 5
The wife called the **son**.

"**Come** help us!" said the wife.

The **son** grabbed the wife.  
The wife grabbed the **man**.  
The **man** grabbed the **turnip**.

**Pull! Pull! Pull!**

**But** the **turnip** did **not** come up.

[Pull! Getting stronger!]

---

### Page 6
The **son** called the dog.

"**Come** help us!" said the **son**.

The dog grabbed the **son**.  
The **son** grabbed the wife.  
The wife grabbed the **man**.  
The **man** grabbed the **turnip**.

**Pull! Pull! Pull!**

**But** the **turnip** did **not** come up.

[Woof! Woof! Bark like the dog!]

---

### Page 7
The dog called the **cat**.

"**Come** help us!" said the dog.

The **cat** grabbed the dog.  
The dog grabbed the **son**.  
The **son** grabbed the wife.  
The wife grabbed the **man**.  
The **man** grabbed the **turnip**.

**Pull! Pull! Pull!**

**But** the **turnip** did **not** come up.

[Meow! Meow! Meow like a cat!]

---

### Page 8
The **cat** called the mouse.

"**Come** help us!" said the **cat**.

The tiny mouse said, "I will help!"

[Use a teeny tiny voice for the mouse!]

---

### Page 9
The mouse grabbed the **cat**.  
The **cat** grabbed the dog.  
The dog grabbed the **son**.  
The **son** grabbed the wife.  
The wife grabbed the **man**.  
The **man** grabbed the **turnip**.

**Pull! Pull! Pull!**

[Everyone pull! Pull! Pull! Pull!]

---

### Page 10
**POP!**

Up came the **turnip**!

**BOOM!**

They all fell down!

[Fall down! BOOM! Laugh!]

---

### Page 11
"We did it!" said the **man**.

"We did it!" said the wife.

"We did it!" said the **son**.

"We did it!" said the dog.

"We did it!" said the **cat**.

"We did it!" said the mouse.

[Cheer! Yay! We did it!]

---

### Page 12
They had **turnip** soup.  
They had **turnip** **jam**.  
They had **turnip** pie.

**Yum, yum, yum!**

And they all said,

"**Teamwork** is the best!"

**The end!**

[Pretend to eat! Yum, yum, yum!]

### Talk About the Story

1. What did the man plant?
2. How big did the turnip grow?
3. Who came to help FIRST? (wife)
4. Who came LAST? (mouse)
5. Who was the biggest helper? Who was the smallest?
6. Did the mouse matter even though he was small?
7. What does "teamwork" mean?

### Extension Activities

#### **Sequence Chain**
Put the helpers in order! Use toys or draw pictures:
1. Man
2. Wife
3. Son
4. Dog
5. Cat
6. Mouse (smallest but IMPORTANT!)

#### **Act It Out**
- Line up family members or stuffed animals
- Pretend to pull an enormous turnip!
- Everyone say "Pull! Pull! Pull!" together
- Fall down when it pops up! BOOM!

#### **Size Sorting**
- Find toys: biggest â†’ smallest
- Talk about: enormous, big, small, tiny
- The mouse was TINY but still helped!

#### **Teamwork Discussion**
- What is teamwork?
- Have you ever worked with a team?
- Why is teamwork important?
- Even small people can help in BIG ways!

#### **Make Turnip "Soup"**
- Pretend to make soup together
- What would you put in it?
- Practice cooking vocabulary (stir, chop, pour, taste)

#### **Billy's Connection**
Billy the Goat says: "Even the smallest goat can climb a big mountain if everyone works together!" Just like the tiny mouse helped pull up the enormous turnip, even beginning readers can tackle big stories with help from teachers, parents, and friends!
