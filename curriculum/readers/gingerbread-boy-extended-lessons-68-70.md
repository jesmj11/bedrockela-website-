# The Gingerbread Boy Runs Away (Extended)
## A Beginning Reader for Lessons 68-70
### A Folktale Retelling For Beginning Readers

---

## For the Teacher or Parent

**Teacher/Parent Note:**  
This extended version focuses on **compound words** (someone, something, outside) and **contractions** (can't, won't, don't). It's designed to be read across lessons 68-70 as students learn these concepts. **Bold words** feature target patterns. Theme: Overconfidence leads to trouble!

**Target Lessons:** 68-70  
**Phonics Focus:** Compound words, contractions, plurals  
**Key Sight Words:** the, said, he, they, you, very  
**Decodable Words:** gingerbread, something, everyone, anyone, outside, inside, can't, don't, won't  
**Comprehension Skills:** Sequence, cause/effect, prediction

---

## The Story

### Page 1
An old woman made a **gingerbread** boy. She gave him raisin eyes and a smile.

"**Someone** will love to eat you!" she said.

She put him in the oven to bake.

But when she opened the oven door, the **gingerbread** boy jumped out!

"You **can't** catch me!" he cried. And he ran **outside**!

---

### Page 2
The old woman ran after him. "Come back!"

But the **gingerbread** boy just laughed. "Run, run, as fast as you can! You **can't** catch me, I'm the **gingerbread** man!"

He ran past **everyone** he saw. The old man tried to catch him. The cow tried too. But **they** **couldn't** catch him!

---

### Page 3
The **gingerbread** boy came to a river. He **couldn't** swim!

A fox was sitting by the water. "I **won't** eat you," said the fox. "But I can help you across. Jump on my back!"

## Back Matter

### Blending Practice
**Compound Words:** gingerbread, something, someone, everyone, anyone, outside, inside, cannot, himself, yourself

**Contractions:** can't, won't, don't, couldn't, didn't, I'm, he's

**Talk About the Story:**
1. Why did the gingerbread boy run away?
2. What did he say to everyone? ("You can't catch me!")
3. How did the fox trick him?
4. What's the lesson? (Don't be overconfident!)
