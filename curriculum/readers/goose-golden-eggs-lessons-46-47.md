# The Goose with Golden Eggs
## A Beginning Reader for Lessons 46-47
### An Aesop's Fable Retelling For Beginning Readers

---

## For the Teacher or Parent

**Teacher/Parent Note:**  
This Aesop fable uses **OA/OE vowel team patterns** and **EW/UE** words. The moral is clear: Don't be greedy! **Bold words** feature target vowel teams. *Underlined words* are sight words. Short and powerful!

**Target Lessons:** 46-47  
**Phonics Focus:** OA/OE vowel teams, EW/UE vowel teams  
**Key Sight Words:** the, a, was, one, they, said, of, every  
**Decodable Words:** goose, gold, toad, coat, boat, road, slow, show, know, grow, new, few, flew  
**Comprehension Skills:** Moral lesson, cause and effect, greed vs. patience

---

## How to Use This Book:

1. **First Read:** Read aloud. Pause before the ending to let the child predict!
2. **Second Read:** Discuss the moral lesson. What does "greed" mean?
3. **Third Read:** Point to OA/OE and EW/UE words.
4. **Practice:** Two vowels together make one sound!

## The Story

### Page 1
Once there **was** a man and a woman. They had very little **gold**. They lived in a small hut by the **road**.

One day, they found a **goose**.

"Let us keep it," said the woman. "We can sell its eggs."

[A goose! They found it by the road!]

---

### Page 2
The next morning, the woman went to check on the **goose**.

She gasped! There **was** an egg. But it **was** not white. It **was** **gold**!

"Look!" she cried. "A **golden** egg!"

They sold the **gold** egg. They bought food and a **new** **coat**.

[A golden egg! What a surprise!]

---

### Page 3
**Every** day, the **goose** laid one **golden** egg. Just one.

The man and woman would sell it. **Slowly**, they got more and more **gold**.

They bought **new** clothes. They fixed their hut. They had plenty to eat.

Life **was** good!

[One egg every day. Slow and steady!]

---

### Page 4
But soon, the man got greedy.

"We only get one egg a day!" he said. "That is too **slow**! I want all the **gold** at once!"

"Let's cut open the **goose**," he said. "All the **golden** eggs must be inside!"

The woman did not **know** if this **was** a good idea. But she agreed.

[Uh oh! This sounds like a bad idea!]

---

### Page 5
They caught the **goose**. They cut it open.

But inside? There **was** nothing. No **golden** eggs. Just an ordinary **goose**.

Now the **goose** **was** dead. It could not lay any more **golden** eggs. Not even one.

The man and woman had nothing. Their greed cost them everything.

[Oh no! What did they do?]

---

### Page 6
The man and woman sat by the **road**. They were sad.

"We should have been patient," said the woman. "We had one **golden** egg **every** day. Now we have none."

They learned a lesson that day:

**Don't be greedy. Be grateful for what you have.**

[What a sad ending! But they learned their lesson!]

---

### Page 7
If they had waited, the **goose** would have given them one **golden** egg **every** day.

**Slow** and steady is better than trying to get everything at once.

Greed can make you lose it all.

**THE END**

[Remember: Be patient and grateful!]

## Back Matter

### Blending Practice
**OA/OE Vowel Team Words:**  
goose, gold, toad, coat, boat, road, loaf, soap, toast, toe, hoe, Joe

**EW/UE Vowel Team Words:**  
slow, show, know, grow, new, few, flew, blew, grew, crew

**Other Decodable Words:**  
man, hut, got, egg, nest, cut, just, fed, let, but, sad

### Repeated Phrases to Practice
**"A golden egg!"** | **"Every day"** | **"Slow and steady"**

### Talk About the Story
1. What did the goose lay? (A golden egg!)
2. How many golden eggs did the goose lay each day? (One)
3. Why did the man cut open the goose? (He was greedy and wanted all the gold at once)
4. What happened when they cut open the goose? (There was nothing inside! The goose died.)
5. What is the lesson? (Don't be greedy. Be patient and grateful.)

### Extension Activities
- **OA/OE Hunt:** Circle all the words with OA or OE.
- **Act It Out:** Pretend to find the golden egg! Gasp with surprise!
- **Draw & Write:** Draw the goose laying a golden egg. What would you do if you found a golden egg?
- **Billy's Connection:** Billy the Goat knows that patience pays off! Climbing a mountain takes time. You can't rush to the top!
