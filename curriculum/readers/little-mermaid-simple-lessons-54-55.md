# The Little Mermaid (Simple Version)
## A Beginning Reader for Lessons 54-55
### A Fairy Tale Retelling For Beginning Readers

---

## For the Teacher or Parent

**Teacher/Parent Note:**  
This simplified version of The Little Mermaid reviews **ALL r-controlled vowels** (AR, OR, ER, IR, UR). The underwater setting is magical and engaging! **Bold words** feature r-controlled patterns. *Underlined words* are sight words. Themes: Sacrifice, love, longing.

**Target Lessons:** 54-55  
**Phonics Focus:** Review all r-controlled vowels (AR, OR, ER, IR, UR)  
**Key Sight Words:** the, was, a, they, she, her, could, would  
**Decodable Words:** mermaid, sister, water, under, over, far, star, never, forever, hurt, pearl, fern, first, bird  
**Comprehension Skills:** Character motivation, theme, sequence, story arc

---

## How to Use This Book:

1. **First Read:** Read with wonder! Make the ocean sound magical!
2. **Second Read:** Talk about the mermaid's feelings. Why did she want legs?
3. **Third Read:** Hunt for r-controlled words. Review the patterns!
4. **Practice:** R changes vowel sounds! AR, OR, ER, IR, UR!

## The Story

### Page 1
**Far** down **under** the sea, there lived a little mermaid.

She had a beautiful tail and long hair. She lived with **her** **sisters** in a **pearl** palace.

The little mermaid loved to swim **over** coral reefs and through forests of sea **ferns**.

But **her** favorite thing **was** to swim to the surface and look at the **stars**.

[Under the sea! A mermaid with her sisters!]

---

### Page 2
One day, the little mermaid saw a ship on the **water** above.

She swam to the surface. She saw a prince on the ship! He **was** kind and handsome.

Suddenly, a **storm** came! The ship rocked! The prince fell into the **water**!

"I must help him!" cried the little mermaid.

[A prince! A storm! The prince is in danger!]

---

### Page 3
The little mermaid dove **under** the waves. She found the prince sinking down, down, down.

She grabbed him and pulled him up! She swam with him to the shore.

She laid him on the sand. He **was** safe! He would not be **hurt**.

Then she swam away before he **woke** up.

[She saved him! The prince is safe!]

---

### Page 4
The little mermaid swam home. But she could not stop thinking about the prince.

"I want to see him again," she said. "I want to be **with** him **forever**."

"But I am a mermaid," she said sadly. "He is a human. We can **never** be together."

**Her** **sisters** tried to cheer **her** up. But the little mermaid **was** too sad.

[She loves the prince! But she's a mermaid!]

---

### Page 5
The little mermaid swam **far** from home. She found the sea witch.

"I want legs," said the little mermaid. "I want to walk on land and be with the prince."

The sea witch smiled. "I can help you," she said. "But there is a price."

[The sea witch! She can give the mermaid legs!]

---

### Page 6
"I will give you legs," said the witch. "But you must give me **your** voice."

"And remember," said the witch, "if the prince does not love you, you will **turn** into sea foam **forever**."

The little mermaid thought hard. She loved the prince so much.

"I will do it," she said.

[Giving up her voice! A big sacrifice!]

---

### Page 7
The sea witch gave the little mermaid a potion. She drank it.

**Her** tail began to change! It split into two legs!

But when she tried to speak, no sound came out. **Her** voice **was** gone.

The little mermaid swam to the surface. She climbed onto the shore.

[She has legs! But no voice!]

---

### Page 8
The little mermaid walked to the prince's castle. **Every** step **hurt**! It felt like walking on knives!

But she did not cry. She walked and walked.

The prince saw **her**. "Who are you?" he asked.

She could not answer. She had no voice.

[She can't tell him! She has no voice!]

---

### Page 9
The prince **was** kind. He let **her** stay at the castle.

The little mermaid danced **for** him. She smiled. She **was** with him, and that made **her** happy.

But the prince did not know she **was** the one who saved him. He did not know she loved him.

[She's with him! But he doesn't know the truth!]

---

### Page 10
One day, the prince told the little mermaid sad news.

"I must marry a princess from another land," he said. "My **father** says I must."

The little mermaid's heart broke. But still, she could not speak.

She danced at the prince's wedding. Tears filled **her** eyes.

[He's marrying someone else! The mermaid is heartbroken!]

---

### Page 11
That night, the little mermaid sat by the **water**. She **was** very sad.

**Her** **sisters** came up from the sea. "We have a way to save you!" they said.

"Take this knife. You must **hurt** the prince. Then you can be a mermaid again!"

But the little mermaid shook **her** head. She loved the prince. She would **never** **hurt** him.

[Her sisters want to help! But she won't hurt the prince!]

---

### Page 12
The little mermaid dropped the knife into the **water**. She looked at the **stars** one last time.

Then she began to **turn** into sea foam.

But instead of disappearing, she felt light! She **was** floating up, up, up!

Kind spirits of the air caught **her**. "You gave everything **for** love," they said. "You will live **forever** with us."

And the little mermaid became a spirit of the air, watching **over** the sea and the **stars**.

[A bittersweet ending! She becomes a spirit of the air!]

## Back Matter

### Blending Practice
**AR R-Controlled:**  
far, star, hard, shark, garden, dark, barn, farm

**OR R-Controlled:**  
forever, for, storm, torn, horn, corn, sport

**ER R-Controlled:**  
mermaid, her, fern, sister, water, under, over, never, father

**IR R-Controlled:**  
first, third, bird, girl, swirl, twirl, shirt

**UR R-Controlled:**  
turn, hurt, burn, purr, fur, turtle, purple

**Other Decodable Words:**  
tail, sea, swim, ship, save, sand, legs, walk, dance, smile, kind

### Repeated Phrases to Practice
**"Far under the sea"** | **"Forever"** | **"Never hurt"** | **"Her sisters"**

### Talk About the Story
1. Where did the little mermaid live? (Far under the sea with her sisters)
2. Who did she save? (The prince!)
3. What did she give the sea witch? (Her voice)
4. What did she get in return? (Legs to walk on land)
5. Why didn't she hurt the prince? (She loved him too much)
6. What happened at the end? (She became a spirit of the air)

### Extension Activities
- **R-Controlled Review:** Sort all r-controlled words by pattern (AR, OR, ER, IR, UR)
- **Act It Out:** Pretend to swim like a mermaid! Then walk on painful legs!
- **Draw & Write:** Draw the little mermaid as a mermaid and as a spirit of the air.
- **Billy's Connection:** Billy the Goat knows that love means putting others first. The mermaid gave everything for love!
