## For the Teacher or Parent

**Teacher/Parent Note:**  
This is the FULL version of The Little Red Hen! Perfect for practicing **reading with EXPRESSION**! The repetitive dialogue ("Not I!" vs. "Then I will!") gives students lots of chances to practice different emotional tones. **Bold words** show where to use expression. *Underlined words* are sight words.

**Target Lessons:** 76-78  
**Phonics Focus:** Review all patterns from Units 1-2, FLUENCY focus!  
**Key Sight Words:** the, a, said, I, will, not, who, you, then, and, we, all, no, very, some  
**Expression Focus:** Angry voice (Red Hen), lazy voices (animals), proud voice (Red Hen at end)  
**Comprehension Skills:** Work ethic, consequences, fairness, sequence, cause & effect

### Page 1

with a **lazy** cat,  
a **lazy** dog,  
and a **lazy** pig.

The Little Red Hen worked **hard** every day.

But the **other** animals did **nothing**!

[Point to each lazy animal!]

### Page 2

"Look!" she said. "I can **plant** these seeds  
and grow wheat!  
**Then** I can make **bread**!"

"That sounds like a **lot** of work," said the cat.

## Back Matter

### Expression Practice!

**This story is PERFECT for reading with FEELING!**

**Different voices to practice:**

1. **Little Red Hen (asking for help):** Hopeful, friendly  
   *"Who will help me plant the wheat?"*

2. **Lazy animals:** Bored, lazy, don't-care voice  
   *"NOT I!"* (yawn, stretch)

3. **Little Red Hen (doing work):** Determined, maybe a little frustrated  
   *"THEN I WILL DO IT MYSELF!"*

4. **Little Red Hen (angry at end):** VERY angry!  
   *"OH NO YOU WON'T!"*

5. **Eager animals:** Suddenly excited!  
   *"I WILL! I WILL!"* (too late!)

**Practice reading the SAME words with DIFFERENT feelings!**

---

### Punctuation Guides Your Voice!

**Notice the punctuation:**

- **!** (exclamation point) = Read with FEELING! Loud! Excited or angry!
- **?** (question mark) = Voice goes UP at the end (asking)
- **.** (period) = Normal voice, statement

**Examples:**
- "Not I**.**" (boring, flat)
- "Not I**!**" (stronger, more definite)
- "Who will help me**?**" (voice goes up‚Äîasking!)

---

### Repeated Phrases to Practice

"**Who will help me** _____?"  
"**NOT I!**" (said three times by three animals!)  
"**THEN I WILL DO IT MYSELF!**"  
"**I WILL!**" (eager animals)  
"**OH NO YOU WON'T!**"

**Challenge:** Can you say "NOT I!" in a LAZY voice?  
Can you say "I WILL!" in an EAGER voice?

---

### Talk About the Story

1. Who lived on the farm with the Little Red Hen?
2. What did she find? (grains of wheat)
3. How many times did she ask for help? (6 times!)
4. Did anyone help her? (NO!)
5. What did she have to say every time? ("Then I will do it myself!")
6. What did the lazy animals want at the end? (to eat the bread!)
7. Did the Little Red Hen share? (NO!)
8. Was that fair? (YES! She did all the work!)
9. What lesson did the animals learn?

#### **Sequence All the Steps**

1. Found wheat seeds
2. **Planted** seeds
3. **Watered** and weeded
4. **Cut** the wheat
5. **Threshed** the wheat
6. **Carried** to mill
7. **Ground** into flour
8. **Mixed** dough
9. **Kneaded** dough
10. **Shaped** loaf
11. **Baked** bread
12. **Ate** it all herself!

**That's 12 steps! SO MUCH WORK!**

#### **Cause & Effect**

| CAUSE | EFFECT |
|-------|--------|
| Animals didn't help plant | Hen planted alone |
| Animals didn't help cut | Hen cut alone |
| Animals didn't help with ANYTHING | Hen did EVERYTHING alone |
| Bread smelled good | Animals came running |
| Animals wanted to eat | Hen said NO! |
| Animals didn't work | Animals didn't eat |

**Big Lesson:** Your CHOICES have CONSEQUENCES!

---

#### **Fairness Discussion**

**Was the Little Red Hen MEAN or FAIR?**

**Arguments for FAIR:**
- She did ALL the work
- They had MANY chances to help
- If you don't work, you shouldn't get the reward

**Could argue MEAN:**
- She could have shared a little bit
- Friends should share

**Most people say:** **FAIR!** She earned that bread!

**Question:** What would YOU have done?
