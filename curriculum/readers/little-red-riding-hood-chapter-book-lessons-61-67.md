# Little Red Riding Hood
## A Chapter Book for Lessons 61-67
### A Public Domain Retelling For Beginning Readers

---

## For the Teacher or Parent

**Teacher/Parent Note:**  
This is the FIRST CHAPTER BOOK students will read! Little Red Riding Hood is perfect for practicing **soft C/G, silent letters, compound words, contractions, and more!** Reading a "real chapter book" is a HUGE milestone! **Bold words** are target pattern words. *Underlined words* are sight words to practice.

**Target Lessons:** 61-67  
**Phonics Focus:** Soft C (nice, place, face, slice, dance, voice, twice), Soft G (village, gentle, giant, age, stage), CK (back, basket, track, knock, quick, stick, black, trick), TCH/DGE (catch, watch, match, patch, edge, hedge, bridge), Silent letters (knock, knife, know, knight, wrong, write, wrap), PH (phonics review), WH (what, when, where, white, why), Compound words (grandmother, woodcutter, someone, outside, inside, pathway), Contractions (don't, can't, won't, I'll, she's, he's, didn't, couldn't, shouldn't), Plurals & verb endings review  
**Key Sight Words:** the, a, was, were, said, I, you, she, he, they, will, would, could, should, all, some, very, little, through, once, upon, time  
**Decodable Words:** (see pattern lists above‚ÄîTONS of target words!)  
**Comprehension Skills:** Chapter structure, predictions, character motivation, problem/solution, sequencing, story climax, safety lessons

## How to Use This Book:

1. **Read one chapter per day** (or session)‚Äîit's a REAL chapter book!
2. **Use different voices:** Soft voice for Red, gruff voice for Wolf, old voice for Grandma
3. **Pause between chapters** to predict what happens next!
4. **Celebrate finishing:** This is a BIG accomplishment!

## Chapter 1: The Journey Begins

### Page 1
**Once** upon a time, there was a **little** girl  
who lived in a **village** near the woods.

Everyone called her **Little** Red Riding Hood  
because she always **wore** a red cape with a hood.

Her **grandmother** **made** it for her,  
and it was her favorite thing to **wear**.

[Point to your hood! Little Red Riding Hood!]

---

### Page 2
One day, **Little** Red's mother said,

"Your **grandmother** is sick.  
Can you take this **basket** of food to her?  
She lives on the other side of the woods."

"Yes, Mother!" said **Little** Red.  
"I will go right away!"

Mother packed the **basket** with:  
- **Bread**
- **Butter**  
- A **slice** of **cake**
- Some fresh **eggs**

[Pack the basket! Bread, butter, cake, eggs!]

---

### Page 3
"Now listen," said Mother.

"Stay on the **pathway**.  
**Don't** talk to strangers.  
Go straight to **Grandmother's** house.  
**Don't** stop in the woods!"

"I **won't**, Mother!" said **Little** Red.  
"I **promise**!"

She put on her red hood,  
picked up the **basket**,  
and headed into the woods.

[Wave goodbye! Off to Grandma's house!]

---

### Page 4
The woods were beautiful.

**Little** Red walked along the **pathway**.

She saw:  
- Birds singing in the trees  
- Flowers growing by the **edge** of the path  
- Butterflies dancing in the air

"What a **nice** day!" said **Little** Red.

She hummed a **little** song as she walked.

[Hum a happy tune! La la la!]

---

### Page 5
Suddenly, a **big**, **gray** wolf stepped onto the **pathway**!

"Good morning, **little** girl," said the wolf  
in a **gentle** **voice**.

**Little** Red stopped.

She **knew** she should **not** talk to strangers.

But the wolf seemed **nice**...

**Wrong** choice, **Little** Red!

[Wolf appears! Use a sneaky voice!]

---

### Page 6
"**Where** are you going?" asked the wolf.

"To my **grandmother's** house," said **Little** Red.  
"She lives on the other side of the woods.  
She is sick, so I am bringing her food."

"How **nice** of you!" said the wolf.

But inside, he was thinking:  
*"Mmm! A **little** girl AND her **grandmother**!  
I **could** eat them both!"*

[Wolf is thinking sneaky thoughts!]

---

### Page 7
"**Why** **don't** you pick some flowers for your **grandmother**?"  
said the wolf.

"Look! There are beautiful flowers over there  
by that **hedge**!"

"Oh! Good idea!" said **Little** Red.

She left the **pathway**  
and went to pick flowers.

**Wrong** again, **Little** Red!

[Pick flowers! But uh oh... wrong choice!]

---

### Page 8
As soon as **Little** Red left the **pathway**,  
the wolf ran ahead!

He **knew** a **quick** **shortcut** through the woods.

"I will get to **Grandmother's** house first!" he said.  
"**Then** I will eat **Grandmother**,  
and **when** **Little** Red arrives,  
I will eat her too!"

The wolf laughed his wicked laugh.

**Chapter 1 ends here!**  
**What will happen next?**

[Wolf runs ahead! Oh no!]

---

## Chapter 2: At Grandmother's House

### Page 9
The wolf arrived at **Grandmother's** little house.

**Knock, knock, knock!**

(Remember: **knock** has a **silent K**!)

"**Who** is there?" called **Grandmother** from **inside**.

"It's me, **Little** Red!" said the wolf  
in a high, fake **voice**.

(He was trying to **trick** **Grandmother**!)

[Knock! Knock! Knock! Use a high fake voice!]

---

### Page 10
"The door is unlocked, dear," said **Grandmother**.  
"Come **inside**!"

The wolf opened the door and went **inside**.

**Poor Grandmother**!

She was lying in bed.  
She was old and weak.

When she saw the wolf,  
she tried to scream‚Äî

But it was too late!

[Gasp! The wolf is inside!]

---

### Page 11
The wolf gobbled up **Grandmother**  
in one big gulp!

**(Don't worry‚Äîin the real story, she's OK later!)**

**Then** the wolf put on **Grandmother's** nightgown.

He put on her nightcap.

He put on her **glasses**.

He climbed into her bed  
and pulled up the covers.

"Now I will wait for **Little** Red!" he said.

[Wolf dresses up like Grandma! Sneaky!]

---

### Page 12
Meanwhile, **Little** Red was still picking flowers.

She picked:  
- **White** daisies  
- Yellow buttercups  
- Purple **violets**

She **made** a beautiful **bouquet**.

"**Grandmother** will love these!" she said.

**Then** she remembered:  
"Oh! I should hurry!  
Mother said **not** to stop in the woods!"

She ran back to the **pathway**  
and hurried to **Grandmother's** house.

[Pick flowers! Run back to the path!]

---

### Page 13
At last, **Little** Red arrived at the little house.

**Knock, knock, knock!**

"**Who** is there?" called a **voice** from **inside**.

(It was the wolf's **voice**,  
but **Little** Red **didn't** know that!)

"It's me, **Little** Red!" she said.

"Come **inside**, dear!" said the **voice**.

**Little** Red opened the door and went in.

[Knock knock! Go inside!]

---

### Page 14
The room was dark.

**Little** Red walked to the bed.

She could see someone under the covers.

"Hello, **Grandmother**!" said **Little** Red.  
"I brought you food and flowers!"

"Thank you, dear," said the wolf  
in his fake old-woman **voice**.  
"Come closer so I can see you!"

**Little** Red came closer.

**Chapter 2 ends here!**  
**What will Little Red notice?**

[Come closer to the bed... closer...]

---

## Chapter 3: Something Is Wrong!

### Page 15
**Little** Red looked at "**Grandmother**."

Something was **wrong**.

"**Grandmother**," she said,  
"**What** big ears you have!"

"The **better** to hear you with, my dear!"  
said the wolf.

**Little** Red came a **little** closer.

[Look carefully! Something's not right!]

---

### Page 16
"**Grandmother**," said **Little** Red,  
"**What** big **eyes** you have!"

"The **better** to see you with, my dear!"  
said the wolf.

**Little** Red felt nervous.

She took a step back.

[Step back! This is weird!]

---

### Page 17
"**Grandmother**," said **Little** Red,  
"**What** big **teeth** you have!"

The wolf **grinned** a big, **wicked** grin.

"The **better** to EAT you with!"  
he **roared**!

And he **jumped** out of bed!

**Little** Red **screamed**!

"HELP! HELP! It's a WOLF!"

[ROAR! Jump! SCREAM! Help!]

---

### Page 18
The wolf **chased** **Little** Red around the room!

She ran behind the table!  
She ran behind the chair!

"Help! **Someone** help me!" she cried.

The wolf was **fast**!

He was getting closer and closer!

**Little** Red was in terrible danger!

**Chapter 3 ends here!**  
**Will someone save her?**

[Run! Chase around the room!]

---

## Chapter 4: The Rescue!

### Page 19
Just **then**‚Äî

A **woodcutter** was walking by **outside**!

(A **woodcutter** is someone **who** cuts down trees.  
**Woodcutter** is a **compound word**:  
wood + cutter = **woodcutter**!)

He heard **Little** Red **screaming**!

"That sounds like trouble!" he said.

He ran to the house with his **axe**!

[Woodcutter runs! To the rescue!]

---

### Page 20
The **woodcutter** burst through the door!

He saw the wolf **chasing** **Little** Red!

"Stop right there, wolf!" he **shouted**.

The wolf turned around.

When he saw the **big** **woodcutter** with the **big** **axe**,  
the wolf got **scared**!

"Uh oh!" said the wolf.

[Woodcutter bursts in! Stop, wolf!]

---

### Page 21
The wolf tried to run away!

But the **woodcutter** was **quick**!

He **caught** the wolf!

"Spit out **Grandmother**, you **wicked** wolf!"  
said the **woodcutter**.

The wolf had to spit out **Grandmother**.

She was OK! (A **little** wet, but OK!)

[Spit her out! She's OK!]

---

### Page 22
The **woodcutter** **chased** the wolf out of the house.

"Get out of here!" he **shouted**.  
"And **don't** come back!"

The wolf ran into the woods as **fast** as he could.

He **never** came back.

**Little** Red and **Grandmother** were **safe**!

[Wolf runs away! Don't come back!]

---

### Page 23
"Thank you for saving us!" said **Little** Red.

"You are very **brave**!" said **Grandmother**.

"I am just glad you are both **safe**!" said the **woodcutter**.

**Grandmother** got out of bed.

**Little** Red unpacked the **basket** of food.

They all sat down and had a **nice** meal together.

[Sit down! Eat together! Safe and happy!]

---

### Page 24
After the meal, the **woodcutter** walked **Little** Red **home**.

When they arrived, Mother was so happy to see her!

"Did you stay on the **pathway**?" asked Mother.

**Little** Red hung her head.

"No, Mother," she said. "I **didn't**.  
I talked to the wolf.  
I left the **pathway** to pick flowers.  
I'm sorry. I **shouldn't** have done that."

[Hang your head. I made mistakes.]

---

### Page 25
"I'm just glad you're **safe**," said Mother.

"I learned my lesson!" said **Little** Red.

"From now on, I **will**:
- Stay on the **pathway**
- **Not** talk to strangers
- **Listen** to what you say!"

"That's my **smart** girl!" said Mother.

And **Little** Red **never** went into the woods alone again.

**The lesson:**

**Listen** to good advice!  
Stay **safe**!  
**Don't** talk to strangers!

**THE END!**

[Smile! Happy, safe ending!]

#### **Safety Lessons**

This story teaches IMPORTANT safety rules:

1. **Stay on the path** (don't wander off)
2. **Don't talk to strangers** (even if they seem nice!)
3. **Listen to your parents** (they keep you safe!)
4. **Trust your instincts** (if something feels wrong, it IS wrong!)

**Discussion:** How can YOU stay safe?

---

#### **Mistake Analysis**

**Little Red's Mistakes:**
1. Talked to the wolf (stranger!)
2. Told him where she was going
3. Left the path to pick flowers
4. Didn't recognize the wolf in disguise right away

**What she SHOULD have done:**
1. Not talk to the wolf
2. Stay on the path
3. Go straight to Grandma's
4. Run away when she first noticed something wrong

**Lesson:** Mistakes can be dangerous! Listen to good advice!
