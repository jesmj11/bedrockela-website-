# The Magic Fish
## A Beginning Reader for Lessons 34-36
### A Folktale Retelling For Beginning Readers

---

## For the Teacher or Parent

**Teacher/Parent Note:**  
This retelling of The Magic Fish (also known as The Fisherman and His Wife) uses **Magic E patterns** with a_e and i_e words. The repeated phrase "Fish, fish, in the sea..." encourages fluency. **Bold words** feature magic e patterns. *Underlined words* are sight words. The story teaches gratitude and contentment.

**Target Lessons:** 34-36  
**Phonics Focus:** Magic E (a_e, i_e), short vowels, consonant teams  
**Key Sight Words:** the, a, said, was, of, they  
**Decodable Words:** fish, wish, wife, life, made, wave, cave, fine, time, nine, five, gave  
**Comprehension Skills:** Character motivation, cause and effect, moral lesson

---

## How to Use This Book:

1. **First Read:** Read aloud. Use different voices for the fish and the wife!
2. **Second Read:** Have the child say the magic phrase with you.
3. **Third Read:** Child reads magic e words (underlined or in bold).
4. **Practice:** Review magic e rule: Silent e makes the vowel say its name!

---

## The Story

### Page 1
Once upon a *time*, a man lived in a small hut by the sea. He had a **wife**. They had very little.

Each day, the man would fish. He would **make** a catch and they would eat. Their **life** was simple and quiet.

[Point to each magic e word. What makes the vowel say its name?]

---

### Page 2
One day, the man felt a tug on his line. He pulled and pulled. Up came a big **fish**!

But this was not just any fish. It could talk!

"Please, let me go!" said the **fish**. "I am a **magic** fish. I can grant a **wish**!"

[Use a special voice for the magic fish!]

---

### Page 3
The man was kind. "I do not need a **wish**," he said. "You may go."

He let the **fish** swim away. **Waves** splashed as it dove back into the sea.

The man went home. He told his **wife** the story.

[Act out releasing the fish. Splash your hands like waves!]

---

### Page 4
"What?" cried his **wife**. "You let it go? You did not **make** a **wish**?"

"We are **fine**," said the man. "We have all we need."

"Go back!" said his **wife**. "Ask for a nice home. This hut is too small!"

[Use an upset voice for the wife!]

---

### Page 5
The man went back to the sea. He called:

**"Fish, fish, in the sea,
My wife made a wish for me!"**

The **fish** came up from the **waves**.

"What does she wish?" asked the **fish**.

"A nice home," said the man.

[Say the magic rhyme together!]

---

### Page 6
"Go back. You will see," said the **fish**.

The man went home. The little hut was gone! In its **place** stood a fine home with a **gate** and a **path**.

His **wife** was happy. For a while.

[What happened? The fish used its magic!]

---

### Page 7
But soon, the **wife** wanted more.

"Go back to the **fish**!" she said. "Ask for a **palace**! I want to be queen!"

The man did not want to. But he went to the sea. He called:

**"Fish, fish, in the sea,
My wife made a wish for me!"**

[How does the man feel? Is he happy about this?]

---

### Page 8
The **fish** came up. "What does she wish now?"

"She wants to be queen," said the man. "She wants a **palace**."

"Go back. You will see," said the **fish**.

The man went home. A huge **palace** stood in **place** of the home! His **wife** wore a crown and sat on a throne.

[Use your hands to show how BIG the palace is!]

---

### Page 9
But the **wife** still wanted more.

"Go back!" she said. "I want to rule the sun and the moon! I want to tell *them* when to **rise**!"

The man was sad. But he went to the sea one more **time**. **Waves** were tall and dark. The sky was gray.

He called:

**"Fish, fish, in the sea,
My wife made a wish for me!"**

[The weather is changing. What does that mean?]

---

### Page 10
The **fish** came up. The water was wild.

"What does she wish *this* **time**?" asked the **fish**.

"She... she wants to rule the sun and the moon," the man said quietly.

The **fish** was silent. Then it said, "Go back. You will see."

[Do you think the fish will grant this wish?]

---

### Page 11
The man went home. The **palace** was gone! The **fine** home was gone!

There was only the little hut, just as before.

His **wife** sat inside. She had no crown. She had no throne.

They were back where they started.

[What happened? Why did everything disappear?]

---

### Page 12
And that is where they stayed.

The man went fishing each day. They ate what he caught. They had very little, just like before.

But they learned a lesson: Be happy with what you have.

[Did they learn their lesson?]

## Back Matter

### Blending Practice
**Magic E Words (a_e, i_e) from the Story:**  
made, make, wave, gate, place, palace, path, gave  
wife, life, fine, time, nine, five, rise  

**Other Decodable Words:**  
fish, wish, man, big, hut, sit, tug, swim, his, let, got, back, went, just

### Repeated Phrases to Practice
**"Fish, fish, in the sea, my wife made a wish for me!"**

### Talk About the Story
1. What did the man catch? (A magic fish!)
2. What was the first wish? (A nice home)
3. What was the second wish? (A palace, to be queen)
4. What was the last wish? (To rule the sun and the moon)
5. What happened at the end? (They went back to the little hut)
6. What is the lesson? (Be grateful for what you have; don't be greedy)

### Extension Activities
- **Magic E Hunt:** Find all the magic e words. Circle the silent e!
- **Act It Out:** Act out the man calling the fish. Use hand motions for waves!
- **Draw & Write:** Draw the little hut, the nice home, and the palace. Which would you choose?
- **Billy's Connection:** Billy the Goat is happy with his simple life on the mountain. He doesn't need a palace! Talk about what makes you happy.

### Character Education
**Theme:** Gratitude and contentment. The wife wanted more and more, but greed made her lose everything. Be thankful for what you have!
