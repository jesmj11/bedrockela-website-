# The North Wind and the Sun
## A Beginning Reader for Lessons 49-51
### A Public Domain Retelling For Beginning Readers

---

## For the Teacher or Parent

**Teacher/Parent Note:**  
This retelling of The North Wind and the Sun (Aesop's Fable) is PERFECT for introducing **R-controlled vowels** (AR, OR, ER)! It's a short, fun competition story with a clear moral. **Bold words** are R-controlled words. *Underlined words* are sight words to practice.

**Target Lessons:** 49-51  
**Phonics Focus:** R-controlled vowels AR (hard, harder, part, start, far, farmer, march, dark), OR (north, storm, force, more, before, or, short, worn), ER (her, ever, never, better, over, after, under, corner)  
**Key Sight Words:** the, a, was, were, said, I, you, he, who, can, will, are, one, day, some, very, could, would  
**Decodable R-Controlled Words:** north, hard, harder, warm, storm, farmer, force, short, before, better, never, ever, over, after, part, start, or, more, far, under, corner, march, dark, her, worn  
**Comprehension Skills:** Compare/contrast, cause & effect, theme/moral (gentle persuasion vs. force), predictions

---

## How to Use This Book:

1. **First Read:** Read the story aloud. Use a FIERCE voice for North Wind, WARM voice for Sun!
2. **Second Read:** Have the child find all the R-controlled words! AR, OR, ER!
3. **Third Read:** Let the child act out blowing wind and shining warmly!
4. **Practice:** Talk about the lesson: Being kind works better than being mean!

## The Story

### Page 1
One day, the **North** Wind and the Sun had an argument.

"I am stronger than you!" said the **North** Wind.

"No, I am stronger!" said the Sun.

"Let's have a contest!" said the **North** Wind.

"Whoever can make that **farmer** take off his coat  
will win!"

"Deal!" said the Sun.

[Point! There's the farmer!]

---

### Page 2
The **farmer** was walking down the road.

He **wore** a thick **warm** coat.

"Watch me!" said the **North** Wind.  
"I will make him take off his coat!"

The **North** Wind began to blow.

**WHOOOOSH!**

[Blow like the wind! WHOOOOSH!]

---

### Page 3
The **North** Wind blew **hard**.

**WHOOOOSH! WHOOOOSH!**

But the **farmer** just pulled his coat tighter.

"It's cold!" said the **farmer**.  
"I need my coat!"

The **North** Wind blew **harder**!

**WHOOOOOOOOSH!**

[Blow HARDER! Whoooosh!]

---

### Page 4
The **North** Wind blew and blew.

He blew so **hard** that a **storm** came!

**Dark** clouds filled the sky.

The **farmer** held his coat even tighter.

"This **storm** is terrible!" said the **farmer**.  
"I must keep my coat on  
**or** I will freeze!"

The **North** Wind blew until he was tired.

[Huff and puff! So tired!]

---

### Page 5
At last, the **North** Wind stopped.

He was tired.

"I could **not** make him take off his coat!" he said.

"You used too much **force**," said the Sun.  
"Now it is my turn.  
Watch me!"

[Sun smiles! My turn now!]

---

### Page 6
The Sun began to shine.

**Gently**.  
**Warmly**.

The **farmer** looked up at the sky.

"Oh!" he said.  
"The **storm** is **over**!  
The Sun is out!  
It feels **warm**!"

[Shine gently! Feel the warm sun!]

---

### Page 7
The Sun shone a little **more**.

It got **warmer** and **warmer**.

"It's getting hot!" said the **farmer**.

He unbuttoned his coat.

But he did **not** take it off yet.

[Unbutton the coat! Getting warm!]

---

### Page 8
The Sun shone even **more**.

Now it was very **warm**!

"I'm so hot!" said the **farmer**.

He took off his coat!

"That's **better**!" he said.

He put his coat **over** his **arm**  
and kept walking.

[Take off the coat! Ahhhh! So much better!]

---

### Page 9
The Sun smiled.

"I won!" said the Sun.

The **North** Wind was quiet.

"You see," said the Sun,  
"**Warm** kindness works **better**  
than cold **force**.

Being gentle is **stronger**  
than being **hard**!"

**The lesson:**

Gentleness and kindness  
work **better** than **force**!

**The end!**

[Sun wins! Gentle is better than harsh!]

## Back Matter

### R-Controlled Vowel Practice

**AR words:** **ar**m, **far**mer, h**ar**d, h**ar**der, w**ar**m, w**ar**mer, m**ar**ch, d**ar**k, st**ar**t, p**ar**t  

**OR words:** N**or**th, **or**, st**or**m, sh**or**t, f**or**ce, bef**or**e, m**or**e, c**or**ner, w**or**e, w**or**n  

**ER words:** **ov**er, **aft**er, **und**er, **nev**er, **ev**er, **bett**er, h**er**  

**"Bossy R" Rule:** When a vowel is followed by R, the R changes the sound!
- AR says /ar/ like in "car"
- OR says /or/ like in "for"
- ER says /er/ like in "her"

**AR Practice Sentence:**  
"The **far**mer walked **far** from the b**ar**n in the **dar**k with h**ar**d shoes on!"

**OR Practice Sentence:**  
"The N**or**th wind blew bef**or**e the st**or**m with f**or**ce **or** m**or**e!"

**ER Practice Sentence:**  
"The Sun is **bett**er than **ev**er when it shines **ov**er h**er** aft**er** the st**or**m!"

### Talk About the Story

1. Who had an argument? (the North Wind and the Sun)
2. What were they arguing about? (who was stronger)
3. What was the contest? (make the farmer take off his coat)
4. What did the North Wind do? (blew hard, made a storm)
5. Did it work? (NO! The farmer held his coat tighter!)
6. What did the Sun do? (shone gently and warmly)
7. Did it work? (YES! The farmer took off his coat!)
8. What is the lesson?

### Extension Activities

#### **Compare & Contrast**

| NORTH WIND | SUN |
|------------|-----|
| Blew HARD | Shone GENTLY |
| Used FORCE | Used WARMTH |
| Made a STORM | Made it WARM |
| Farmer held coat TIGHTER | Farmer took coat OFF |
| FAILED | WON! |

**Question:** Why did the Sun WIN?

**Answer:** Gentle kindness works better than harsh force!

---

#### **Cause & Effect**

| CAUSE | EFFECT |
|-------|--------|
| North Wind blew hard | Farmer held coat tighter |
| North Wind made storm | Farmer needed coat more |
| Sun shone gently | Farmer felt warm |
| Sun shone more | It got warmer |
| It got very warm | Farmer took off coat! |

**Lesson:** How you DO something matters!

---

#### **Theme: Kindness vs. Force**

**FORCE approach (North Wind):**
- Harsh, mean
- Blowing, storming
- Makes people resist

**KINDNESS approach (Sun):**
- Gentle, warm
- Shining, warming
- Makes people cooperate

**Real-life examples:**
- Parent yelling vs. parent talking kindly
- Bully forcing vs. friend asking nicely
- Teacher being mean vs. teacher being patient

**Question:** Which works BETTER? (Kindness!)

---

#### **Act It Out**
- **North Wind:** Blow hard! WHOOOOSH! Make wind sounds! Wave arms!
- **Farmer:** Hold coat tight! It's cold! Brrr!
- **Sun:** Smile! Shine gently! Spread arms like rays!
- **Farmer:** Get warm! Unbutton coat! Take it off! Ahhhh!

Take turns being each character!

#### **Science Connection: Weather**

**North Wind brings:**
- COLD air
- STORMS
- Dark clouds
- Wind

**Sun brings:**
- WARMTH
- Light
- Blue sky
- Nice weather

**Question:** Which weather do YOU like better?

---

#### **Persuasion Discussion**

**What is PERSUASION?** Getting someone to do what you want

**Two ways to persuade:**
1. **Force:** Making them do it (North Wind)
2. **Kindness:** Convincing them gently (Sun)

**Which is BETTER?** Kindness!

**Questions:**
- Has anyone ever FORCED you to do something? How did it feel?
- Has anyone ever been NICE and you WANTED to help? How did that feel?
- Which way do YOU want to treat people?

#### **Compare to Other Fables**

- **Lion & Mouse:** Kindness pays off (mouse saved lion)
- **North Wind & Sun:** Kindness WINS (Sun won contest)
- **Boy Who Cried Wolf:** Being mean (lying) = bad result
- **North Wind & Sun:** Being mean (force) = bad result
- **Pattern:** KINDNESS = GOOD! MEANNESS = BAD!

**Play weather symphony:** Everyone makes different weather sounds!

#### **Billy's Connection**

Billy the Goat says: "When learning to read gets HARD, don't be harsh on yourself like the North Wind! Be WARM and gentle like the Sun! If you're kind to yourself and keep trying GENTLY, you'll learn BETTER than if you get mad and frustrated! Gentle practice works better than forcing it!"

Also: The Sun won by being SMART, not by being HARSH. In reading, work SMARTER, not HARDER! Use tricks like R-controlled vowels to make reading EASIER!
