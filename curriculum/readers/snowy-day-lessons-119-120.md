# The Snowy Day
## Lessons 119-120 - Time Words & Feelings

**One winter morning**, Peter woke up. Snow had fallen! He put on his snowsuit and went **outside**.

**First**, he made tracks **with** his feet. **Then** he dragged a stick. **After that**, he made angels.

He packed a snowball **for later** and put it in his pocket.

**At lunchtime**, he went **inside**. **While** he ate, he told his mother about his day.

**In the afternoon**, he went back **outside**. He climbed a snowy mountain and slid down!

**Before dinner**, he came **inside**. He remembered the snowball! But it had melted‚Äîhis pocket was wet!

**That night** **before** bed, he felt sad about the snowball. But he was happy about tomorrow's snow.

**The next morning**, new snow had fallen! Peter called his friend. **Soon** they were playing together!

**Time Words:**
- **Morning**, **afternoon**, **night**
- **Before**, **after**, **while**, **during**
- **First**, **then**, **next**, **finally**
- **Soon**, **later**, **tomorrow**

**Feeling Words:**
- **Excited** (seeing snow!)
- **Happy** (playing!)
- **Sad** (snowball melted)
- **Hopeful** (tomorrow's snow!)
- **Joyful** (playing with friend!)

**Theme:** Simple joys of childhood! Finding wonder in everyday things!

**Historical Note:** First picture book with African-American protagonist (1962)! Important milestone!
