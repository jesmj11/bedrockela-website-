# Stone Soup
## A Beginning Reader for Lessons 34-39
### A Public Domain Retelling For Beginning Readers

---

## For the Teacher or Parent

**Teacher/Parent Note:**  
This retelling of Stone Soup is PERFECT for practicing **Magic E (CVCe) patterns**! Every vowel gets the Magic E treatment: a_e, i_e, o_e, u_e, e_e. The story teaches cooperation and sharing. **Bold words** are Magic E words. *Underlined words* are sight words to practice.

**Target Lessons:** 34-39  
**Phonics Focus:** Magic E / CVCe patterns (make/made, time/times, bone/bones, use/used, take/gave/came/place/name/same/taste/safe/hope/home/stone/alone/those/close/cute/huge)  
**Key Sight Words:** the, a, was, were, said, I, we, you, they, have, come, will, what, some, into, could, would  
**Decodable Magic E Words:** stone, made, came, gave, take, taste, place, same, name, safe, hope, home, bone, those, close, alone, rude, huge, cute, use, time  
**Comprehension Skills:** Cooperation, sharing, community, sequence (steps in making soup), cause & effect, predictions

---

## How to Use This Book:

1. **First Read:** Read the story aloud. Use different voices for each villager!
2. **Second Read:** Have the child find all the Magic E words! Circle them or point them out!
3. **Third Read:** Let the child read the Magic E words when you pause!
4. **Practice:** Act out making soup! Each person brings an ingredient!

## The Story

### Page 1
Three travelers **came** to a small village.

They were tired.  
They were hungry.

"We need a **place** to rest," they said.  
"We need some food to eat."

But the village people were **not** kind.

[Walk like tired travelers! So tired!]

---

### Page 2
The travelers went to the first **home**.

**Knock, knock, knock!**

"Can you give us some food?" they asked.

"**No!**" said the woman.  
"I have **none** to spare!  
Go away!"

And she shut the door.

[Knock on the door! Knock, knock, knock!]

---

### Page 3
The travelers went to another **home**.

**Knock, knock, knock!**

"Can you share some food?" they asked.

"**No!**" said the man.  
"I am too poor!  
Go away!"

And he shut the door.

[Shake your head! No, no, no!]

---

### Page 4
The travelers tried every **home** in the village.

But **no one** would help.

"These people are **rude**," said one traveler.

"They will **not** share," said another.

"But we can **make** them share!" said the third traveler.

"How?" asked the others.

[Look curious! How?]

---

### Page 5
"We will **make** soup from a **stone**!" he said.

"A **stone**?" said the others.  
"You can't **make** soup from a **stone**!"

"Just wait and see!" he said.

And he picked up a **huge** **stone** from the ground.

[Pick up a pretend huge stone!]

---

### Page 6
The travelers went to the village square.

They **made** a big fire.

They **placed** a **huge** pot over the fire.

They filled it with water.

**Then** they dropped the **stone** into the pot.

**SPLASH!**

All the villagers **came** to watch.

[Make a splashing sound! SPLASH!]

---

### Page 7
"What are you doing?" asked a villager.

"We are making **stone** soup!" said the travelers.

"**Stone** soup?" said the villagers.  
"That sounds strange!"

"Oh, it is the **best** soup!" said the travelers.  
"**Stone** soup is amazing!"

The villagers **came** closer to see.

[Look amazed! Stone soup?!]

---

### Page 8
One traveler tasted the soup.

"**Mmmm**!" he said.  
"This **stone** soup will **taste** so good!  
But it would **taste** even **better**  
with just a little onion..."

A villager said, "I have an onion at **home**!  
I will go get it!"

And she ran **home** and **came** back with an onion.

**PLOP!** Into the soup it went!

[Run to get the onion! PLOP it in!]

---

### Page 9
Another traveler tasted the soup.

"**Mmmm**!" he said.  
"This is getting good!  
But it would **taste** even **better**  
with a carrot..."

"I have a carrot!" said a villager.

He ran **home** and **came** back with a **huge** carrot.

**PLOP!** Into the soup it went!

[Get the carrot! PLOP!]

---

### Page 10
The third traveler tasted the soup.

"**Mmmm**!" he said.  
"**Nice**!  
But it would **taste** even **better**  
with a potato..."

"I have potatoes!" said a woman.

She ran **home** and **came** back with **five** potatoes.

**PLOP, PLOP, PLOP, PLOP, PLOP!**

[Count five! PLOP them all in!]

---

### Page 11
"You know what else would **make** this soup amazing?" said a traveler.  
"A **bone** with some meat on it!"

"I have a **bone**!" said the butcher.

He ran **home** and **came** back with a **huge** **bone**.

**PLOP!** Into the soup it went!

[Pretend to carry a huge bone!]

---

### Page 12
Soon, **everyone** was bringing things!

**Cabbage!** **PLOP!**  
**Beans!** **PLOP!**  
**Tomatoes!** **PLOP!**  
**Celery!** **PLOP!**  
**Spices!** **PLOP!**

The pot was **full** of good things!

The soup began to **smell** amazing!

[Sniff! Mmmmm! Smells so good!]

---

### Page 13
The travelers stirred the soup.

"**Almost** ready!" they said.

The villagers set up tables.  
They brought **plates** and **spoons**.  
They brought **bread** and **cake**.

"We will **all** eat together!" they said.

[Stir the soup! Round and round!]

---

### Page 14
At last, the soup was done!

The travelers **gave** each villager a big bowl of soup.

Everyone tasted it.

"**This** is the **best** soup I ever tasted!" said one.

"**Amazing**!" said another.

"I can't believe we **made** this from a **stone**!" said a third.

[Pretend to taste! Mmmm! So good!]

---

### Page 15
The villagers and the travelers ate together.

They talked.  
They laughed.  
They shared stories.

"Thank you for teaching us to **make** **stone** soup!" said the villagers.

The travelers smiled.

[Smile! Everyone is happy!]

---

### Page 16
The next morning, the travelers got ready to leave.

The villagers **gave** them food for their trip.  
They **gave** them warm blankets.  
They **gave** them kind words.

"Come back any **time**!" said the villagers.

"We will!" said the travelers.

And off they went.

[Wave goodbye! Come back soon!]

---

### Page 17
After the travelers left, one villager said,

"You know, I don't think it was really the **stone**  
that **made** the soup so good..."

"I think it was **all** of **us** working together!" said another.

"And **sharing** what we **have**!" said a third.

They all smiled.

**The lesson:**

When we **share** and work together,  
amazing things can happen!

**The end!**

[Everyone smile! We learned something!]

### Magic E Practice: ALL VOWELS!

**A_E words:** **m**a**de**, **c**a**me**, **g**a**ve**, **t**a**ke**, **t**a**ste**, **pl**a**ce**, **s**a**me**, **n**a**me**, **s**a**fe**, **pl**a**tes**  
**I_E words:** **n**i**ce**, **t**i**me**  
**O_E words:** **st**o**ne**, **h**o**me**, **b**o**ne**, **th**o**se**, **cl**o**se**, **al**o**ne**  
**U_E words:** **r**u**de**, **h**u**ge**, **c**u**te**, **u**se**  
**E_E words:** (th**e**se - silent e at end doesn't make long e, but good to notice!)

**Magic E Rule:** When a word has a vowel, then a consonant, then an E at the end, the E is SILENT and makes the vowel say its NAME!

**Examples:**
- **can** â†’ **cane** (short a â†’ long a)
- **hop** â†’ **hope** (short o â†’ long o)
- **cut** â†’ **cute** (short u â†’ long u)

### Talk About the Story

1. Who came to the village? (three travelers)
2. What did they need? (food and a place to rest)
3. Did the villagers help at first? (NO! They said no!)
4. What did the travelers say they would make? (stone soup!)
5. What did the villagers bring? (onion, carrot, potatoes, bone, cabbage, beans, tomatoes, spices...)
6. Was the soup really made from a stone? (No! It was made from all the things people shared!)
7. What did the villagers learn?
8. What is the lesson of this story?

## Additional Teaching Notes

### Why This Story is Perfect for Lessons 34-39:

1. **TONS of Magic E words:** Every vowel pattern represented
2. **Cooperation theme:** Beautiful lesson about working together
3. **Repetitive structure:** Each ingredient = same pattern (builds confidence)
4. **Community focus:** Relatable to kids' lives (sharing, helping)
5. **Cumulative:** Like Enormous Turnip and Bremen Musiciansâ€”adds people one by one
6. **Satisfying ending:** Everyone happy, lesson learned
7. **Culturally universal:** Versions exist in many cultures

### Cross-Curricular Connections:

- **Social Studies:** Community, cooperation, sharing
- **Math:** Counting ingredients, addition (how many vegetables total?)
- **Science:** Cooking, how heat changes food, nutrition
- **Character Education:** Generosity, cooperation, overcoming selfishness
- **Cooking:** Can actually MAKE stone soup as a class/family project!

### Vocabulary to Teach:

- **traveler** (someone who goes from place to place)
- **village** (small town)
- **rude** (not polite, not kind)
- **share** (give some of what you have to others)
- **cooperation** (working together)
- **community** (group of people living/working together)
- **generous** (willing to give and share)
