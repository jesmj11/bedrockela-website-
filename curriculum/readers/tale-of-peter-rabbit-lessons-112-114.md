# The Tale of Peter Rabbit
## Lessons 112-114 - Action & Describing Words

Peter Rabbit was **naughty**. His mother warned, "Don't go into Mr. McGregor's garden!"

But Peter **squeezed** under the fence and **hopped** into the garden!

He **munched** lettuce, **chomped** radishes, and **gobbled** beans. Then he felt **sick**.

Mr. McGregor **spotted** him! "Stop, thief!" he **roared**, **chasing** Peter!

Peter **ran**, **frightened**. He **dashed** past the tool shed and **darted** around the pond!

His jacket **caught** on a fence! Mr. McGregor almost **grabbed** him!

Peter **wriggled** free and **raced** toward home! He **dove** under the fenceâ€”safe!

At home, his **good** sisters ate **delicious** bread and blackberries. **Exhausted** Peter got chamomile tea and went to bed.

**Action Words (Verbs):**
- Movement: hopped, squeezed, ran, dashed, darted, raced, dove, wriggled
- Eating: munched, chomped, gobbled
- Other: spotted, roared, caught, grabbed

**Describing Words (Adjectives):**
- **Naughty** vs. **good** (opposites!)
- **Frightened**, **scared**, **terrified** (shades of meaningâ€”all mean scared, but terrified is MOST scared!)
- **Sick**, **exhausted**, **tired**
- **Delicious**, **tasty**, **yummy**

**Lesson:** Listen to your mother! Disobedience leads to trouble!
