# The Three Bears Go Camping
## A Beginning Reader for Lessons 71-73
### An Original Sequel For Beginning Readers

---

## For the Teacher or Parent

**Teacher/Parent Note:**  
This original sequel brings back the beloved three bears! It focuses on **verb endings** (-ing, -ed) and **comparatives** (-er, -est). Read across lessons 71-73 as students practice these word endings. **Bold words** feature target patterns. Theme: Family fun and nature!

**Target Lessons:** 71-73  
**Phonics Focus:** -ING, -ED endings, -ER/-EST comparatives  
**Key Sight Words:** the, were, said, they, very, into  
**Decodable Words:** camping, walking, fishing, sleeping, camped, fished, bigger, biggest, smaller, smallest  
**Comprehension Skills:** Sequence, comparing, description

---

## The Story

### Page 1
One sunny day, Papa Bear said, "Let's go **camping**!"

"Yes!" said Baby Bear. "I want to go **fishing**!"

Mama Bear packed food. Papa Bear packed the tent. Baby Bear packed his **smallest** toy.

Off they went, **walking** into the woods.

---

### Page 2
They found the perfect spot by a lake. The trees were tall. The lake was calm.

Papa Bear set up the **biggest** tent. Mama Bear set up a **smaller** one for Baby Bear.

"Mine is **smaller**, but I like it!" said Baby Bear happily.

---

### Page 3
They went **fishing**! Papa Bear caught the **biggest** fish. Mama Bear caught a medium fish. Baby Bear caught the **smallest** fish.

"Mine may be **smaller**, but it's perfect for me!" said Baby Bear.

They **cooked** their fish over the fire. It tasted delicious!

---

### Page 4
That night, they sat by the fire. They sang songs. They looked at the stars.

"This is the **best** day ever!" said Baby Bear.

Then they went to bed in their tents. They were tired from all the **walking** and **fishing**.

**THE END**

The three bears had a wonderful **camping** trip!

---

## Back Matter

### Blending Practice
**-ING Verbs:** camping, walking, fishing, cooking, sleeping, singing, looking

**-ED Verbs:** camped, packed, fished, cooked, looked

**Comparatives:** bigger, biggest, smaller, smallest, best

**Talk About the Story:**
1. Where did the bears go? (Camping by a lake!)
2. What did they do? (Fishing, cooking, singing, looking at stars)
3. Who caught the biggest fish? (Papa Bear!)
4. How did Baby Bear feel about having smaller things? (He was happy!)
