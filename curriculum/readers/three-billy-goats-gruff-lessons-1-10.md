# The Three Billy Goats Gruff!
## A Beginning Reader for Lessons 1-10
### A Public Domain Retelling For Beginning Readers

---

## For the Teacher or Parent

**Teacher/Parent Note:**  
This retelling of The Three Billy Goats Gruff uses simple CVC and sight words that reinforce the letter sounds taught in Lessons 1-10 (m, s, p, a, n, t, b, j, g, d, c, y, h, k, r, i, v, f, z, o, l, w). Repetitive phrases encourage blending practice and predictive reading. **Bold words** are key blending words. *Underlined words* are sight words to practice. Have the child "read" the repeated phrases with you!

**Target Lessons:** 1-10  
**Phonics Focus:** CVC words, basic letter sounds (m,s,p,a,n,t,b,j,g,d,c,y,h,k,r,i,v,f,z,o,l,w), blending practice  
**Key Sight Words:** the, is, to, I, he, my, no, so, do, go, will, was, all, said, come, there, were, they, who, you, then, three  
**Decodable CVC Words:** tap, tip, big, bad, mid, wee, bit, sat, ran, mad, sad, fat, got, hit, him, not, nab, did, jot, yum, nom, pal, let  
**Comprehension Skills:** Listening comprehension, sequence, character identification, predictions

---

## How to Use This Book:

1. **First Read:** Read the story aloud to the child. Let them enjoy it!
2. **Second Read:** Read together. Pause at repeated phrases and let the child fill them in.
3. **Third Read:** Let the child try to read with your help. Point to each word.
4. **Practice:** Use the blending words page at the back to practice decoding.

## The Story

### Page 1
There were **three** billy goats.

A **big** goat.  
A **mid** goat.  
A **wee bit** of a goat.

They all wanted to go up the hill.  
Up, up, up to the top!

The grass was so good up on top.

[Point to each goat as you read — big, mid, wee.]

---

### Page 2
But a **big**, **bad** troll **sat** by the path.

He was **mad**.  
He was **sad**.  
He was a very **bad** troll.

"No one can go past me!" said the troll.  
"Not a **bit**! Not a **jot**! Not at all!"

[Use a grumpy voice for the troll!]

---

### Page 3
The **wee** goat **ran** to the path.

**Tap, tap, tap.**  
**Tap, tap, tap.**

"Who is that?" said the troll.

"It is I! The **wee** billy goat!"

[Tap the table — tap, tap, tap! Have the child tap along.]

---

### Page 4
"I am **big** and **bad**!" said the troll.  
"I will not **let** you pass!"

"Do not **nab** me!" said the **wee** goat.  
"I am too **wee**. I am too slim."

"My **big** **pal** will come. He is so **fat**!"

"Hm," said the troll. "Go on, then."

[Use a tiny voice for the wee goat.]

---

### Page 5
The **wee** goat **ran** and **ran**.  
Up, up, up to the top!

And the **wee** goat began to munch.  
**Nom, nom, nom.**  
**Yum, yum, yum!**

[Have the child repeat: Up, up, up to the top! Nom, nom, nom!]

---

### Page 6
Then the **mid** goat **ran** to the path.

**Tip, tap, tip, tap.**  
**Tip, tap, tip, tap.**

"Who is that?" said the troll.

"It is I! The **mid** billy goat!"

[Tap louder now — tip, tap, tip, tap!]

---

### Page 7
"I am **big** and **bad**!" said the troll.  
"I will not **let** you pass!"

"Do not **nab** me!" said the **mid** goat.  
"I am not that **big**. I am not that **fat**."

"My **big** **pal** will come. He is SO **big**!"

"Hm," said the troll. "Go on, then."

[Use a medium voice for the mid goat.]

---

### Page 8
The **mid** goat **ran** and **ran**.  
Up, up, up to the top!

And the **mid** goat began to munch.  
**Nom, nom, nom.**  
**Yum, yum, yum!**

[Have the child repeat: Up, up, up to the top! Nom, nom, nom!]

---

### Page 9
Then the **BIG** goat **ran** to the path.

**STOMP, STOMP, STOMP!**  
**STOMP, STOMP, STOMP!**

"WHO IS THAT?" said the troll.

"It is I! The **BIG** billy goat!"

[Stomp your feet! STOMP, STOMP, STOMP! Go loud!]

---

### Page 10
"I am **big** and **bad**!" said the troll.  
"I will not **let** you pass!"

"Oh yes, you WILL," said the **big** goat.

The **big** goat **ran** at the troll.  
He **hit** him with a BONK!  
He **got** him with a BASH!  
He **did** a **big**, **big** KICK!

[Use your biggest, bravest voice!]

---

### Page 11
The troll **ran** off.  
He **ran** and **ran** and **ran**.  
He **did** not stop!

"Do not come back!" said the **big** goat.

And the troll **did** not.  
Not a **bit**.  
Not a **jot**.  
Not at all.

[Wave goodbye to the troll!]

---

### Page 12
The **big** goat **ran** up the hill.  
Up, up, up to the top!

And all **three** goats **sat** in the grass.

**Nom, nom, nom.**  
**Yum, yum, yum!**

**The end!**

[Have the child say it all together: Nom, nom, nom. Yum, yum, yum! The end!]

## Back Matter

### Blending Practice
**CVC Words from the Story:**  
tap, tip, big, bad, mid, wee, bit, sat, ran, mad, sad, fat, got, hit, him, not, nab, did, jot, yum, nom, pal, let, hm

### Repeated Phrases to Practice
"**Tap, tap, tap.**"  
"Up, up, up to the top!"  
"**Nom, nom, nom.**"  
"**Yum, yum, yum!**"  
"I am **big** and **bad**!"  
"Not a **bit**. Not a **jot**. Not at all."

### Talk About the Story
1. How many goats were there?
2. Who sat by the path?
3. Which goat went first? Which went last?
4. What did the troll say?
5. What happened to the troll at the end?
6. Can you retell the story in your own words?

### Extension Activities
- **Letter Sound Hunt:** Find words that start with: g (goat, got), b (big, bad), m (mid, mad), t (troll, tap)
- **Act It Out:** Be the goats! Tap across the "bridge" (a pillow or line on the floor). Take turns being the troll!
- **Size Sorting:** Find three toys or objects — big, medium (mid), and small (wee). Put them in order!
- **Billy's Connection:** Billy is a goat just like the Three Billy Goats! He helps kids climb the reading mountain, just like the goats climbed to the top of the hill.
