# The Tortoise and the Hare (Full Version)
## A Beginning Reader for Lessons 56-58
### An Aesop's Fable Retelling For Beginning Readers

---

## For the Teacher or Parent

**Teacher/Parent Note:**  
This FULL version of a Unit 1 favorite focuses on **OU/OW diphthongs**. We've seen this story before‚Äînow with more detail and dialogue! **Bold words** feature OU/OW patterns. *Underlined words* are sight words. The classic moral: Slow and steady wins the race!

**Target Lessons:** 56-58  
**Phonics Focus:** OU/OW diphthongs (out, about, how, now, down)  
**Key Sight Words:** the, was, a, said, you, are, very, they  
**Decodable Words:** down, how, now, proud, shout, around, found, out, about, sound, ground, slow, boast, coast  
**Comprehension Skills:** Character traits, moral lesson, cause and effect

## How to Use This Book:

1. **First Read:** Use a boastful voice for Hare and a calm voice for Tortoise!
2. **Second Read:** Predict what will happen. Will Hare win?
3. **Third Read:** Find all the OU and OW words!
4. **Practice:** OU and OW can both say /ow/ (like in "cow")!

## The Story

### Page 1
One day, Hare **was** hopping **around** the forest.

He **was** **very** fast. And he **was** **very** **proud** of it.

"Look **how** fast I am!" Hare would **shout**. "No one can beat me!"

Tortoise walked by, **slow** and steady.

Hare laughed. "**How** can you move so slowly? You are like a snail!"

[Hare is VERY proud! He brags a lot!]

---

### Page 2
Tortoise did not get upset. He said calmly, "I may be **slow**, but I always get where I am going."

"Ha!" laughed Hare. "You? Get anywhere? That takes you all day!"

"Let's have a race," said Tortoise. "From here to the big oak tree **down** by the stream."

Hare's eyes grew wide. Then he **burst** **out** laughing!

"You? Race me? This will be easy!" Hare said **proudly**.

[Tortoise challenged Hare! Hare thinks it's funny!]

---

### Page 3
All the forest animals came **out** to watch.

Fox stood at the starting line. "On your mark... get set... GO!"

Hare shot **down** the path like lightning! **Zoom!**

Tortoise began to walk, **slow** and steady. One step at a time.

"**How** is this even a race?" laughed Hare as he looked back. Tortoise **was** just a tiny dot far behind.

[Hare is SO fast! Tortoise is SO slow!]

---

### Page 4
Hare ran and ran. Soon, he **was** halfway to the oak tree.

He looked **around**. Tortoise **was** nowhere in sight!

"I am so far ahead!" Hare said **proudly**. "Tortoise is probably still at the start! I have plenty of time."

Hare sat **down** under a shady tree. "I will rest here for a bit. When Tortoise gets close, I will run and win!"

[Hare is resting! He thinks he has lots of time!]

---

### Page 5
Hare lay **down** on the soft grass. The sun **was** warm. The breeze **was** cool.

"Just a short nap," Hare said. "Then I will **race** to the finish line."

Hare closed his eyes. Soon, he **was** fast asleep. He began to snore.

**"Zzzzzz... zzzzz..."**

[Uh oh! Hare fell asleep!]

---

### Page 6
Meanwhile, Tortoise kept walking. **Slow** and steady. One step. Then another. Then another.

Step... step... step...

He did not stop. He did not rest. He just kept going.

After a while, Tortoise walked past the shady tree. He saw Hare sleeping on the **ground**!

Tortoise smiled. But he did not **shout**. He did not **wake** Hare. He just kept walking.

[Tortoise passes Hare! Hare is still sleeping!]

---

### Page 7
Tortoise walked and walked. **Slow** and steady.

Up the hill. **Down** the hill. **Around** the bend.

**Now** the big oak tree **was** in sight!

Tortoise kept going. Step... step... step...

He **was** getting close! Very close!

[Tortoise is almost there!]

---

### Page 8
Back at the shady tree, Hare woke up!

He stretched and yawned. "**How** long **was** I asleep?" he wondered.

He looked **around**. Still no sign of Tortoise!

"Good!" said Hare. "I still have time!"

Hare hopped **down** the path. He **was** not worried. "I will win this race easily!" he said **proudly**.

[Hare thinks he's still ahead!]

---

### Page 9
But as Hare came **around** the bend, he **stopped**.

What did he see?

Tortoise **was** at the oak tree! The finish line!

All the animals were cheering! "**Tortoise won! Tortoise won!**"

Hare's m**outh** fell open. "**How** did this happen?" he cried.

[TORTOISE WON! Hare is shocked!]

---

### Page 10
Hare ran as fast as he could! He zoomed **down** the path!

But it **was** too late. Tortoise had already crossed the finish line!

Hare stopped, panting. "I... I lost?" he said. "But I am so fast! **How** did you beat me?"

Tortoise smiled. "You were fast, Hare. But you **stopped**. I kept going. **Slow** and steady wins the race."

[Tortoise teaches Hare a lesson!]

---

### Page 11
Hare hung his head. He **was** not **proud** anymore. He **was** embarrassed.

"You are right," Hare said. "I **was** too **proud**. I thought I could not lose. But I **did**."

"It is good to be fast," said Tortoise kindly. "But it is also good to keep trying and not give up. Speed is not everything."

Hare nodded. "You have taught me a lesson, Tortoise. Thank you."

[Hare learned his lesson!]

---

### Page 12
From that day on, Hare **was** still fast. But he **was** not so **proud**.

And he never **boasted** again.

Tortoise kept walking, **slow** and steady, just as always.

**THE END**

**Slow and steady wins the race!**

[The classic lesson: Don't give up! Keep going!]

## Back Matter

### Blending Practice
**OU Diphthong Words:**  
out, about, shout, proud, found, sound, ground, around, sprout, cloud, loud, mouse, house, mouth

**OW Diphthong Words:**  
how, now, down, brown, cow, town, crown, frown, owl, growl, vowel

**Other Decodable Words:**  
hare, race, fast, slow, tree, rest, nap, win, beat, kept, step, stop

### Repeated Phrases to Practice
**"Slow and steady!"** | **"How can you?"** | **"Down the path!"** | **"Around the bend!"**

### Talk About the Story
1. Why was Hare so proud? (He was very fast!)
2. What did Tortoise challenge Hare to? (A race!)
3. What mistake did Hare make? (He stopped to rest and fell asleep!)
4. What did Tortoise do while Hare slept? (He kept walking, slow and steady!)
5. Who won the race? (Tortoise!)
6. What is the lesson? (Slow and steady wins the race! Don't give up!)

### Extension Activities
- **OU/OW Hunt:** Find all the OU and OW words. Do they all say /ow/?
- **Act It Out:** Act out the race! One person be fast Hare, one be slow Tortoise!
- **Draw & Write:** Draw Hare sleeping and Tortoise passing by!
- **Billy's Connection:** Billy the Goat climbs mountains slowly and carefully. Rushing can be dangerous! Slow and steady gets you to the top safely!

### Character Education
**Theme:** Persistence beats talent when talent doesn't try. Hare was faster, but Tortoise never gave up. Hard work and determination are just as important as natural ability!

**Discussion:** Have you ever worked hard at something even when it was difficult? Did you give up or keep going?
