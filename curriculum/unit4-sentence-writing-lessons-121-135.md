## Lesson 121: What is a Sentence?

### Concept:
A sentence is a group of words that tells a complete thought. It has a beginning, a middle, and an end.

### Billy Says:
"A sentence is like a trail! It starts somewhere, goes somewhere, and ends somewhere. You can't just stop in the middle of the mountain!"

### Examples from Billy's World:
‚úÖ **Complete Sentences:**
- Billy climbs the mountain.
- The goat eats grass.
- Mountains are tall.
- I see a bird.

‚ùå **NOT Complete Sentences:**
- Billy climbs (Where? What?)
- Eats grass (Who eats grass?)
- Are tall (What is tall?)

## Lesson 123: End Mark Mountain

### Concept:
Every sentence needs an end mark: period (.) question mark (?) or exclamation point (!)

### Billy Says:
"End marks are like reaching a viewpoint‚Äîyou STOP and look! Each mark tells you how to feel!"

### The Three End Marks:

**Period (.)** = Telling/Statement
- Billy climbs the mountain.
- I see a bird.

**Question Mark (?)** = Asking
- Where is Billy?
- Can goats climb?

**Exclamation Point (!)** = Excitement/Strong Feeling
- Watch out for that rock!
- Billy reached the top!

### Practice Activity:
**Add the correct end mark:**

1. Billy is hungry___
2. Where is the trail___
3. Wow, what a view___
4. Goats have hooves___
5. Can you see the top___
6. Help, I'm slipping___

## Lesson 127: Describing Words (Adjectives)

### Concept:

### Billy Says:

## Lesson 128: Stretching Sentences

### Concept:
Add details to make sentences more interesting! Answer: Who? What? When? Where? Why? How?

### Billy Says:
"Don't just say 'Billy climbed.' Tell the whole story!"

### Example:
**Basic:** Billy climbed.

**Stretched:**
- Billy climbed quickly. (How?)
- Billy climbed the mountain. (What?)
- Billy climbed yesterday. (When?)
- Billy climbed to find food. (Why?)
- Billy climbed with his friend. (With whom?)

### Practice Activity:
**Stretch each sentence by adding details:**

1. The goat ate.
2. I walked.
3. Birds sing.
4. The sun shines.
5. We found something.

## Lesson 129: Sentence Combining

### Concept:
Join two sentences with "and" to make writing flow better!

### Billy Says:
"Two short sentences can team up with 'and' to make one strong sentence!"

### Examples:
**Before:**
- Billy climbed the mountain. Billy reached the top.

**After:**
- Billy climbed the mountain and reached the top.

**Before:**
- I see a bird. I see a rabbit.

**After:**
- I see a bird and a rabbit.

### Practice Activity:
**Combine each pair of sentences using "and":**

1. Billy eats grass. Billy drinks water.
2. The trail is long. The trail is steep.
3. I packed my backpack. I started hiking.

## Lesson 130: Statements & Questions
