## Lesson 151: Planning Your Writing

### Concept:
Good writers PLAN before they write! Planning helps organize your ideas.

### Billy Says:
"Before I climb, I plan my route! Before you write, plan your ideas!"

### Planning Tools:

**1. Brainstorm:** Write down ALL your ideas (no wrong answers!)

**2. Organize:** Put ideas in order
- What comes first?
- What comes next?
- How does it end?

**3. Planning Web:**
```
        [Detail]
           |
    [TOPIC] - [Detail]
           |
        [Detail]
```

## Lesson 153: Revising - Making It Better

### Concept:
Revising means changing your writing to make it BETTER! You add, delete, or rearrange ideas.

### Billy Says:
"Revising is like finding a better trail. You look at what you have and improve it!"

### Revising Checklist:

**ADD:**
- [ ] Can I add more details?
- [ ] Can I add describing words?
- [ ] Can I add a better ending?

**DELETE:**
- [ ] Are there words I don't need?
- [ ] Did I repeat myself?

**REARRANGE:**
- [ ] Does the order make sense?
- [ ] Should I move sentences around?

### Example:

**First Draft:**
Billy climbed. He saw stuff. It was good.

**After Revising:**
Billy climbed the steep mountain trail on Monday morning. At the top, he saw three eagles flying overhead and a beautiful sunset. Billy felt proud and happy!

### Practice Activity:
**Revise this paragraph. Add details and better words!**

The goat walked. He ate. It was fun.

## Lesson 155: Spelling Patterns Review

### Concept:
Use phonics rules you've learned to spell words correctly!

### Billy Says:
"You know SO many spelling patterns now! Use them when you write!"

### Quick Spelling Review:

**CVC Words:** cat, big, run, hop  
**Magic E:** make, time, hope, cute  
**Vowel Teams:** rain, boat, tree, moon  
**R-Controlled:** car, her, bird, turn  
**Blends:** stop, blend, train, spring  
**Digraphs:** ship, chat, think, phone

### Spelling Strategy:
1. Sound out the word
2. Think about what pattern it uses
3. Write it down
4. Check: Does it look right?

## Lesson 156: Frequently Used Words

### Concept:
Some words don't follow rules‚Äîyou just have to remember them! These are called sight words.

### Billy Says:

### Important Sight Words to Spell:

**Set 1:** the, a, an, is, are, was, were  
**Set 2:** I, me, my, you, your, we, our  
**Set 3:** said, have, do, does, what, when, where

## Lesson 157: Using Resources

### Concept:
Good writers use tools to help them! Word walls, dictionaries, and asking for help are all smart strategies!

### Billy Says:
"I use a map when I climb! You can use resources when you write!"

### Writing Resources:

**1. Word Wall**
- Lists of words organized by pattern
- Sight words displayed
- Check spelling here first!

**2. Picture Dictionary**
- Shows words with pictures
- Helps you spell and understand words

**3. Ask for Help**
- Ask a teacher, parent, or friend
- "How do you spell _____?"
- "Does this sentence make sense?"

**4. Reading**
- Read books to see how words are spelled
- Notice how authors write

### Practice Activity:

## Lesson 158: Publishing Your Work

### Concept:
Publishing means creating a final, perfect copy of your writing to share!

### Billy Says:
"Publishing is like reaching the summit‚Äîyou did it! Now show everyone!"

### Steps to Publishing:

**1. Write final copy neatly**
- Use your best handwriting OR type it
- Include all your edits

**2. Add illustrations**
- Draw pictures that match your story
- Color them nicely

**3. Add a title**
- Make it big and clear
- Decorate it if you want!

**4. Add your name**
- "By [Your Name]"
- You're the author!

**5. Share it!**
- Read it aloud to others
- Display it on the wall
- Make copies for family

### Publishing Checklist:
- [ ] Final copy is neat and readable
- [ ] All mistakes are fixed
- [ ] Title is clear
- [ ] Author name is included
- [ ] Illustrations are complete

## Lesson 159: Sharing Writing

### Concept:
Authors share their writing with others! Practice reading your work aloud!

### Billy Says:
"I love telling stories about my adventures! You should share your writing too!"

### How to Share Your Writing:

**1. Practice First**
- Read your writing to yourself
- Practice reading clearly and slowly
- Decide where to pause

**2. Read with Expression**
- Use different voices for characters
- Change your tone for exciting parts
- Pause at periods

**3. Make Eye Contact**
- Look at your audience
- Don't hide behind your paper

**4. Speak Loudly**
- Make sure everyone can hear
- Don't rush‚Äîtake your time

**5. Answer Questions**
- Listen to what people say
- Answer questions about your writing

### Practice Activity:
**Choose a piece of writing you're proud of. Practice reading it aloud 3 times. Then share it with someone!**
